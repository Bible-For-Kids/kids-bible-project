# Children's Bible Version (CBV) Translation Principles

## Overview
The Children's Bible Version (CBV) is a complete verse-by-verse translation designed specifically for children ages 5-10. This document outlines the core principles and methodologies that guide the translation process.

## Core Mission Statement

**"Every verse, every child, complete understanding."**

The CBV exists to ensure that any child, regardless of age or reading ability, can pick up a Bible and understand any verse they read, while maintaining complete fidelity to the original biblical text.

## Translation Philosophy

### 1. Verse-by-Verse Completeness
- **Complete Coverage**: Every verse from Genesis 1:1 to Revelation 22:21
- **Exact Correspondence**: Each CBV verse corresponds exactly to traditional verse numbering
- **No Omission**: No verses are skipped, simplified away, or consolidated
- **Cross-Reference Compatibility**: Children can follow along with any sermon, Bible study, or devotional

### 2. Age-Appropriate Accessibility
Each verse is translated two ways:
- **Ages 5-7**: Simple, concrete, immediate understanding
- **Ages 8-10**: Growing complexity, introduction to abstract concepts

### 3. Biblical Fidelity
- **Meaning Preservation**: Core biblical meaning is never compromised
- **Context Integrity**: Verses maintain their contextual relationships
- **Theological Accuracy**: Doctrinal truths are preserved in age-appropriate language
- **Historical Accuracy**: Historical and cultural context is maintained

### Age-Specific Translation Guidelines

#### Ages 5-7: Foundation Level
**Characteristics:**
- **Sentence Length**: 3-5 words maximum
- **Vocabulary**: 500-800 most common words
- **Concepts**: Concrete, tangible, observable
- **Focus**: Who, what, where, when (simple)
- **Tone**: Gentle, reassuring, positive
- **Depth Preservation**: Maintain biblical richness while simplifying only complex words

**Translation Strategy:**
- Keep the story-like flow and biblical richness
- Replace only truly complex words with simpler alternatives
- Break very long sentences into shorter, connected sentences
- Maintain the original sentence structure when possible
- Use familiar words that carry the same meaning
- Preserve the emotional and spiritual depth

**Example - Genesis 1:2:**
- *Original*: "Now the earth was formless and empty, darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters."
- *CBV 5-7*: "The earth was empty and didn't have a shape yet. There was complete darkness over the deep waters. And God's Spirit was hovering over the waters."

**Example - John 3:16:**
- *Original*: "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."
- *CBV 5-7*: "God loved the world so very much that He gave us his only Son Jesus. And anyone who believes in him will not die but live forever with God."

#### Ages 8-10: Growing Level
**Characteristics:**
- **Sentence Length**: 6-10 words average
- **Vocabulary**: 1,500-2,000 words including some abstract terms
- **Concepts**: Introduction to abstract thinking
- **Focus**: Adding "why" and "how" to basic understanding
- **Tone**: Educational, engaging, thought-provoking
- **Depth Preservation**: Maintain most original language with minimal simplification

**Translation Strategy:**
- Keep the original sentence structure and flow
- Replace only the most complex or archaic words
- Maintain the story-like narrative quality
- Preserve the theological depth and richness
- Use more sophisticated but still accessible vocabulary
- Keep the biblical tone and rhythm

**Example - Genesis 1:2:**
- *Original*: "Now the earth was formless and empty, darkness was over the surface of the deep, and the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters."
- *CBV 8-10*: "The earth was formless and empty, and darkness covered the surface of the deep. The Spirit of God was hovering over the waters."

**Example - John 3:16:**
- *Original*: "For God so loved the world that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."
- *CBV 8-10*: "God loved the world so much that he gave his one and only Son, that whoever believes in him shall not perish but have eternal life."

## Quality Control Standards

### Translation Accuracy Checklist
For each verse, translators must verify:
- [ ] **Original Meaning**: Core meaning is preserved
- [ ] **Context Integrity**: Verse fits within chapter and book context
- [ ] **Theological Soundness**: Doctrinal truth is maintained
- [ ] **Age Appropriateness**: Language suitable for target age group
- [ ] **Readability**: Meets reading level standards
- [ ] **Cultural Accuracy**: Historical and cultural context preserved

### Multi-Layer Review Process

#### Layer 1: Translator Review
- Initial translation following CBV principles
- Self-check against original text
- Age-appropriateness verification

#### Layer 2: Theological Review
- Biblical scholar review for accuracy
- Doctrinal integrity verification
- Cross-reference compatibility check

#### Layer 3: Child Testing
- Read to children in target age group
- Comprehension questions and feedback
- Engagement and interest assessment

#### Layer 4: Final Editorial Review
- Grammar and style consistency
- Format and structure compliance
- Final approval for publication

## Special Translation Considerations

### Difficult Concepts Strategy

#### Violence and Sensitive Topics
- **Ages 5-7**: Focus on protection and rescue, minimize graphic details
- **Ages 8-10**: Acknowledge difficult realities with emphasis on God's protection
- **Ages 11-12**: Honest treatment with theological context and hope

#### Theological Abstractions
- **Trinity**: Use "God the Father, Jesus, and the Holy Spirit" with relational language
- **Salvation**: Focus on "being saved," "forgiveness," and "life with God"
- **Sin**: Use "making wrong choices," "disobeying God," or "missing God's best"

#### Cultural and Historical Context
- **Ancient Practices**: Explain with modern equivalents
- **Geographic Locations**: Use familiar references when possible
- **Social Customs**: Explain without judgment or confusion

### Poetry and Metaphor Translation
- **Maintain Imagery**: Keep visual and sensory language
- **Explain Symbols**: Clarify symbolic meanings simply
- **Preserve Beauty**: Maintain poetic quality while ensuring clarity

### Proverbial and Wisdom Literature
- **Direct Application**: Make practical wisdom clear
- **Universal Principles**: Extract timeless truths
- **Actionable Guidance**: Provide clear behavioral direction
