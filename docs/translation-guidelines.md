# Children's Bible Version (CBV) Translation Guidelines

## Overview
This document provides comprehensive guidelines for translating the complete Bible (verse-by-verse) into age-appropriate language for children ages 5-10, creating the Children's Bible Version (CBV).

## CBV Core Mission
**Every verse, every child, complete understanding.**

The CBV ensures that any child can read any verse in the Bible and understand it immediately, while maintaining complete fidelity to the original biblical text and verse structure.

## Verse-by-Verse Translation Requirements

### Complete Coverage Mandate
- **Every Verse**: All 31,102 verses from Genesis 1:1 to Revelation 22:21
- **Exact Correspondence**: Each CBV verse matches traditional verse numbering exactly
- **No Omissions**: No verses are skipped, simplified away, or consolidated
- **Cross-Reference Compatible**: Children can follow along with any sermon or Bible study

### Two-Level Translation Structure
Each verse must have two distinct translations:

#### Level 1: Ages 5-7 (Foundation)
- **Sentence Length**: Short, clear sentences. Do not force a 3-5 word cap when a few more simple words help a child picture the verse faithfully.
- **Vocabulary**: 500-800 most common words
- **Concepts**: Concrete, tangible, observable
- **Focus**: Immediate understanding with vivid Bible-shaped storytelling
- **Tone**: Gentle, visual, reverent, and engaging

#### Level 2: Ages 8-10 (Growing)  
- **Sentence Length**: 6-10 words average
- **Vocabulary**: 1,500-2,000 words including some abstract terms
- **Concepts**: Introduction to abstract thinking
- **Focus**: Adding "why" and "how" to basic understanding
- **Tone**: Educational, engaging, thought-provoking

## Age Group Targeting

### Ages 5-7 (Early Childhood)
**Characteristics:**
- Short attention spans (3-5 minutes)
- Concrete thinking
- Limited vocabulary
- Learning through repetition
- Literal interpretation

**Language Guidelines:**
- Prefer short sentences, but allow richer simple phrasing when it paints the scene without changing the meaning
- Simple, common words only
- Present tense preferred
- Active voice only
- One concept per sentence
- Use familiar objects and situations
- Keep the visual story tradition: darkness covering everything, water gathered, light shining, people moving through real places

**Example:**
- *Original*: "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."
- *Kid Version*: "God made the sky and the ground."

### Ages 8-10 (Middle Childhood)
**Characteristics:**
- Longer attention spans (10-15 minutes)
- Beginning abstract thinking
- Expanding vocabulary
- Can follow simple sequences
- Understanding cause and effect

**Language Guidelines:**
- 6-10 words per sentence
- Introduction to some abstract concepts
- Simple compound sentences
- Past tense acceptable
- Basic storytelling elements

**Example:**
- *Original*: "In the beginning God created the heavens and the earth."
- *Kid Version*: "Long ago, God created everything in the sky and on the earth."

## Translation Principles

### 1. Biblical Accuracy
- Maintain the core meaning and truth of Scripture
- Do not add or remove essential details
- Preserve the historical and cultural context
- Keep the moral and spiritual lessons intact
- Keep chapter summaries and key lessons theologically precise, not just memorable. For example, prefer "God speaks, and his words come to pass" over "God speaks, and creation obeys" unless the text itself presents personal obedience. Prefer text-shaped wording such as "the great lights of the sky" over flatter wording like "lights in the sky" when the biblical wording carries meaning.

### 2. Age Appropriateness
- Consider emotional maturity
- Avoid frightening or overly complex concepts
- Use examples children can relate to
- Respect developmental stages

### 3. Cultural Sensitivity
- Use universal examples when possible
- Avoid culturally specific references
- Consider global audience
- Respect different family structures

### 4. Educational Value
- Introduce new vocabulary gradually
- Teach moral lessons clearly
- Provide context for understanding
- Encourage questions and discussion

## Content Structure Guidelines

### Story Format
Each Bible story should follow this structure:

```markdown
# [Story Title]

## Bible Reference
[Book Chapter:Verses]

## Age Group
[Target age range]

## Story Content
[The simplified story text]

## Key Lesson
[Main moral or spiritual lesson]

## Discussion Questions
1. [Question 1]
2. [Question 2]
3. [Question 3]

## Memory Verse
[Simplified memory verse]

## Prayer
[Simple prayer related to the story]
```

### Word Choice Guidelines

#### Use These Words:
- Simple, common words (happy, sad, good, bad)
- Action words (run, jump, help, love)
- Concrete objects (tree, house, water, food)
- Family terms (mom, dad, brother, sister)
- Nature words (sun, moon, stars, animals)

#### Avoid These Words:
- Abstract theological terms (redemption, salvation, justification)
- Complex vocabulary (behold, thou, thy, henceforth)
- Archaic language (verily, whence, thence)
- Violent or scary imagery (unless contextually necessary and handled carefully)
- Culturally specific idioms or expressions

## Specific Translation Rules

### Names and Places
- Keep original biblical names (Noah, Abraham, Jerusalem)
- Provide simple pronunciation guides if needed
- Explain unfamiliar places briefly
- Use consistent name spellings

### Numbers and Measurements
- Use simple numbers (one, two, ten)
- Convert complex measurements to relatable terms
- Example: "40 days" becomes "almost 6 weeks"
- Example: "cubit" becomes "about as long as your arm"

### Time References
- Use simple time concepts (morning, night, today, tomorrow)
- Explain historical time periods simply
- Use "long ago" instead of specific dates
- Relate time to children's experiences

### Dialogue
- Keep conversations simple and direct
- Use natural speech patterns
- Explain who is speaking clearly
- Break long speeches into shorter exchanges

## Quality Control Process

### Self-Review Checklist
- [ ] Is the language appropriate for the target age group?
- [ ] Does it maintain biblical accuracy?
- [ ] Is it engaging and interesting?
- [ ] Are sentences the right length?
- [ ] Is vocabulary appropriate?
- [ ] Are difficult concepts explained simply?

### Peer Review Process
1. **Content Review**: Check biblical accuracy
2. **Age Review**: Verify age appropriateness
3. **Language Review**: Check grammar and clarity
4. **Final Review**: Overall quality assessment

### Testing with Children
- Read stories to children in target age groups
- Ask comprehension questions
- Note any confusing parts
- Get feedback on engagement level

## Common Translation Challenges

### Violence in Scripture
- Acknowledge difficult events without graphic details
- Focus on the bigger picture and God's protection
- Use gentle language for sensitive topics
- Provide context and reassurance

### Complex Theological Concepts
- Break down into simple, relatable ideas
- Use analogies children can understand
- Focus on the practical application
- Avoid overly abstract explanations

### Cultural Differences
- Explain cultural context simply
- Use universal human experiences
- Avoid modern interpretations that change meaning
- Respect the original historical context
