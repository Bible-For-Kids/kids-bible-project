# Judges 17

## Book
Judges

## Chapter
17

## Verses

### Judges 17:1
There was a man of the hill country of Ephraim, whose name was Micah.

### Judges 17:2
Micah said to his mother, "The eleven hundred pieces of silver that were taken from you, the silver you spoke a curse about in my hearing-look, I have it. I took it." His mother said, "May the Lord bless my son!"

### Judges 17:3
He gave the eleven hundred pieces of silver back to his mother. Then his mother said, "I dedicate this silver to the Lord for my son, to make a carved idol and a metal idol. Now I give it back to you."

### Judges 17:4
When Micah gave the money back to his mother, she took two hundred pieces of silver and gave them to a silversmith. He made a carved idol and a metal idol from it, and they were put in Micah's house.

### Judges 17:5
Micah had a house full of false gods. He made a special priestly vest and household idols. Then he set apart one of his sons to become his priest.

### Judges 17:6
In those days there was no king in Israel. Everyone did what was right in his own eyes.

### Judges 17:7
There was a young Levite from Bethlehem in Judah, from the family of Judah, and he lived there.

### Judges 17:8
The young man left Bethlehem in Judah to find another place to live. As he traveled, he came to Micah's house in the hill country of Ephraim.

### Judges 17:9
Micah said to him, "Where did you come from?" He said to him, "I am a Levite of Bethlehem Judah, and I am looking for a place to live."

### Judges 17:10
Micah said to him, "Stay with me and be like a father and priest to me. I will give you ten silver pieces a year, clothes, and food." So the Levite went in.

### Judges 17:11
The Levite agreed to live with Micah, and the young man became like one of his sons.

### Judges 17:12
Micah set apart the Levite, and the young man became his priest, and was in the house of Micah.

### Judges 17:13
Then Micah said, "Now I know that the Lord will do good to me, since I have a Levite as my priest."
