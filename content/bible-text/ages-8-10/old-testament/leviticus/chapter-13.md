# Leviticus 13

## Book
Leviticus

## Chapter
13

## Verses

### Leviticus 13:1
The Lord spoke to Moses and to Aaron, saying,

### Leviticus 13:2
"When a man has a swelling in his body's skin, or a scab, or a bright spot, and it becomes in the skin of his body the serious mark of disease, then he must be brought to Aaron the priest or to one of his sons, the priests.

### Leviticus 13:3
The priest must examine the mark of disease in the skin of the body. If the hair in the mark of disease has turned white, and the appearance of the mark of disease is deeper than the body's skin, it is the serious mark of disease; so the priest must examine him and pronounce him unclean.

### Leviticus 13:4
If the bright spot is white in the skin of his body, and its appearance isn't deeper than the skin, and its hair hasn't turned white, then the priest must isolate the infected person for seven days.

### Leviticus 13:5
The priest must examine him on the seventh day. If the priest sees that the mark of disease has stopped spreading and the mark of disease hasn't spread in the skin, then the priest must isolate him for seven more days.

### Leviticus 13:6
The priest must examine him again on the seventh day. If the mark of disease has faded and the mark of disease hasn't spread in the skin, then the priest must pronounce him clean. It is a scab. He must wash his clothes, and be clean.

### Leviticus 13:7
But if the scab spreads on the skin after he has shown himself to the priest for his cleansing, he must show himself to the priest again.

### Leviticus 13:8
The priest must examine him; and if the scab has spread on the skin, then the priest must pronounce him unclean. It is serious disease.

### Leviticus 13:9
"When the serious mark of disease is in a man, then he must be brought to the priest;

### Leviticus 13:10
and the priest must examine him. If there is a white swelling in the skin, and it has turned the hair white, and there is raw flesh in the swelling,

### Leviticus 13:11
it is a chronic serious disease in the skin of his body, and the priest must pronounce him unclean. He must not isolate him, for he is already unclean.

### Leviticus 13:12
"If the serious disease breaks out all over the skin, and the serious disease covers all the skin of the infected person from his head even to his feet, as far as it appears to the priest,

### Leviticus 13:13
then the priest must examine him. If the serious disease has covered all his flesh, he must pronounce him clean of the mark of disease. It has all turned white: he is clean.

### Leviticus 13:14
But whenever raw flesh appears in him, he will be unclean.

### Leviticus 13:15
The priest must examine the raw flesh, and pronounce him unclean: the raw flesh is unclean. It is serious disease.

### Leviticus 13:16
Or if the raw flesh turns again, and is changed to white, then he must come to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:17
The priest must examine him. If the mark of disease has turned white, then the priest must pronounce him clean of the mark of disease. He is clean.

### Leviticus 13:18
"When the body has a boil on its skin, and it has healed,

### Leviticus 13:19
and in the place of the boil there is a white swelling, or a bright spot, reddish-white, then it must be shown to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:20
The priest must examine it. If its appearance is deeper than the skin, and its hair has turned white, then the priest must pronounce him unclean. It is the serious mark of disease. It has broken out in the boil.

### Leviticus 13:21
But if the priest examines it, and there are no white hairs in it, and it isn't deeper than the skin, but is dim, then the priest must isolate him seven days.

### Leviticus 13:22
If it spreads in the skin, then the priest must pronounce him unclean. It is a mark of disease.

### Leviticus 13:23
But if the bright spot stays in its place, and hasn't spread, it is the scar from the boil; and the priest must pronounce him clean.

### Leviticus 13:24
"Or when the body has a burn from fire on its skin, and the raw flesh of the burn becomes a bright spot, reddish-white, or white,

### Leviticus 13:25
then the priest must examine it; and if the hair in the bright spot has turned white, and its appearance is deeper than the skin, it is serious disease. It has broken out in the burning, and the priest must pronounce him unclean. It is the serious mark of disease.

### Leviticus 13:26
But if the priest examines it, and there is no white hair in the bright spot, and it isn't deeper than the skin, but has faded, then the priest must isolate him seven days.

### Leviticus 13:27
The priest must examine him on the seventh day. If it has spread in the skin, then the priest must pronounce him unclean. It is the serious mark of disease.

### Leviticus 13:28
If the bright spot stays in its place, and hasn't spread in the skin, but is faded, it is the swelling from the burn, and the priest must pronounce him clean, for it is the scar from the burn.

### Leviticus 13:29
"When a man or woman has a mark of disease on the head or on the beard,

### Leviticus 13:30
then the priest must examine the mark of disease; and if its appearance is deeper than the skin, and the hair in it is yellow and thin, then the priest must pronounce him unclean. It is an itch. It is serious disease of the head or of the beard.

### Leviticus 13:31
If the priest examines the mark of disease of itching, and its appearance isn't deeper than the skin, and there is no black hair in it, then the priest must isolate the person infected with itching seven days.

### Leviticus 13:32
On the seventh day the priest must examine the mark of disease; and if the itch hasn't spread, and there is no yellow hair in it, and the appearance of the itch isn't deeper than the skin,

### Leviticus 13:33
then he must be shaved, but he must not shave the itch. Then the priest must isolate the one who has the itch seven more days.

### Leviticus 13:34
On the seventh day, the priest must examine the itch; and if the itch hasn't spread in the skin, and its appearance isn't deeper than the skin, then the priest must pronounce him clean. He must wash his clothes and be clean.

### Leviticus 13:35
But if the itch spreads in the skin after his cleansing,

### Leviticus 13:36
then the priest must examine him; and if the itch has spread in the skin, the priest must not look for the yellow hair; he is unclean.

### Leviticus 13:37
But if the priest sees that the itch has stopped spreading and black hair has grown in it, then the itch is healed. He is clean. The priest must pronounce him clean.

### Leviticus 13:38
"When a man or a woman has bright spots in the skin of the body, even white bright spots,

### Leviticus 13:39
then the priest must examine them. If the bright spots on the skin of their body are a dull white, it is a harmless rash. It has broken out in the skin. He is clean.

### Leviticus 13:40
"If a man's hair has fallen from his head, he is bald. He is clean.

### Leviticus 13:41
If his hair has fallen off from the front part of his head, he is bald on the forehead. He is clean.

### Leviticus 13:42
But if a reddish-white mark of disease is in the bald head or the bald forehead, it is serious disease breaking out in his bald head or his bald forehead.

### Leviticus 13:43
Then the priest must examine him. If the swelling of the mark of disease is reddish-white in his bald head, or in his bald forehead, like the appearance of serious disease in the skin of the body,

### Leviticus 13:44
he has a serious disease. He is unclean. The priest must surely pronounce him unclean. His mark of disease is on his head.

### Leviticus 13:45
"The person with the serious disease must wear torn clothes, and the hair of his head must hang loose. He must cover his upper lip, and must cry, 'Unclean! Unclean!

### Leviticus 13:46
All the days in which the mark of disease is in him he will be unclean. He is unclean. He must dwell alone. His dwelling must be outside of the camp.

### Leviticus 13:47
"The garment also that the serious mark of disease is in, whether it is a woolen garment, or a linen garment;

### Leviticus 13:48
whether it is in warp or woof; of linen or of wool; whether in a leather, or in anything made of leather;

### Leviticus 13:49
if the mark of disease is greenish or reddish in the garment, or in the leather, or in the warp, or in the woof, or in anything made of leather; it is the serious mark of disease, and must be shown to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:50
The priest must examine the mark of disease, and isolate the mark of disease seven days.

### Leviticus 13:51
He must examine the mark of disease on the seventh day. If the mark of disease has spread in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in the skin, whatever use the skin is used for, the mark of disease is a destructive mildew. It is unclean.

### Leviticus 13:52
He must burn the garment, whether the warp or the woof, in wool or in linen, or anything of leather, in which the mark of disease is, for it is a destructive mildew. It must be burned in the fire.

### Leviticus 13:53
"If the priest examines it, and the mark of disease hasn't spread in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in anything of skin;

### Leviticus 13:54
then the priest must command that they wash the thing that the mark of disease is in, and he must isolate it seven more days.

### Leviticus 13:55
Then the priest must examine it, after the mark of disease is washed; and if the mark of disease hasn't changed its color, and the mark of disease hasn't spread, it is unclean; you must burn it in the fire. It is a mildewed spot, whether the bareness is inside or outside.

### Leviticus 13:56
If the priest looks, and the mark of disease has faded after it is washed, then he must tear it out of the garment, or out of the skin, or out of the warp, or out of the woof;

### Leviticus 13:57
and if it appears again in the garment, either in the warp, or in the woof, or in anything of skin, it is spreading. You must burn with fire whatever has the mark of disease.

### Leviticus 13:58
The garment, either the warp, or the woof, or whatever thing of skin it is, which you must wash, if the mark of disease has departed from them, then it must be washed the second time, and it will be clean."

### Leviticus 13:59
This is the law of the mark of disease of mildew in a garment of wool or linen, either in the warp, or the woof, or in anything of skin, to pronounce it clean, or to pronounce it unclean.
