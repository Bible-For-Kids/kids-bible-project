# Exodus 2

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
2

## Verses

### Exodus 2:1
A man from the family of Levi went and married a daughter of Levi.

### Exodus 2:2
The woman became pregnant and gave birth to a son. When she saw that he was a beautiful child, she hid him for three months.

### Exodus 2:3
When she could hide him no longer, she took a basket made of reeds and covered it with tar and pitch. She placed the child in it and set it among the reeds by the riverbank.

### Exodus 2:4
His sister stood far away to see what would happen to him.

### Exodus 2:5
Pharaoh's daughter came down to wash herself at the river, and her attendants walked along the riverbank. She saw the basket among the reeds and sent her servant to get it.

### Exodus 2:6
When she opened it, she saw the child. The baby was crying, and she felt compassion for him. She said, "This is one of the Hebrews' children."

### Exodus 2:7
Then his sister said to Pharaoh's daughter, "Should I go and call a Hebrew woman to nurse the child for you?"

### Exodus 2:8
Pharaoh's daughter said to her, "Go." So the girl went and called the child's mother.

### Exodus 2:9
Pharaoh's daughter said to her, "Take this child and nurse him for me, and I will pay you." So the woman took the child and nursed him.

### Exodus 2:10
When the child grew older, she brought him to Pharaoh's daughter, and he became her son. She named him Moses, saying, "I drew him out of the water."

### Exodus 2:11
When Moses was grown, he went out to his own people and saw their burdens. He saw an Egyptian striking a Hebrew, one of his own people.

### Exodus 2:12
Moses looked this way and that way. When he saw no one, he killed the Egyptian and hid him in the sand.

### Exodus 2:13
The next day Moses went out again. He saw two Hebrew men fighting and said to the one who was wrong, "Why are you hitting your neighbor?"

### Exodus 2:14
The man said, "Who made you a ruler and judge over us? Are you planning to kill me as you killed the Egyptian?" Moses was afraid and thought, "Surely this thing is known."

### Exodus 2:15
When Pharaoh heard about this, he tried to kill Moses. But Moses fled from Pharaoh and lived in the land of Midian. He sat down by a well.

### Exodus 2:16
Now the priest of Midian had seven daughters. They came to draw water and fill the troughs to water their father's flock.

### Exodus 2:17
Some shepherds came and drove them away, but Moses stood up and helped them. Then he watered their flock.

### Exodus 2:18
When they came to their father Reuel, he said, "How did you come home so quickly today?"

### Exodus 2:19
They said, "An Egyptian rescued us from the shepherds. He also drew water for us and watered the flock."

### Exodus 2:20
He said to his daughters, "Where is he? Why did you leave the man? Call him, so he may eat with us."

### Exodus 2:21
Moses was willing to live with the man, and he gave Moses his daughter Zipporah as his wife.

### Exodus 2:22
She gave birth to a son, and Moses named him Gershom, saying, "I have been a stranger in a foreign land."

### Exodus 2:23
After many days, the king of Egypt died. The children of Israel groaned because of their slavery, and they cried out. Their cry for help rose up to God.

### Exodus 2:24
God heard their groaning, and God remembered his covenant, the serious promise relationship he made with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

### Exodus 2:25
God looked upon the children of Israel, and God knew their suffering.
