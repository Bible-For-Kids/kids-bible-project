# 2 Kings 7

## Book
2 Kings

## Chapter
7

## Verses

### 2 Kings 7:1
Elisha said, "Hear the Lord's word. The Lord says, 'Tomorrow about this time, fine flour and barley will be sold cheaply at the gate of Samaria.'"

### 2 Kings 7:2
The commander whose hand the king leaned on answered the man of God, "Look, even if the Lord made windows in heaven, could this happen?" Elisha said, "You will see it with your eyes, but you will not eat any of it."

### 2 Kings 7:3
Four men with serious skin disease were at the entrance of the city gate. They said to one another, "Why should we sit here until we die?

### 2 Kings 7:4
If we go into the city, the famine is there, and we will die. If we sit here, we will die too. So come, let us surrender to the Syrian army. If they spare us, we will live. If they kill us, we will only die."

### 2 Kings 7:5
At twilight they got up to go to the Syrian camp. When they reached the edge of the camp, look, no one was there.

### 2 Kings 7:6
The Lord had made the Syrian army hear the sound of chariots, horses, and a great army. They said to one another, "The king of Israel has hired the kings of the Hittites and Egyptians to attack us!"

### 2 Kings 7:7
Therefore they got up and ran away in the twilight, and left their tents, and their horses, and their donkeys, even the camp as it was, and ran away for their life.

### 2 Kings 7:8
When these men with serious skin disease came to the outermost part of the camp, they went into one tent, and ate and drank, and carried away silver, gold, and clothing, and went and hid it. Then they came back, and entered into another tent, and carried things from there also, and went and hid them.

### 2 Kings 7:9
Then the men said to one another, "We are not doing right. Today is a day of good news, and we are keeping silent. If we wait until morning, punishment may come on us. Come, let us go tell the king's household."

### 2 Kings 7:10
So they came and called to the city gatekeepers; and they told them, "We came to the camp of the Syrians, and, look, there was no man there, not even a man's voice, but the horses tied, and the donkeys tied, and the tents as they were."

### 2 Kings 7:11
He called the gatekeepers; and they told it to the king's household within.

### 2 Kings 7:12
The king got up in the night and said to his servants, "I will tell you what the Syrians have done. They know we are hungry, so they have left the camp to hide in the field. They are saying, 'When Israel comes out of the city, we will capture them alive and enter the city.'"

### 2 Kings 7:13
One servant answered, "Please let some men take five of the horses that are still left in the city. The people and animals left here are already near death. Let us send them and see."

### 2 Kings 7:14
Therefore they took two chariots with horses; and the king sent them out to the Syrian army, saying, "Go and see."

### 2 Kings 7:15
They followed the trail as far as the Jordan. The whole road was full of clothes and equipment the Syrians had thrown away as they hurried away. The messengers returned and told the king.

### 2 Kings 7:16
Then the people went out and plundered the Syrian camp. So fine flour and barley were sold cheaply, according to the Lord's word.

### 2 Kings 7:17
The king appointed the commander on whose hand he leaned to be in charge of the gate; and the people trampled over him in the gate, and he died as the man of God had said, who spoke when the king came down to him.

### 2 Kings 7:18
It happened as the man of God had spoken to the king, saying, "Two measures of barley for a silver piece, and a measure of fine flour for a silver piece, must be tomorrow about this time in the gate of Samaria;"

### 2 Kings 7:19
and that commander answered the man of God, and said, "Now, look, if the Lord made windows in heaven, might such a thing be?" and he said, "Look, you will see it with your eyes, but will not eat of it."

### 2 Kings 7:20
It happened like that to him; for the people trampled over him in the gate, and he died.
