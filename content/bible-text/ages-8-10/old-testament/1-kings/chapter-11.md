# 1 Kings 11

## Book
1 Kings

## Chapter
11

## Verses

### 1 Kings 11:1
King Solomon loved many foreign women, along with Pharaoh's daughter: women from Moab, Ammon, Edom, Sidon, and the Hittites.

### 1 Kings 11:2
They came from the nations about whom the Lord had told the people of Israel, "You must not marry them, and they must not marry you, because they will surely turn your heart after their gods." Solomon held fast to these women in love.

### 1 Kings 11:3
He had seven hundred wives, princesses, and three hundred secondary wives. His wives turned his heart away.

### 1 Kings 11:4
When Solomon was old, his wives turned his heart after false gods. His heart was not wholly devoted to the Lord his God, as David his father's heart had been.

### 1 Kings 11:5
For Solomon went after Ashtoreth the goddess of the Sidonians, and after Milcom the detestable sin of the Ammonites.

### 1 Kings 11:6
Solomon did what was evil in the Lord's sight, and didn't go fully after the Lord, as David his father did.

### 1 Kings 11:7
Then Solomon built a high worship place for Chemosh the detestable sin of Moab, on the mountain that is before Jerusalem, and for Molech the detestable sin of the Ammonites.

### 1 Kings 11:8
So he did for all his foreign wives, who burned sweet-smelling incense and sacrificed to their gods.

### 1 Kings 11:9
The Lord was angry with Solomon, because his heart had turned away from the Lord, the God of Israel, who had appeared to him twice.

### 1 Kings 11:10
The Lord had commanded Solomon not to follow false gods, but Solomon did not keep what the Lord commanded.

### 1 Kings 11:11
So the Lord said to Solomon, "Because this has been in your heart, and you have not kept my covenant and my laws that I commanded you, I will surely tear the kingdom from you and give it to your servant.

### 1 Kings 11:12
Nevertheless, I will not do it in your days, for David your father's sake; but I will tear it out of your son's hand.

### 1 Kings 11:13
However I will not tear away all the kingdom; but I will give one tribe to your son, for David my servant's sake, and for Jerusalem's sake that I have chosen."

### 1 Kings 11:14
The Lord raised up an adversary to Solomon: Hadad the Edomite. He was one of the king's offspring in Edom.

### 1 Kings 11:15
For when David was in Edom, and Joab the commander of the army had gone up to bury the killed, and had struck every male in Edom

### 1 Kings 11:16
(for Joab and all Israel remained there six months, until he had cut off every male in Edom);

### 1 Kings 11:17
Hadad ran away, he and certain Edomites of his father's servants with him, to go into Egypt, when Hadad was still a little child.

### 1 Kings 11:18
They got up out of Midian, and came to Paran; and they took men with them out of Paran, and they came to Egypt, to Pharaoh king of Egypt, who gave him a house, and appointed him food, and gave him land.

### 1 Kings 11:19
Hadad found great favor in the sight of Pharaoh, so that he gave him as wife the sister of his own wife, the sister of Tahpenes the queen.

### 1 Kings 11:20
Tahpenes's sister gave birth to Hadad's son Genubath, and Tahpenes weaned him in Pharaoh's house. Genubath lived in Pharaoh's house among Pharaoh's sons.

### 1 Kings 11:21
When Hadad heard in Egypt that David died and was buried with his fathers, and that Joab the commander of the army was dead, Hadad said to Pharaoh, "Let me depart, that I may go to my own country."

### 1 Kings 11:22
Pharaoh said to Hadad, "What have you lacked while you were with me? Why do you want to go back to your own country?" Hadad answered, "Nothing, but please let me go."

### 1 Kings 11:23
God raised up an adversary to him, Rezon the son of Eliada, who had run away from his lord Hadadezer king of Zobah.

### 1 Kings 11:24
He gathered men to himself, and became commander over a troop, when David killed them of Zobah. They went to Damascus, and lived there, and ruled in Damascus.

### 1 Kings 11:25
Rezon was an enemy of Israel all the days of Solomon, besides the trouble Hadad caused. He hated Israel and ruled over Syria.

### 1 Kings 11:26
Jeroboam the son of Nebat, an Ephraimite of Zeredah, a servant of Solomon, whose mother's name was Zeruah, a widow, also lifted up his hand against the king.

### 1 Kings 11:27
This was the reason why he lifted up his hand against the king: Solomon built Millo, and repaired the breach of his father David's city.

### 1 Kings 11:28
The man Jeroboam was a mighty man of valor; and Solomon saw the young man that he was industrious, and he put him in charge of all the labor of the house of Joseph.

### 1 Kings 11:29
At that time, Jeroboam went out of Jerusalem. The prophet Ahijah from Shiloh met him on the road. Ahijah was wearing a new cloak, and the two of them were alone in the field.

### 1 Kings 11:30
Ahijah took the new cloak he was wearing and tore it into twelve pieces.

### 1 Kings 11:31
Ahijah said to Jeroboam, "Take ten pieces for yourself, because the Lord, the God of Israel, says, 'Look, I will tear the kingdom out of Solomon's hand and give ten tribes to you.

### 1 Kings 11:32
(but he must have one tribe, for my servant David's sake and for Jerusalem's sake, the city that I have chosen out of all the tribes of Israel);

### 1 Kings 11:33
The Lord said this because they had forsaken him and worshiped Ashtoreth, the goddess of the Sidonians, Chemosh, the god of Moab, and Milcom, the god of the Ammonites. They had not walked in his ways or done what was right in his eyes, and they had not kept his laws and rules as David had done.

### 1 Kings 11:34
"However I will not take the whole kingdom out of his hand; but I will make him leader all the days of his life, for David my servant's sake whom I chose, who kept my commands and my laws;

### 1 Kings 11:35
but I will take the kingdom out of his son's hand, and will give it to you, even ten tribes.

### 1 Kings 11:36
I will give one tribe to his son, that David my servant may have a lamp always before me in Jerusalem, the city that I have chosen for myself to put my name there.

### 1 Kings 11:37
I will take you, and you will rule according to all that your soul desires, and must be king over Israel.

### 1 Kings 11:38
It must be, if you will listen to all that I command you, and will walk in my ways, and do what is right in my eyes, to keep my laws and my commands, as David my servant did; that I will be with you, and will build you a sure house, as I built for David, and will give Israel to you.

### 1 Kings 11:39
I will afflict the offspring of David for this, but not forever.' "

### 1 Kings 11:40
Therefore Solomon sought to kill Jeroboam; but Jeroboam got up, and ran away into Egypt, to Shishak king of Egypt, and was in Egypt until the death of Solomon.

### 1 Kings 11:41
Now the rest of the acts of Solomon, and all that he did, and his wisdom, aren't they written in the book of the acts of Solomon?

### 1 Kings 11:42
The time that Solomon ruled in Jerusalem over all Israel was forty years.

### 1 Kings 11:43
Solomon died and was buried with his fathers in his father David's city; and Rehoboam his son ruled in his place.
