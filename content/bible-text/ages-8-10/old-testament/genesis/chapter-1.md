# Genesis 1

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
1

## Verses

### Genesis 1:1
In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth.

### Genesis 1:2
The earth was formless and empty. Darkness covered the deep waters, and the Spirit of God was hovering over them.

### Genesis 1:3
Then God said, "Let there be light," and light came into being.

### Genesis 1:4
God saw that the light was good, and he separated the light from the darkness.

### Genesis 1:5
God called the light "Day," and the darkness he called "Night." Evening came, and morning came: the first day.

### Genesis 1:6
God said, "Let there be an expanse between the waters, to separate water from water."

### Genesis 1:7
So God made the expanse and separated the waters below it from the waters above it. And it was so.

### Genesis 1:8
God called the expanse "Sky." Evening came, and morning came: the second day.

### Genesis 1:9
God said, "Let the waters under the sky gather into one place, and let dry ground appear." And it was so.

### Genesis 1:10
God called the dry ground "Land," and the gathered waters he called "Seas." And God saw that it was good.

### Genesis 1:11
Then God said, "Let the land produce plants: seed-bearing plants and fruit trees with seed in their fruit, each according to its kind." And it was so.

### Genesis 1:12
The land produced plants: plants bearing seed and trees bearing fruit with seed in it, each according to its kind. And God saw that it was good.

### Genesis 1:13
Evening came, and morning came: the third day.

### Genesis 1:14
God said, "Let lights appear in the sky to separate the day from the night. Let them be signs for seasons, days, and years."

### Genesis 1:15
God said, "Let them be lights in the sky to shine on the earth." And it was so.

### Genesis 1:16
God made two great lights: the greater light to govern the day and the smaller light to govern the night. He also made the stars.

### Genesis 1:17
God set the lights in the sky to shine on the earth.

### Genesis 1:18
They governed the day and the night and separated light from darkness. And God saw that it was good.

### Genesis 1:19
Evening came, and morning came: the fourth day.

### Genesis 1:20
God said, "Let the waters swarm with living creatures, and let birds fly above the earth across the sky."

### Genesis 1:21
So God created the great sea creatures and every living creature that moves in the water, each according to its kind. He also created every winged bird according to its kind. And God saw that it was good.

### Genesis 1:22
God blessed them and said, "Be fruitful and increase in number. Fill the waters in the seas, and let birds increase on the earth."

### Genesis 1:23
Evening came, and morning came: the fifth day.

### Genesis 1:24
God said, "Let the land produce living creatures according to their kinds: livestock, creatures that move along the ground, and wild animals, each according to its kind." And it was so.

### Genesis 1:25
God made the wild animals according to their kinds, the livestock according to their kinds, and all the creatures that move along the ground according to their kinds. And God saw that it was good.

### Genesis 1:26
Then God said, "Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness. Let them rule over the fish of the sea, the birds of the sky, the livestock, the wild animals, and every creature that moves along the ground."

### Genesis 1:27
So God created mankind in his own image; in the image of God he created them; male and female he created them.

### Genesis 1:28
God blessed them and said, "Be fruitful and increase in number. Fill the earth and bring it under your care. Rule over the fish, the birds, and every living creature that moves on the earth."

### Genesis 1:29
Then God said, "I have given you every seed-bearing plant on the whole earth and every tree with fruit that has seed in it. They will be yours for food."

### Genesis 1:30
"And to every beast of the earth, every bird of the sky, and every creature that moves on the ground and has the breath of life, I have given every green plant for food." And it was so.

### Genesis 1:31
God saw all that he had made, and it was very good. Evening came, and morning came: the sixth day.
