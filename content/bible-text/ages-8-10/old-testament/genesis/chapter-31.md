# Genesis 31

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
31

## Verses

### Genesis 31:1
Jacob heard Laban's sons saying, "Jacob has taken everything that belonged to our father. He has gained all this wealth from what was our father's."

### Genesis 31:2
Jacob noticed that Laban's attitude toward him was no longer the same as before.

### Genesis 31:3
The Lord said to Jacob, "Return to the land of your fathers and to your relatives, and I will be with you."

### Genesis 31:4
Jacob sent for Rachel and Leah and called them to the field where his flock was.

### Genesis 31:5
He said to them, "I can see that your father's attitude toward me has changed, but the God of my father has been with me.

### Genesis 31:6
"You know that I have served your father with all my strength.

### Genesis 31:7
"Your father has deceived me and changed my wages ten times, but God has not allowed him to harm me.

### Genesis 31:8
"If he said, 'The speckled animals will be your wages,' then all the flock had speckled young. If he said, 'The streaked animals will be your wages,' then all the flock had streaked young.

### Genesis 31:9
"In this way, God has taken livestock from your father and given it to me.

### Genesis 31:10
"At the time when the flock was breeding, I looked up in a dream and saw that the male goats mating with the flock were streaked, speckled, and spotted.

### Genesis 31:11
"The angel of God spoke to me in the dream and said, 'Jacob.' I answered, 'Here I am.'

### Genesis 31:12
"He said, 'Look up and see that all the male goats mating with the flock are streaked, speckled, and spotted, because I have seen everything Laban is doing to you.

### Genesis 31:13
"'I am the God of Bethel, where you poured oil on the stone marker and made a serious promise to me. Now get up, leave this land, and return to the land of your relatives.'"

### Genesis 31:14
Rachel and Leah answered him, "Do we still have any share or family gift left in our father's house?

### Genesis 31:15
"Hasn't he treated us like outsiders? He sold us and has used up the money that belonged to us.

### Genesis 31:16
"All the wealth God has taken from our father belongs to us and to our children. So do everything God has told you to do."

### Genesis 31:17
Then Jacob got up and placed his sons and his wives on camels.

### Genesis 31:18
He drove away all his livestock and took all the possessions he had gained in Padan-aram, heading to his father Isaac in the land of Canaan.

### Genesis 31:19
While Laban had gone to cut the wool from his sheep, Rachel stole her father's household idols, the false gods kept in his home.

### Genesis 31:20
Jacob left secretly from Laban the Aramean, not telling him that he was leaving.

### Genesis 31:21
Jacob ran away with everything he had. He crossed the river and headed toward the hill country of Gilead.

### Genesis 31:22
On the third day, Laban was told that Jacob had run away.

### Genesis 31:23
Laban took his relatives with him and chased Jacob for seven days, catching up with him in the hill country of Gilead.

### Genesis 31:24
God came to Laban the Aramean in a dream at night and said, "Be careful not to try to turn Jacob back with kind words or threats."

### Genesis 31:25
Laban caught up with Jacob. Jacob had pitched his tent on the mountain, and Laban and his relatives pitched their tents in the hill country of Gilead.

### Genesis 31:26
Laban said to Jacob, "What have you done? You slipped away secretly and carried off my daughters like prisoners taken by the sword.

### Genesis 31:27
"Why did you run away secretly and hide it from me? If you had told me, I could have sent you away with celebration, songs, tambourines, and harps.

### Genesis 31:28
"You did not even let me kiss my grandchildren and my daughters goodbye. You have acted foolishly.

### Genesis 31:29
"I have the power to hurt you, but the God of your father spoke to me last night and said, 'Be careful not to try to turn Jacob back with kind words or threats.'

### Genesis 31:30
"Now, you left because you deeply longed for your father's house. But why did you steal my gods?"

### Genesis 31:31
Jacob answered Laban, "I was afraid. I thought you might take your daughters away from me by force.

### Genesis 31:32
"If you find your gods with anyone here, that person shall not live. In front of our relatives, look through what I have, and take whatever is yours." Jacob did not know Rachel had stolen them.

### Genesis 31:33
Laban went into Jacob's tent, Leah's tent, and the tents of the two servant-women, but he did not find the idols. Then he left Leah's tent and entered Rachel's tent.

### Genesis 31:34
Rachel had taken the household idols, put them inside the camel saddle, and sat on them. Laban searched the whole tent but did not find them.

### Genesis 31:35
Rachel said to her father, "Please do not be angry, my lord, that I cannot stand up before you. It is my monthly time as a woman." So Laban searched but did not find the idols.

### Genesis 31:36
Jacob became angry and confronted Laban. He said, "What is my offense? What sin have I committed, that you chased me so fiercely?

### Genesis 31:37
"You have searched all my belongings. What have you found from your household? Put it here before my relatives and yours, so they can judge between us.

### Genesis 31:38
"I have been with you these twenty years. Your female sheep and goats did not lose their young, and I did not eat the rams from your flock.

### Genesis 31:39
"I did not bring you animals torn by wild animals; I paid for the loss myself. You required payment from me for whatever was stolen by day or by night.

### Genesis 31:40
"This was my life: heat wore me out during the day, cold chilled me at night, and sleep left my eyes.

### Genesis 31:41
"For twenty years I lived in your house. I served you fourteen years for your two daughters and six years for your flock, and you changed my wages ten times.

### Genesis 31:42
"If the God of my father, the God of Abraham and the One Isaac fears, had not been with me, you would have sent me away empty-handed. God saw my suffering and the work of my hands, and last night he corrected you."

### Genesis 31:43
Laban answered Jacob, "The daughters are my daughters, the children are my children, and the flocks are my flocks. Everything you see is mine. But what can I do today to these daughters of mine or to the children they have had?

### Genesis 31:44
"Come now, let us make a serious agreement, you and I, and let it be a witness between us."

### Genesis 31:45
Jacob took a stone and set it up as a stone marker.

### Genesis 31:46
Jacob said to his relatives, "Gather stones." They gathered stones and made a pile, and they ate there by the pile.

### Genesis 31:47
Laban called it Jegarsahadutha, but Jacob called it Galeed.

### Genesis 31:48
Laban said, "This pile is a witness between you and me today." Therefore it was named Galeed.

### Genesis 31:49
It was also called Mizpah, because Laban said, "May the Lord watch between you and me when we are hidden from each other.

### Genesis 31:50
"If you mistreat my daughters, or if you take other wives besides my daughters, no person may be with us to see it, but God is witness between you and me."

### Genesis 31:51
Laban said to Jacob, "Look at this pile and this stone marker that I have set between you and me.

### Genesis 31:52
"This pile and this stone marker are witnesses that I will not cross this pile to harm you, and you will not cross this pile and stone marker to harm me.

### Genesis 31:53
"May the God of Abraham and the God of Nahor, the God of their father, judge between us." Jacob swore by the One his father Isaac feared.

### Genesis 31:54
Then Jacob offered a sacrifice on the mountain and called his relatives to eat bread. They ate and stayed all night on the mountain.

### Genesis 31:55
Early in the morning, Laban got up, kissed his grandchildren and his daughters, and blessed them. Then Laban left and returned to his place.
