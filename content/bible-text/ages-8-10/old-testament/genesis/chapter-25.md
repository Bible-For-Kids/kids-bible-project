# Genesis 25

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
25

## Verses

### Genesis 25:1
Abraham took another wife, and her name was Keturah.

### Genesis 25:2
She bore him Zimran, Jokshan, Medan, Midian, Ishbak, and Shuah.

### Genesis 25:3
Jokshan became the father of Sheba and Dedan. The sons of Dedan were Asshurim, Letushim, and Leummim.

### Genesis 25:4
The sons of Midian were Ephah, Epher, Hanoch, Abida, and Eldaah. All these were the children of Keturah.

### Genesis 25:5
Abraham gave everything he had to Isaac.

### Genesis 25:6
But Abraham gave gifts to the sons of his concubines. While he was still living, he sent them away from Isaac his son, eastward to the land of the east.

### Genesis 25:7
These were the years of Abraham's life: 175 years.

### Genesis 25:8
Then Abraham breathed his last and died at a good old age, an old man and full of years, and he was gathered to his people.

### Genesis 25:9
His sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him in the cave of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron son of Zohar the Hittite, near Mamre.

### Genesis 25:10
This was the field Abraham had bought from the sons of Heth. There Abraham was buried, and Sarah his wife also.

### Genesis 25:11
After Abraham's death, God blessed his son Isaac, and Isaac lived by Beer Lahai Roi.

### Genesis 25:12
These are the generations of Ishmael, Abraham's son, whom Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah's servant, bore to Abraham.

### Genesis 25:13
These are the names of Ishmael's sons, listed by their names and family lines: Nebaioth, Ishmael's firstborn, then Kedar, Adbeel, Mibsam,

### Genesis 25:14
Mishma, Dumah, Massa,

### Genesis 25:15
Hadar, Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah.

### Genesis 25:16
These were Ishmael's sons, and these were their names by their settlements and camps: twelve princes according to their nations.

### Genesis 25:17
Ishmael lived 137 years. Then he breathed his last and died, and he was gathered to his people.

### Genesis 25:18
His descendants lived from Havilah to Shur, which is east of Egypt as you go toward Assyria. He died in the presence of all his brothers.

### Genesis 25:19
These are the generations of Isaac, Abraham's son: Abraham became the father of Isaac.

### Genesis 25:20
Isaac was 40 years old when he took Rebekah as his wife. She was the daughter of Bethuel the Aramean from Paddan Aram and the sister of Laban the Aramean.

### Genesis 25:21
Isaac prayed to the Lord for his wife because she could not have children, and the Lord answered his prayer. Rebekah his wife became pregnant.

### Genesis 25:22
The children struggled together inside her, and she said, "If this is so, why is this happening to me?" So she went to ask the Lord.

### Genesis 25:23
The Lord said to her, "Two nations are in your womb, and two peoples will be separated from within you. One people will be stronger than the other, and the older will serve the younger."

### Genesis 25:24
When her time came to give birth, there were twins in her womb.

### Genesis 25:25
The first came out red, all over like a hairy garment, and they named him Esau.

### Genesis 25:26
After that, his brother came out holding Esau's heel, and he was named Jacob. Isaac was 60 years old when Rebekah bore them.

### Genesis 25:27
The boys grew up. Esau became a skillful hunter, a man of the field, while Jacob was a quiet man who lived in tents.

### Genesis 25:28
Isaac loved Esau because he ate of his venison, but Rebekah loved Jacob.

### Genesis 25:29
One day Jacob was cooking stew, and Esau came in from the field, exhausted.

### Genesis 25:30
Esau said to Jacob, "Please feed me some of that red stew, for I am exhausted." That is why he was called Edom.

### Genesis 25:31
Jacob said, "Sell me your birthright today."

### Genesis 25:32
Esau said, "Look, I am about to die. What good is this birthright to me?"

### Genesis 25:33
Jacob said, "Swear to me today." So Esau swore to him and sold his birthright to Jacob.

### Genesis 25:34
Then Jacob gave Esau bread and lentil stew. Esau ate and drank, got up, and went away. In this way Esau despised his birthright.
