# Genesis 21

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
21

## Verses

### Genesis 21:1
The Lord cared for Sarah as he had said, and the Lord did for Sarah what he had promised.

### Genesis 21:2
Sarah became pregnant and bore Abraham a son in his old age, at the very time God had promised him.

### Genesis 21:3
Abraham gave the name Isaac to the son Sarah bore him.

### Genesis 21:4
When his son Isaac was eight days old, Abraham gave him the covenant sign, as God had commanded.

### Genesis 21:5
Abraham was 100 years old when his son Isaac was born to him.

### Genesis 21:6
Sarah said, "God has brought me laughter, and everyone who hears about this will laugh with me."

### Genesis 21:7
She also said, "Who would have said to Abraham that Sarah would nurse children? Yet I have borne him a son in his old age."

### Genesis 21:8
The child grew and was weaned, and on the day Isaac was weaned, Abraham held a great feast.

### Genesis 21:9
Sarah saw that the son Hagar the Egyptian had borne to Abraham was mocking.

### Genesis 21:10
So she said to Abraham, "Send away that slave woman and her son, because that woman's son must not share the family inheritance with my son Isaac."

### Genesis 21:11
This matter distressed Abraham greatly because it concerned his son.

### Genesis 21:12
But God said to Abraham, "Do not be distressed about the boy and your slave woman. Listen to whatever Sarah tells you, because your promised family line will be counted through Isaac.

### Genesis 21:13
I will also make the son of the slave woman into a nation, because he is your child too."

### Genesis 21:14
Early the next morning, Abraham took bread and a water-skin bag and gave them to Hagar. He placed them on her shoulder and sent her away with the boy. She wandered in the wilderness of Beersheba.

### Genesis 21:15
When the water in the skin was gone, she put the boy under one of the bushes.

### Genesis 21:16
Then she went and sat down about as far away as an arrow can fly, because she thought, "I cannot watch the boy die." As she sat there, she began to sob.

### Genesis 21:17
God heard the boy crying, and the angel of God called to Hagar from heaven. He said, "What is wrong, Hagar? Do not be afraid. God has heard the boy where he is.

### Genesis 21:18
Get up, lift the boy up, and take him by the hand, because I will make him into a great nation."

### Genesis 21:19
Then God opened Hagar's eyes, and she saw a well of water. She went, filled the skin with water, and gave the boy a drink.

### Genesis 21:20
God was with the boy as he grew up. He lived in the wilderness and became an archer, a man skilled with a bow and arrows.

### Genesis 21:21
While he lived in the wilderness of Paran, his mother got a wife for him from Egypt.

### Genesis 21:22
At that time, Abimelek and Phicol, the commander of his army, said to Abraham, "God is with you in everything you do.

### Genesis 21:23
Now swear to me here before God that you will not deal falsely with me, my children, or my descendants. Show me and the land where you live the same kindness I have shown you."

### Genesis 21:24
Abraham said, "I swear it."

### Genesis 21:25
Then Abraham complained to Abimelek about a well of water that Abimelek's servants had seized.

### Genesis 21:26
Abimelek said, "I do not know who has done this. You did not tell me, and I only heard about it today."

### Genesis 21:27
So Abraham brought sheep and cattle and gave them to Abimelek, and the two men made a covenant, a serious promise agreement.

### Genesis 21:28
Abraham set apart seven female lambs from the flock.

### Genesis 21:29
Abimelek asked Abraham, "What is the meaning of these seven lambs you have set apart?"

### Genesis 21:30
Abraham answered, "Accept these seven lambs from my hand as a witness, a sign that I dug this well."

### Genesis 21:31
That is why the place was called Beersheba, because the two men swore an oath there.

### Genesis 21:32
After they made the covenant at Beersheba, Abimelek and Phicol returned to the land of the Philistines.

### Genesis 21:33
Abraham planted a tamarisk tree in Beersheba, and there he called on the name of the Lord, the Everlasting God who lives forever.

### Genesis 21:34
Abraham stayed in the land of the Philistines for a long time.
