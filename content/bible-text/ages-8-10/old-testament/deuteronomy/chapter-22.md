# Deuteronomy 22

## Book
Deuteronomy

## Chapter
22

## Verses

### Deuteronomy 22:1
If you see your brother's ox or sheep wandering away, you must not hide yourself from them. You must bring them back to your brother.

### Deuteronomy 22:2
If your brother isn't near to you, or if you don't know him, then you must bring it home to your house, and it will be with you until your brother comes looking for it, and you must give it back to him.

### Deuteronomy 22:3
Do the same with his donkey, his clothing, and every lost thing your brother has lost and you have found. Do not hide from helping.

### Deuteronomy 22:4
If you see your brother's donkey or ox fallen on the road, you must not hide yourself from them. You must help him lift it up again.

### Deuteronomy 22:5
A woman must not wear men's clothing, and a man must not put on women's clothing, because whoever does these things is hateful to the Lord your God.

### Deuteronomy 22:6
If you come across a bird's nest on the way, in any tree or on the ground, with young ones or eggs, and the mother bird sitting on the young, or on the eggs, you must not take the mother bird with the young.

### Deuteronomy 22:7
Let the mother bird go, but you may take the young for yourself, so things may go well with you and your days may be long.

### Deuteronomy 22:8
When you build a new house, make a railing around your roof, so no one falls from it and brings bloodguilt on your house.

### Deuteronomy 22:9
Do not plant your vineyard with two kinds of seed, or the whole harvest will be treated as set apart and not for ordinary use.

### Deuteronomy 22:10
You must not plow with an ox and a donkey together.

### Deuteronomy 22:11
You must not wear clothes of wool and linen woven together.

### Deuteronomy 22:12
Make tassels for yourselves on the four corners of the cloak you use to cover yourself.

### Deuteronomy 22:13
If a man takes a wife, acts with her as her husband, and then hates her,

### Deuteronomy 22:14
and accuses her with shameful words, gives her a bad name, and says, "I married this woman, but I did not find proof that she had kept herself for marriage,"

### Deuteronomy 22:15
then the young woman's father and mother must bring the proof that she had kept herself for marriage to the elders at the city gate.

### Deuteronomy 22:16
The young woman's father must tell the elders, "I gave my daughter to this man as his wife, and he hates her.

### Deuteronomy 22:17
He has accused her with shameful words and said he found no proof that she had kept herself for marriage. But here is the proof for my daughter." Then they must spread the cloth before the elders of the city.

### Deuteronomy 22:18
The elders of that city must take the man and discipline him.

### Deuteronomy 22:19
They must fine him one hundred silver pieces, and give them to the father of the young woman, because he has given a bad name to a young woman of Israel. She will be his wife. He may not divorce her all his days.

### Deuteronomy 22:20
But if the accusation is true, and no proof of the young woman's faithfulness is found,

### Deuteronomy 22:21
then they must bring the young woman to the door of her father's house, and the men of her city must throw stones at her until she dies, because she has done a disgraceful sin in Israel by being sexually unfaithful in her father's house. So you must remove the evil from among you.

### Deuteronomy 22:22
If a man is found acting with another man's wife in the way only marriage should have, then both of them must die, the man and the woman. So you must remove the evil from Israel.

### Deuteronomy 22:23
If a young woman is promised to be married and has kept herself for marriage, and a man finds her in the city and acts with her as only marriage should,

### Deuteronomy 22:24
then you must bring them both to the gate of that city and throw stones at them until they die: the young woman because she did not cry out for help in the city, and the man because he dishonored his neighbor's future wife. So you must remove the evil from among you.

### Deuteronomy 22:25
But if the man finds the young woman promised to be married out in the field, and he overpowers her and harms her in that way, then only the man who did this to her must die.

### Deuteronomy 22:26
Do nothing to the young woman. She has not done a sin worthy of death. This matter is like a man attacking his neighbor and killing him.

### Deuteronomy 22:27
The man found her in the field. The young woman promised to be married cried out, but there was no one there to save her.

### Deuteronomy 22:28
If a man finds a young woman who has kept herself for marriage, who is not promised to be married, and he takes hold of her and acts with her as only marriage should, and they are found,

### Deuteronomy 22:29
then the man must give the young woman's father fifty silver pieces. She will be his wife because of what he did to her, and he may not divorce her all his days.

### Deuteronomy 22:30
A man must not take his father's wife or dishonor his father's marriage bed.
