# Deuteronomy 23

## Book
Deuteronomy

## Chapter
23

## Verses

### Deuteronomy 23:1
A man whose private parts have been badly injured or cut off must not join the Lord's gathered people.

### Deuteronomy 23:2
A person born of a forbidden union must not join the Lord's gathered people; even to the tenth generation no one from his family may join the Lord's gathered people.

### Deuteronomy 23:3
An Ammonite or a Moabite must not join the Lord's gathered people; even to the tenth generation no one belonging to them may join the Lord's gathered people forever,

### Deuteronomy 23:4
because they didn't meet you with bread and with water on the way when you came out of Egypt, and because they hired against you Balaam the son of Beor from Pethor of Mesopotamia, to curse you.

### Deuteronomy 23:5
Nevertheless the Lord your God wouldn't listen to Balaam, but the Lord your God turned the curse into a blessing to you, because the Lord your God loved you.

### Deuteronomy 23:6
You must not seek their peace nor their prosperity all your days forever.

### Deuteronomy 23:7
You must not hate an Edomite, for he is your brother. You must not hate an Egyptian, because you lived as a foreigner in his land.

### Deuteronomy 23:8
The children of the third generation who are born to them may join the Lord's gathered people.

### Deuteronomy 23:9
When you go out and camp against your enemies, then you must keep yourselves from every evil thing.

### Deuteronomy 23:10
If there is among you any man who is not clean because of something that happens to his body at night, then he must go outside the camp. He must not come within the camp;

### Deuteronomy 23:11
but it will be, when evening comes, he must bathe himself in water. When the sun is down, he will come within the camp.

### Deuteronomy 23:12
You must have a place also outside of the camp where you go relieve yourself.

### Deuteronomy 23:13
You must have a small digging tool among your weapons. It will be, when you relieve yourself, you must dig with it, and must turn back and cover your waste;

### Deuteronomy 23:14
for the Lord your God walks in the middle of your camp, to deliver you, and to hand your enemies over to you. Therefore your camp will be holy, that he may not see an unclean thing in you, and turn away from you.

### Deuteronomy 23:15
You must not deliver to his master a servant who has escaped from his master to you.

### Deuteronomy 23:16
He must live with you, among you, in the place which he must choose in one of your towns, where it pleases him best. You must not oppress him.

### Deuteronomy 23:17
No daughter of Israel may live in sexual sin, and no son of Israel may do sexual sin in idol worship.

### Deuteronomy 23:18
You must not bring money earned through sexual sin into the house of the Lord your God for any vow; for that money is a detestable thing to the Lord your God.

### Deuteronomy 23:19
You must not lend on interest to your brother: interest of money, interest of food, interest of anything that is lent on interest.

### Deuteronomy 23:20
You may charge a foreigner interest; but you must not charge your brother interest, that the Lord your God may bless you in all that you put your hand to, in the land where you go in and take it.

### Deuteronomy 23:21
When you make a vow to the Lord your God, you must not be slow to keep it, for the Lord your God will surely require it of you; and it would be sin in you.

### Deuteronomy 23:22
But if you refrain from making a vow, it will be no sin in you.

### Deuteronomy 23:23
You must keep and do what you promised. Whatever you have vowed to the Lord your God as a free will offering, that you promised with your mouth, you must do.

### Deuteronomy 23:24
When you come into your neighbor's vineyard, then you may eat your fill of grapes at your own pleasure; but you must not put any in your container.

### Deuteronomy 23:25
When you come into your neighbor's grain field, then you may pick heads of grain with your hand; but you must not use a sickle on your neighbor's grain field.
