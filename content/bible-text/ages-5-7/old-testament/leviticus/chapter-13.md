# Leviticus 13

## Book
Leviticus

## Chapter
13

## Verses

### Leviticus 13:1
The Lord spoke to Moses and to Aaron, saying,

### Leviticus 13:2
"If a man has a swelling, a scab, or a bright spot on his skin, and it looks like a serious skin disease, he must be brought to Aaron the priest or to one of Aaron's sons, the priests.

### Leviticus 13:3
The priest must look closely at the mark on the skin. If the hair in the mark has turned white, and the mark looks deeper than the skin around it, it is a serious skin disease. The priest must say the man is not clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:4
But if the bright spot is white and does not look deeper than the skin, and the hair has not turned white, the priest must keep the person with the mark away from others for seven days.

### Leviticus 13:5
On the seventh day, the priest must look at him again. If the mark has stopped spreading, the priest must keep him away from others for seven more days.

### Leviticus 13:6
On the next seventh day, the priest must look at him again. If the mark has faded and has not spread, the priest must say he is clean for worship. It was only a scab. The man must wash his clothes, and he will be clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:7
But if the scab spreads on the skin after the man has shown himself to the priest to be made clean, he must show himself to the priest again.

### Leviticus 13:8
The priest must look at him. If the scab has spread on the skin, the priest must say he is not clean for worship. It is a serious disease.

### Leviticus 13:9
"When a man has a serious skin disease, he must be brought to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:10
The priest must look closely. If there is a white swelling on the skin, and it has turned the hair white, and there is raw skin in the swelling,

### Leviticus 13:11
it is a long-lasting serious disease in his skin. The priest must say he is not clean for worship. The priest must not keep him away for more watching, because the disease is already clear.

### Leviticus 13:12
"But if the serious disease breaks out all over the skin, and it covers the person from head to foot, as far as the priest can see,

### Leviticus 13:13
then the priest must look closely. If the disease has covered all his body and the skin has all turned white, the priest must say the person is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:14
But whenever raw skin appears on him, he is not clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:15
The priest must look at the raw skin and say he is not clean for worship, because the raw skin is not clean for worship. It is a serious disease.

### Leviticus 13:16
But if the raw skin turns white again, the person must come back to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:17
The priest must look at him. If the mark has turned white, the priest must say the person is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:18
"When the body has a boil on its skin, and it has healed,

### Leviticus 13:19
and in the place of the boil there is a white swelling, or a bright spot, reddish-white, then it must be shown to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:20
The priest must examine it. If its appearance is deeper than the skin, and its hair has turned white, then the priest must say he is not clean for worship. It is the serious skin-disease mark. It has broken out in the boil.

### Leviticus 13:21
But if the priest examines it, and there are no white hairs in it, and it isn't deeper than the skin, but is dim, then the priest must keep him away from others seven days.

### Leviticus 13:22
If it spreads in the skin, then the priest must say he is not clean for worship. It is a disease mark.

### Leviticus 13:23
But if the bright spot stays in its place, and hasn't spread, it is the scar from the boil; and the priest must say he is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:24
"Or when the body has a burn from fire on its skin, and the raw skin of the burn becomes a bright spot, reddish-white, or white,

### Leviticus 13:25
then the priest must examine it; and if the hair in the bright spot has turned white, and its appearance is deeper than the skin, it is serious disease. It has broken out in the burning, and the priest must say he is not clean for worship. It is the serious skin-disease mark.

### Leviticus 13:26
But if the priest examines it, and there is no white hair in the bright spot, and it isn't deeper than the skin, but has faded, then the priest must keep him away from others seven days.

### Leviticus 13:27
The priest must examine him on the seventh day. If it has spread in the skin, then the priest must say he is not clean for worship. It is the serious skin-disease mark.

### Leviticus 13:28
If the bright spot stays in its place, and hasn't spread in the skin, but is faded, it is the swelling from the burn, and the priest must say he is clean for worship, for it is the scar from the burn.

### Leviticus 13:29
"When a man or woman has a disease mark on the head or on the beard,

### Leviticus 13:30
then the priest must examine the disease mark; and if its appearance is deeper than the skin, and the hair in it is yellow and thin, then the priest must say he is not clean for worship. It is an itch. It is serious disease of the head or of the beard.

### Leviticus 13:31
If the priest looks at the itchy mark and it does not look deeper than the skin, and there is no black hair in it, the priest must keep the person with the itch away from others for seven days.

### Leviticus 13:32
On the seventh day the priest must examine the disease mark; and if the itch hasn't spread, and there is no yellow hair in it, and the appearance of the itch isn't deeper than the skin,

### Leviticus 13:33
Then the person must shave, but he must not shave the itchy place. The priest must keep the person with the itch away from others for seven more days.

### Leviticus 13:34
On the seventh day, the priest must examine the itch; and if the itch hasn't spread in the skin, and its appearance isn't deeper than the skin, then the priest must say he is clean for worship. He must wash his clothes and be clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:35
But if the itch spreads on the skin after the priest said he was clean for worship,

### Leviticus 13:36
then the priest must examine him; and if the itch has spread in the skin, the priest must not look for the yellow hair; he is not clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:37
But if the priest sees that the itch has stopped spreading and black hair has grown in it, then the itch is healed. He is clean for worship. The priest must say he is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:38
"When a man or a woman has bright spots in the skin of the body, even white bright spots,

### Leviticus 13:39
then the priest must examine them. If the bright spots on the skin of their body are a dull white, it is a harmless rash. It has broken out in the skin. He is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:40
"If a man's hair has fallen from his head, he is bald. He is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:41
If his hair has fallen off from the front part of his head, he is bald on the forehead. He is clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:42
But if a reddish-white disease mark is in the bald head or the bald forehead, it is serious disease breaking out in his bald head or his bald forehead.

### Leviticus 13:43
Then the priest must examine him. If the swelling of the disease mark is reddish-white in his bald head, or in his bald forehead, like the appearance of serious disease in the skin of the body,

### Leviticus 13:44
he has a serious disease. He is not clean for worship. The priest must surely say he is not clean for worship. His disease mark is on his head.

### Leviticus 13:45
The person with the serious skin disease must wear torn clothes, leave his hair loose, cover his upper lip, and call out, "Not clean for worship! Not clean for worship!"

### Leviticus 13:46
As long as the mark of disease is on him, he is not clean for worship. He must live alone outside the camp.

### Leviticus 13:47
"A mark like mildew can also appear in a piece of clothing, whether the clothing is made from wool or linen.

### Leviticus 13:48
It may appear in woven threads of linen or wool, in leather, or in anything made from leather.

### Leviticus 13:49
If the mark is greenish or reddish in the clothing, the leather, the woven threads, or anything made from leather, it is a serious mildew mark. It must be shown to the priest.

### Leviticus 13:50
The priest must look at the mark and keep the thing with the mark separate for seven days.

### Leviticus 13:51
On the seventh day, he must look at the mark again. If the mark has spread in the clothing, the woven threads, the leather, or anything made from leather, it is a destructive mildew. It is not clean for worship.

### Leviticus 13:52
He must burn the clothing, the woven cloth, or the leather thing where the mark is found, because it is a destructive mildew. It must be burned in the fire.

### Leviticus 13:53
"But if the priest looks at it and the mark has not spread in the clothing, the woven threads, or the leather thing,

### Leviticus 13:54
then the priest must tell them to wash the thing with the mark. Then he must keep it separate for seven more days.

### Leviticus 13:55
After the thing is washed, the priest must look at it again. If the mark has not changed color, even if it has not spread, it is not clean for worship. It must be burned in the fire, whether the mildew is on the inside or the outside.

### Leviticus 13:56
But if the priest looks and the mark has faded after washing, he must tear the marked part out of the clothing, the leather, or the woven cloth.

### Leviticus 13:57
If the mark appears again in the clothing, the woven cloth, or anything made from leather, it is spreading. Whatever has the mark must be burned in the fire.

### Leviticus 13:58
But if the mark leaves the clothing, woven cloth, or leather thing after it is washed, it must be washed a second time, and it will be clean for worship."

### Leviticus 13:59
This is the law for mildew marks in clothing made from wool or linen, in woven cloth, or in leather things. It teaches when to say something is clean for worship and when to say it is not clean for worship.
