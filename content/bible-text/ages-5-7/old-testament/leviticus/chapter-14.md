# Leviticus 14

## Book
Leviticus

## Chapter
14

## Verses

### Leviticus 14:1
The Lord spoke to Moses, saying,

### Leviticus 14:2
"This must be the law of the person with the serious disease in the day of his cleansing: He must be brought to the priest,

### Leviticus 14:3
and the priest must go out of the camp. The priest must examine him. If the serious mark of disease is healed in the person with the serious disease,

### Leviticus 14:4
then the priest must command them to take for the person who is to be cleansed two living clean birds, cedar wood, scarlet, and hyssop.

### Leviticus 14:5
The priest must command them to kill one of the birds in an earthen vessel over running water.

### Leviticus 14:6
As for the living bird, he must take it, the cedar wood, the scarlet, and the hyssop, and must dip them and the living bird in the blood of the bird that was killed over the running water.

### Leviticus 14:7
He must sprinkle on the person who is to be cleansed from the serious disease seven times, and must pronounce him clean, and must let the living bird go into the open field.

### Leviticus 14:8
"Whoever is to be cleansed must wash his clothes, and shave off all his hair, and bathe himself in water; and he will be clean. After that he must come into the camp, but must dwell outside his tent seven days.

### Leviticus 14:9
It must be on the seventh day, that he must shave all his hair off his head and his beard and his eyebrows, even all his hair he must shave off. He must wash his clothes, and he must bathe his body in water. Then he will be clean.

### Leviticus 14:10
"On the eighth day he must take two male lambs with nothing wrong with it, one ewe lamb a year old with nothing wrong with it, three tenths of an ephah of fine flour for a grain offering, mixed with oil, and one log of oil.

### Leviticus 14:11
The priest who cleanses him must set the man who is to be cleansed, and those things, before the Lord, at the door of the Tent of Meeting.

### Leviticus 14:12
"The priest must take one of the male lambs, and offer him for a guilt offering, with the log of oil, and wave them for a wave offering before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:13
He must kill the male lamb in the place where they kill the sin offering and the burnt offering, in the place of the sanctuary; for as the sin offering is the priest's, so is the guilt offering. It is most holy.

### Leviticus 14:14
The priest must take some of the blood of the guilt offering, and the priest must put it on the tip of the right ear of the person who is to be cleansed, and on the thumb of his right hand, and on the big toe of his right foot.

### Leviticus 14:15
The priest must take some of the log of oil, and pour it into the palm of his own left hand.

### Leviticus 14:16
The priest must dip his right finger in the oil that is in his left hand, and must sprinkle some of the oil with his finger seven times before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:17
The priest must put some of the rest of the oil that is in his hand on the tip of the right ear of the person who is to be cleansed, and on the thumb of his right hand, and on the big toe of his right foot, upon the blood of the guilt offering.

### Leviticus 14:18
The rest of the oil that is in the priest's hand he must put on the head of the person who is to be cleansed, and the priest must make atonement for him, helping make things right with God before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:19
"The priest must offer the sin offering, and make atonement for him, helping make things right with God who is to be cleansed because of his uncleanness. Afterward he must kill the burnt offering;

### Leviticus 14:20
then the priest must offer the burnt offering and the grain offering on the altar. The priest must make atonement for him, helping make things right with God, and he will be clean.

### Leviticus 14:21
"If he is poor, and can't afford so much, then he must take one male lamb for a guilt offering to be waved, to make atonement for him, helping make things right with God, and one tenth of an ephah of fine flour mixed with oil for a grain offering, and a log of oil;

### Leviticus 14:22
and two turtledoves, or two young pigeons, such as he is able to afford; and the one must be a sin offering, and the other a burnt offering.

### Leviticus 14:23
"On the eighth day he must bring them for his cleansing to the priest, to the door of the Tent of Meeting, before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:24
The priest must take the lamb of the guilt offering, and the log of oil, and the priest must wave them for a wave offering before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:25
He must kill the lamb of the guilt offering. The priest must take some of the blood of the guilt offering and put it on the tip of the right ear of the person who is to be cleansed, and on the thumb of his right hand, and on the big toe of his right foot.

### Leviticus 14:26
The priest must pour some of the oil into the palm of his own left hand;

### Leviticus 14:27
and the priest must sprinkle with his right finger some of the oil that is in his left hand seven times before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:28
Then the priest must put some of the oil that is in his hand on the tip of the right ear of the person who is to be cleansed, and on the thumb of his right hand, and on the big toe of his right foot, on the place of the blood of the guilt offering.

### Leviticus 14:29
The rest of the oil that is in the priest's hand he must put on the head of the person who is to be cleansed, to make atonement for him, helping make things right with God before the Lord.

### Leviticus 14:30
He must offer one of the turtledoves, or of the young pigeons, which ever he is able to afford,

### Leviticus 14:31
of the kind he is able to afford, the one for a sin offering, and the other for a burnt offering, with the grain offering. The priest must make atonement for him, helping make things right with God who is to be cleansed before the Lord."

### Leviticus 14:32
This is the law for him in whom is the serious mark of disease, who is not able to afford the sacrifice for his cleansing.

### Leviticus 14:33
The Lord spoke to Moses and to Aaron, saying,

### Leviticus 14:34
"When you have come into the land of Canaan, which I give to you for a possession, and I put a spreading mildew in a house in the land of your possession,

### Leviticus 14:35
then whoever owns the house must come and tell the priest, saying, 'There seems to me to be some sort of mark of disease in the house.

### Leviticus 14:36
The priest must command that they empty the house, before the priest goes in to examine the mark of disease, that all that is in the house not be made unclean. Afterward the priest must go in to inspect the house.

### Leviticus 14:37
He must examine the mark of disease; and if the mark of disease is in the walls of the house with hollow streaks, greenish or reddish, and it appears to be deeper than the wall,

### Leviticus 14:38
then the priest must go out of the house to the door of the house, and shut up the house seven days.

### Leviticus 14:39
The priest must come again on the seventh day, and look. If the mark of disease has spread in the walls of the house,

### Leviticus 14:40
then the priest must command that they take out the stones in which is the mark of disease, and cast them into an unclean place outside of the city.

### Leviticus 14:41
He must cause the inside of the house to be scraped all over. They must pour out the mortar that they scraped off outside of the city into an unclean place.

### Leviticus 14:42
They must take other stones, and put them in the place of those stones; and he must take other mortar, and must plaster the house.

### Leviticus 14:43
"If the mark of disease comes again, and breaks out in the house after he has taken out the stones, and after he has scraped the house, and after it was plastered,

### Leviticus 14:44
then the priest must come in and look; and if the mark of disease has spread in the house, it is a destructive mildew in the house. It is unclean.

### Leviticus 14:45
He must break down the house, its stones, and its timber, and all the house's mortar. He must carry them out of the city into an unclean place.

### Leviticus 14:46
"Moreover whoever goes into the house while it is shut up will be unclean until the evening.

### Leviticus 14:47
Whoever lies down in the house must wash his clothes; and whoever eats in the house must wash his clothes.

### Leviticus 14:48
"If the priest comes in, and examine it, and the mark of disease hasn't spread in the house, after the house was plastered, then the priest must pronounce the house clean, because the mark of disease is healed.

### Leviticus 14:49
To cleanse the house he must take two birds, cedar wood, scarlet, and hyssop.

### Leviticus 14:50
He must kill one of the birds in an earthen vessel over running water.

### Leviticus 14:51
He must take the cedar wood, the hyssop, the scarlet, and the living bird, and dip them in the blood of the slain bird, and in the running water, and sprinkle the house seven times.

### Leviticus 14:52
He must cleanse the house with the blood of the bird, and with the running water, with the living bird, with the cedar wood, with the hyssop, and with the scarlet;

### Leviticus 14:53
but he must let the living bird go out of the city into the open field. So must he make atonement for the house; and it will be clean."

### Leviticus 14:54
This is the law for any serious mark of disease, and for an itch,

### Leviticus 14:55
and for the destructive mildew of a garment, and for a house,

### Leviticus 14:56
and for a swelling, and for a scab, and for a bright spot;

### Leviticus 14:57
to teach when it is unclean, and when it is clean. This is the law of serious disease.
