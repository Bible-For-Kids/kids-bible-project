# 1 Kings 22

## Book
1 Kings

## Chapter
22

## Verses

### 1 Kings 22:1
They continued three years without war between Syria and Israel.

### 1 Kings 22:2
In the third year, Jehoshaphat the king of Judah came down to the king of Israel.

### 1 Kings 22:3
The king of Israel said to his servants, "You know Ramoth Gilead belongs to us, but we are sitting here and doing nothing to take it back from the king of Syria."

### 1 Kings 22:4
He said to Jehoshaphat, "Will you go with me to battle to Ramoth Gilead?" Jehoshaphat said to the king of Israel, "I am as you are, my people as your people, my horses as your horses."

### 1 Kings 22:5
Jehoshaphat said to the king of Israel, "Please inquire first for the Lord's word."

### 1 Kings 22:6
Then the king of Israel gathered about four hundred prophets and asked them, "Should I go fight for Ramoth Gilead, or should I hold back?" They said, "Go up, for the Lord will give it into the king's hand."

### 1 Kings 22:7
But Jehoshaphat said, "Isn't there here a prophet of the Lord, that we may inquire of him?"

### 1 Kings 22:8
The king of Israel said to Jehoshaphat, "There is still one man by whom we may ask the Lord: Micaiah son of Imlah. But I hate him, because he never speaks good messages about me, only disaster." Jehoshaphat said, "The king should not say that."

### 1 Kings 22:9
Then the king of Israel called an officer, and said, "Quickly get Micaiah the son of Imlah."

### 1 Kings 22:10
The king of Israel and Jehoshaphat king of Judah sat on their thrones, wearing their royal robes, in an open place at the gate of Samaria. All the prophets were speaking before them.

### 1 Kings 22:11
Zedekiah son of Chenaanah made iron horns for himself and said, "The Lord says, 'With these you will push the Syrians until they are destroyed.'"

### 1 Kings 22:12
All the prophets spoke the same way, saying, "Go up to Ramoth Gilead and succeed, for the Lord will give it into the king's hand."

### 1 Kings 22:13
The messenger who went to call Micaiah spoke to him, saying, "See now, the prophets declare good to the king with one mouth. Please let your word be like the word of one of them, and speak good."

### 1 Kings 22:14
Micaiah said, "As the Lord lives, what the Lord says to me, that I will speak."

### 1 Kings 22:15
When Micaiah came to the king, the king asked him, "Micaiah, should we go to Ramoth Gilead for battle, or should we hold back?" Micaiah answered, "Go up and succeed, and the Lord will give it into the king's hand."

### 1 Kings 22:16
The king said to him, "How many times must I make you swear to tell me nothing but the truth in the Lord's name?"

### 1 Kings 22:17
Micaiah said, "I saw all Israel scattered on the mountains like sheep with no shepherd. The Lord said, 'These people have no master. Let each one return home in peace.'"

### 1 Kings 22:18
The king of Israel said to Jehoshaphat, "Didn't I tell you he would not speak good messages about me, but only disaster?"

### 1 Kings 22:19
Micaiah said, "Therefore hear the Lord's word. I saw the Lord sitting on his throne, and all heaven's army standing by him on his right and on his left.

### 1 Kings 22:20
The Lord said, "Who will persuade Ahab, so that he will go up and fall at Ramoth Gilead?" One said one thing, and another said another.

### 1 Kings 22:21
Then a spirit came out and stood before the Lord, saying, "I will persuade him."

### 1 Kings 22:22
The Lord said to him, "How?" He said, "I will go out and be a lying spirit in the mouth of all his prophets." The Lord said, "You will persuade him, and you will succeed. Go out and do so."

### 1 Kings 22:23
Now look, the Lord has put a lying spirit in the mouth of all these prophets of yours, and the Lord has announced disaster for you."

### 1 Kings 22:24
Then Zedekiah the son of Chenaanah came near, and struck Micaiah on the cheek, and said, "Which way did the Lord's Spirit go from me to speak to you?"

### 1 Kings 22:25
Micaiah said, "Look, you will see on that day, when you go into an inner room to hide yourself."

### 1 Kings 22:26
The king of Israel said, "Take Micaiah, and carry him back to Amon the governor of the city, and to Joash the king's son.

### 1 Kings 22:27
Say, "The king says, 'Put this man in prison. Give him only hard prison bread and water until I come back in peace.'"

### 1 Kings 22:28
Micaiah said, "If you return at all in peace, the Lord has not spoken by me." He said, "Listen, all you people!"

### 1 Kings 22:29
So the king of Israel and Jehoshaphat the king of Judah went up to Ramoth Gilead.

### 1 Kings 22:30
The king of Israel said to Jehoshaphat, "I will disguise myself, and go into the battle; but you put on your robes." The king of Israel disguised himself, and went into the battle.

### 1 Kings 22:31
Now the king of Syria had commanded the thirty-two commanders of his chariots, saying, "Don't fight with small nor great, except only with the king of Israel."

### 1 Kings 22:32
When the commanders of the chariots saw Jehoshaphat, they said, "Surely that is the king of Israel!" and they came over to fight against him. Jehoshaphat cried out.

### 1 Kings 22:33
When the commanders of the chariots saw that it was not the king of Israel, they turned back from pursuing him.

### 1 Kings 22:34
But a certain man drew his bow without aiming at anyone in particular and struck the king of Israel between the joints of his armor. Ahab said to the driver of his chariot, "Turn around and carry me out of the battle, for I am wounded."

### 1 Kings 22:35
The battle increased that day. The king was propped up in his chariot facing the Syrians, and died at evening. The blood ran out of the wound into the bottom of the chariot.

### 1 Kings 22:36
As the sun was going down, a cry ran through the army: "Every man back to his city! Every man back to his land!"

### 1 Kings 22:37
So the king died and was brought to Samaria. They buried the king in Samaria.

### 1 Kings 22:38
They washed the chariot by the pool of Samaria, and the dogs licked up Ahab's blood, as the Lord's word had said.

### 1 Kings 22:39
Now the rest of the acts of Ahab, and all that he did, and the ivory house that he built, and all the cities that he built, aren't they written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Israel?

### 1 Kings 22:40
So Ahab died and was buried with his fathers; and Ahaziah his son ruled in his place.

### 1 Kings 22:41
Jehoshaphat the son of Asa began to rule over Judah in the fourth year of Ahab king of Israel.

### 1 Kings 22:42
Jehoshaphat was thirty-five years old when he began to rule; and he ruled twenty-five years in Jerusalem. His mother's name was Azubah the daughter of Shilhi.

### 1 Kings 22:43
He walked in all the way of Asa his father. He didn't turn away from it, doing what was right in the Lord's eyes. However the worship places on hills were not taken away. The people still sacrificed and burned sweet-smelling incense on the worship places on hills.

### 1 Kings 22:44
Jehoshaphat made peace with the king of Israel.

### 1 Kings 22:45
Now the rest of the acts of Jehoshaphat, and his might that he showed, and how he fought, aren't they written in the book of the chronicles of the kings of Judah?

### 1 Kings 22:46
The remnant of the people doing sexual sin in idol worship, that remained in the days of his father Asa, he put away out of the land.

### 1 Kings 22:47
There was no king in Edom. A deputy ruled.

### 1 Kings 22:48
Jehoshaphat made ships of Tarshish to go to Ophir for gold, but they didn't go; for the ships wrecked at Ezion Geber.

### 1 Kings 22:49
Then Ahaziah son of Ahab said to Jehoshaphat, "Let my servants sail with your servants in the ships." But Jehoshaphat would not allow it.

### 1 Kings 22:50
Jehoshaphat died and was buried with his fathers in the city of David his father. Jehoram his son ruled in his place.

### 1 Kings 22:51
Ahaziah the son of Ahab began to rule over Israel in Samaria in the seventeenth year of Jehoshaphat king of Judah, and he ruled two years over Israel.

### 1 Kings 22:52
He did what was evil in the Lord's sight, and walked in the way of his father, and in the way of his mother, and in the way of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, in that he made Israel to sin.

### 1 Kings 22:53
He served Baal and worshiped him, and provoked the Lord, the God of Israel, to anger in all the ways that his father had done so.
