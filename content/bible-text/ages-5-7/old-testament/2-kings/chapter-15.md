# 2 Kings 15

## Book
2 Kings

## Chapter
15

## Verses

### 2 Kings 15:1
In the twenty-seventh year of Jeroboam king of Israel, Azariah son of Amaziah king of Judah began to rule.

### 2 Kings 15:2
He was sixteen years old when he began to rule, and he ruled fifty-two years in Jerusalem. His mother's name was Jecoliah of Jerusalem.

### 2 Kings 15:3
Azariah did what was right in the Lord's eyes, according to all that Amaziah his father had done.

### 2 Kings 15:4
However the worship places on hills were not taken away. The people still sacrificed and burned sweet-smelling incense in the worship places on hills.

### 2 Kings 15:5
The Lord struck the king, so he had a serious skin disease until the day he died. He lived in a separate house, while Jotham the king's son was over the palace and judged the people of the land.

### 2 Kings 15:6
Now the rest of the acts of Azariah, and all that he did, they are written in the history book of the kings of Judah.

### 2 Kings 15:7
Azariah died and was buried with his fathers; and they buried him with his fathers in David's city: and Jotham his son ruled in his place.

### 2 Kings 15:8
In the thirty-eighth year of Azariah king of Judah, Zechariah the son of Jeroboam ruled over Israel in Samaria six months.

### 2 Kings 15:9
He did what was evil in the Lord's sight, as his fathers had done. He didn't depart from the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, that led Israel into sin.

### 2 Kings 15:10
Shallum the son of Jabesh made a secret plan against him, and struck him before the people, and killed him, and ruled in his place.

### 2 Kings 15:11
Now the rest of the acts of Zechariah, look, they are written in the history book of the kings of Israel.

### 2 Kings 15:12
This was the Lord's word that he spoke to Jehu, saying, "Your sons to the fourth generation must sit on the throne of Israel." So it came to pass.

### 2 Kings 15:13
Shallum the son of Jabesh began to rule in the thirty-ninth year of Uzziah king of Judah, and he ruled for a month in Samaria.

### 2 Kings 15:14
Menahem the son of Gadi went up from Tirzah, came to Samaria, struck Shallum the son of Jabesh in Samaria, killed him, and ruled in his place.

### 2 Kings 15:15
Now the rest of the acts of Shallum, and his secret plan which he made, look, they are written in the history book of the kings of Israel.

### 2 Kings 15:16
Then Menahem attacked Tiphsah, and all who were in it, and its border areas, from Tirzah. He attacked it because they didn't open their gates to him, and he ripped up all their women who were with child.

### 2 Kings 15:17
In the thirty ninth year of Azariah king of Judah, Menahem the son of Gadi began to rule over Israel for ten years in Samaria.

### 2 Kings 15:18
He did what was evil in the Lord's sight. He didn't depart all his days from the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, that led Israel into sin.

### 2 Kings 15:19
Pul king of Assyria came against the land, so Menahem gave him one thousand large weights of silver to gain his support and make his kingdom stronger.

### 2 Kings 15:20
Menahem collected the silver from Israel's wealthy men, fifty silver pieces from each man, to give it to Assyria's king. Then Assyria's king turned back and did not stay in the land.

### 2 Kings 15:21
Now the rest of the acts of Menahem, and all that he did, they are written in the history book of the kings of Israel.

### 2 Kings 15:22
Menahem died and was buried with his fathers, and Pekahiah his son ruled in his place.

### 2 Kings 15:23
In the fiftieth year of Azariah king of Judah, Pekahiah the son of Menahem began to rule over Israel in Samaria for two years.

### 2 Kings 15:24
He did what was evil in the Lord's sight. He didn't depart from the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, that led Israel into sin.

### 2 Kings 15:25
Pekah the son of Remaliah, his commander, made a secret plan against him and attacked him in Samaria, in the fortress of the king's house, with Argob and Arieh; and with him were fifty men of the Gileadites. He killed him, and ruled in his place.

### 2 Kings 15:26
Now the rest of the acts of Pekahiah, and all that he did, look, they are written in the history book of the kings of Israel.

### 2 Kings 15:27
In the fifty-second year of Azariah king of Judah, Pekah the son of Remaliah began to rule over Israel in Samaria for twenty years.

### 2 Kings 15:28
He did what was evil in the Lord's sight. He didn't depart from the sins of Jeroboam the son of Nebat, that led Israel into sin.

### 2 Kings 15:29
In the days of Pekah king of Israel, Tiglath Pileser king of Assyria came and captured many cities and regions of Israel. He carried the people away to Assyria.

### 2 Kings 15:30
Hoshea the son of Elah made a secret plan against Pekah the son of Remaliah, attacked him, killed him, and ruled in his place, in the twentieth year of Jotham the son of Uzziah.

### 2 Kings 15:31
Now the rest of the acts of Pekah, and all that he did, look, they are written in the history book of the kings of Israel.

### 2 Kings 15:32
In the second year of Pekah the son of Remaliah king of Israel, Jotham the son of Uzziah king of Judah began to rule.

### 2 Kings 15:33
He was twenty-five years old when he began to rule, and he ruled sixteen years in Jerusalem. His mother's name was Jerusha the daughter of Zadok.

### 2 Kings 15:34
He did what was right in the Lord's eyes. He did according to all that his father Uzziah had done.

### 2 Kings 15:35
However the worship places on hills were not taken away. The people still sacrificed and burned sweet-smelling incense in the worship places on hills. He built the upper gate of the Lord's house.

### 2 Kings 15:36
Now the rest of the acts of Jotham, and all that he did, they are written in the history book of the kings of Judah.

### 2 Kings 15:37
In those days, the Lord began to send Rezin the king of Syria and Pekah the son of Remaliah against Judah.

### 2 Kings 15:38
Jotham died and was buried with his fathers, and was buried with his fathers in his father David's city, and Ahaz his son ruled in his place.
