# 2 Kings 16

## Book
2 Kings

## Chapter
16

## Verses

### 2 Kings 16:1
In the seventeenth year of Pekah the son of Remaliah, Ahaz the son of Jotham king of Judah began to rule.

### 2 Kings 16:2
Ahaz was twenty years old when he began to rule, and he ruled sixteen years in Jerusalem. He did not do what was right in the Lord's eyes, as David his father had done.

### 2 Kings 16:3
Instead, he walked in the ways of the kings of Israel. He even burned his son as an offering, following the detestable practices of the nations the Lord had driven out before the people of Israel.

### 2 Kings 16:4
He sacrificed and burned sweet-smelling incense in the worship places on hills, on the hills, and under every green tree.

### 2 Kings 16:5
Then Rezin king of Syria and Pekah son of Remaliah king of Israel came up to Jerusalem to wage war. They surrounded Ahaz, but could not overcome him.

### 2 Kings 16:6
At that time Rezin king of Syria recovered Elath to Syria, and drove the Jews from Elath; and the Syrians came to Elath, and lived there, to this day.

### 2 Kings 16:7
So Ahaz sent messengers to Tiglath Pileser king of Assyria, saying, "I am your servant and your son. Come up and save me out of the hand of the king of Syria, and out of the hand of the king of Israel, who rise up against me."

### 2 Kings 16:8
Ahaz took the silver and gold that was found in the Lord's house, and in the treasures of the king's house, and sent it for a present to the king of Assyria.

### 2 Kings 16:9
The king of Assyria listened to him; and the king of Assyria went up against Damascus, and took it, and carried its people captive to Kir, and killed Rezin.

### 2 Kings 16:10
King Ahaz went to Damascus to meet Tiglath Pileser king of Assyria. He saw the altar in Damascus and sent Uriah the priest a pattern and plan for the altar, showing exactly how it was made.

### 2 Kings 16:11
Urijah the priest built an altar. According to all that king Ahaz had sent from Damascus, so Urijah the priest made it for the coming of king Ahaz from Damascus.

### 2 Kings 16:12
When the king had come from Damascus, the king saw the altar; and the king came near to the altar, and offered on it.

### 2 Kings 16:13
He burned his burned offering and his meal offering, poured his drink gift, and sprinkled the blood of his peace offerings on the altar.

### 2 Kings 16:14
The bronze altar, which was before the Lord, he brought from the front of the house, from between his altar and the Lord's house, and put it on the north side of his altar.

### 2 Kings 16:15
King Ahaz commanded Urijah the priest, saying, "On the great altar burn the morning burned offering, the evening meal offering, the king's burned offering, his meal offering, with the burned offering of all the people of the land, their meal offering, and their drink gifts; and sprinkle on it all the blood of the burned offering, and all the blood of the sacrifice; but the bronze altar will be for me to inquire by."

### 2 Kings 16:16
Urijah the priest did so, according to all that king Ahaz commanded.

### 2 Kings 16:17
King Ahaz cut off the panels of the bases, and removed the basin from off them, and took down the sea from off the bronze oxen that were under it, and put it on a pavement of stone.

### 2 Kings 16:18
Because of the king of Assyria, Ahaz removed the covered Sabbath entry and the king's outside entrance from the Lord's house.

### 2 Kings 16:19
The rest of what Ahaz did is written in the history book of the kings of Judah.

### 2 Kings 16:20
Ahaz died and was buried with his fathers, and was buried with his fathers in David's city, and Hezekiah his son ruled in his place.
