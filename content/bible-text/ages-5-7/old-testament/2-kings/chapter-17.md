# 2 Kings 17

## Book
2 Kings

## Chapter
17

## Verses

### 2 Kings 17:1
In the twelfth year of Ahaz king of Judah, Hoshea the son of Elah began to rule in Samaria over Israel for nine years.

### 2 Kings 17:2
Hoshea did what was evil in the Lord's sight, though not as wickedly as the kings of Israel before him.

### 2 Kings 17:3
Shalmaneser king of Assyria came against him. Hoshea became his servant and paid him required money each year.

### 2 Kings 17:4
But the king of Assyria found that Hoshea had made a secret plan. Hoshea had sent messengers to So king of Egypt and had stopped paying Assyria as he had done before. So the king of Assyria seized Hoshea, bound him, and put him in prison.

### 2 Kings 17:5
Then the king of Assyria came through the whole land, went up to Samaria, and surrounded it for three years.

### 2 Kings 17:6
In Hoshea's ninth year, the king of Assyria captured Samaria. He carried Israel away to Assyria and settled them in Halah, by the Habor River of Gozan, and in the cities of the Medes.

### 2 Kings 17:7
This happened because the people of Israel had sinned against the Lord their God. He had brought them up out of Egypt and out from under Pharaoh's power, but they honored false gods.

### 2 Kings 17:8
They walked in the customs of the nations the Lord had driven out before Israel, and in the sinful ways Israel's kings had made.

### 2 Kings 17:9
The people of Israel secretly did things that were not right against the Lord their God. In every city, from small watchtower towns to strong walled cities, they built worship places on hills.

### 2 Kings 17:10
They set up stone pillars and Asherah idol poles for themselves on every high hill and under every green tree.

### 2 Kings 17:11
There they burned sweet-smelling incense at all the worship places on hills, just as the nations before them had done. Their wicked actions made the Lord angry.

### 2 Kings 17:12
They served idols, even though the Lord had told them, "You must not do this thing."

### 2 Kings 17:13
Still, the Lord warned Israel and Judah through every prophet and seer. He said, "Turn away from your evil ways. Keep my commands and laws, the law I commanded your fathers and sent to you by my servants the prophets."

### 2 Kings 17:14
But they would not listen. They were stubborn like their fathers, who had not believed in the Lord their God.

### 2 Kings 17:15
They rejected the Lord's laws, his covenant with their fathers, and the warnings he had given them. They followed empty false worship, and their lives became empty. They copied the nations around them, even though the Lord had commanded them not to live that way.

### 2 Kings 17:16
They abandoned all the commands of the Lord their God. They made two metal calves and an Asherah idol, worshiped the sun, moon, and stars of the sky, and served Baal.

### 2 Kings 17:17
They burned their sons and daughters as offerings, used forbidden ways to tell the future, and sold themselves to do what was evil in the Lord's sight. They made him angry.

### 2 Kings 17:18
So the Lord was very angry with Israel and removed them from his sight. Only the tribe of Judah was left.

### 2 Kings 17:19
But Judah also did not keep the commands of the Lord their God. They walked in the sinful customs Israel had made.

### 2 Kings 17:20
The Lord rejected the people of Israel, let them suffer, and gave them over to raiders until he removed them from his sight.

### 2 Kings 17:21
The Lord tore Israel away from David's royal house. The people made Jeroboam son of Nebat king, and Jeroboam pulled Israel away from following the Lord and led them into a great sin.

### 2 Kings 17:22
The people of Israel kept walking in all the sins Jeroboam had done. They would not turn away from them.

### 2 Kings 17:23
At last the Lord removed Israel from his sight, just as he had spoken through all his servants the prophets. Israel was carried away from its own land to Assyria, where they remained.

### 2 Kings 17:24
Then the king of Assyria brought people from Babylon, Cuthah, Avva, Hamath, and Sepharvaim. He settled them in the cities of Samaria in place of the people of Israel. They took Samaria and lived in its cities.

### 2 Kings 17:25
When they first lived there, they did not fear the Lord. So the Lord sent lions among them, and some of the people were killed.

### 2 Kings 17:26
People told the king of Assyria, "The nations you carried away and placed in Samaria do not know the law of the god of the land. He has sent lions among them, and the lions are killing them because they do not know his law."

### 2 Kings 17:27
Then the king of Assyria commanded, "Send back one of the priests you carried away from Samaria. Let him live there and teach them the law of the god of the land."

### 2 Kings 17:28
So one of the priests who had been carried away from Samaria came and lived in Bethel. He taught them how they should fear the Lord.

### 2 Kings 17:29
But each nation still made its own gods and put them in the houses of the worship places on hills that the Samaritans had made. Each nation did this in the cities where they lived.

### 2 Kings 17:30
The men of Babylon made Succoth Benoth, and the men of Cuth made Nergal, and the men of Hamath made Ashima,

### 2 Kings 17:31
and the Avvites made Nibhaz and Tartak; and the Sepharvites burned their children in the fire to Adrammelech and Anammelech, the gods of Sepharvaim.

### 2 Kings 17:32
So the people feared the Lord, but they also chose their own priests for the worship places on hills. Those priests offered sacrifices for them in those houses of worship.

### 2 Kings 17:33
They feared the Lord, but they also served their own gods, following the customs of the nations from which they had been carried away.

### 2 Kings 17:34
To this day they still do what they did before. They do not truly fear the Lord or follow the laws, rules, teachings, and commands the Lord gave to the descendants of Jacob, whom he named Israel.

### 2 Kings 17:35
The Lord had made a covenant with them and commanded, "You must not fear false gods. You must not bow down to them, serve them, or sacrifice to them.

### 2 Kings 17:36
Instead, you must fear the Lord, who brought you up out of Egypt with great power and an outstretched arm. Bow down to him and sacrifice to him.

### 2 Kings 17:37
You must always be careful to keep the laws, rules, teachings, and commands he wrote for you. You must not fear false gods.

### 2 Kings 17:38
You must not forget the covenant I made with you. You must not fear false gods.

### 2 Kings 17:39
But you must fear the Lord your God, and he will rescue you from the power of all your enemies."

### 2 Kings 17:40
But they would not listen. They kept doing what they had done before.

### 2 Kings 17:41
So these nations feared the Lord, but they also served their carved idols. Their children and grandchildren did the same, just as their fathers had done.
