# Exodus 13

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
13

## Verses

### Exodus 13:1
The Lord spoke to Moses.

### Exodus 13:2
He said, "Set apart every firstborn for me. Every firstborn among the Israelites, both people and animals, belongs to me."

### Exodus 13:3
Moses said to the people, "Remember this day, the day you came out of Egypt, out of the house of slavery. The Lord brought you out with his strong hand. Do not eat bread made with yeast.

### Exodus 13:4
Today, in the month of Abib, you are leaving.

### Exodus 13:5
The Lord promised your fathers to give you the land of the Canaanites, Hittites, Amorites, Hivites, and Jebusites, a good land flowing with milk and honey. When he brings you there, keep this service in this month.

### Exodus 13:6
For seven days you must eat bread made without yeast. On the seventh day, there will be a feast to the Lord.

### Exodus 13:7
Eat unleavened bread for seven days. No leavened bread and no yeast should be seen anywhere with you.

### Exodus 13:8
On that day, tell your child, 'We do this because of what the Lord did for me when I came out of Egypt.'

### Exodus 13:9
This will be like a sign on your hand and a reminder before your eyes, so the Lord's law may be in your mouth. The Lord brought you out of Egypt with his strong hand.

### Exodus 13:10
Keep this rule at its proper time every year.

### Exodus 13:11
When the Lord brings you into the land of the Canaanites, as he promised you and your fathers, and gives it to you,

### Exodus 13:12
you must set apart for the Lord every firstborn male. The firstborn males of your animals belong to the Lord.

### Exodus 13:13
Every firstborn donkey must be bought back with a lamb. If you do not buy it back, you must break its neck. Every firstborn son among your children must be bought back.

### Exodus 13:14
In days to come, when your child asks, 'What does this mean?' you will say, 'With a strong hand the Lord brought us out of Egypt, out of the house of slavery.

### Exodus 13:15
When Pharaoh stubbornly refused to let us go, the Lord struck every firstborn in Egypt, both people and animals. That is why I sacrifice every firstborn male animal to the Lord, but I buy back every firstborn son.'

### Exodus 13:16
This will be like a sign on your hand and a reminder before your eyes, because the Lord brought us out of Egypt with his strong hand."

### Exodus 13:17
When Pharaoh let the people go, God did not lead them by the road through the Philistine land, even though it was shorter. God said, "If the people face war, they may change their minds and go back to Egypt."

### Exodus 13:18
So God led the people around by the wilderness road toward the Red Sea. The Israelites went up from Egypt in organized groups.

### Exodus 13:19
Moses took Joseph's bones with him, because Joseph had made the Israelites promise. Joseph had said, "God will surely come to help you, and you must carry my bones away from here with you."

### Exodus 13:20
The Israelites traveled from Succoth and camped at Etham, on the edge of the wilderness.

### Exodus 13:21
The Lord went ahead of them in a pillar of cloud by day to lead them, and in a pillar of fire by night to give them light. This way they could travel by day or night.

### Exodus 13:22
The pillar of cloud by day and the pillar of fire by night stayed in front of the people.
