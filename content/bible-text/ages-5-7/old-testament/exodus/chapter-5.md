# Exodus 5

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
5

## Verses

### Exodus 5:1
After this, Moses and Aaron went to Pharaoh and said, "This is what the Lord God of Israel says: Let my people go, so they may hold a feast for me in the wilderness."

### Exodus 5:2
Pharaoh said, "Who is the Lord, that I should obey his voice and let Israel go? I do not know the Lord, and I will not let Israel go."

### Exodus 5:3
They said, "The God of the Hebrews has met with us. Please let us go three days' journey into the desert and sacrifice to the Lord our God, so he does not strike us with disease or the sword."

### Exodus 5:4
The king of Egypt said to them, "Moses and Aaron, why are you pulling the people away from their work? Go back to your burdens."

### Exodus 5:5
Pharaoh also said, "Look, the people of the land are many now, and you are making them stop their work."

### Exodus 5:6
That same day Pharaoh gave orders to the taskmasters over the people and to their officers.

### Exodus 5:7
He said, "Do not give the people straw for making bricks anymore. Let them go and gather straw for themselves.

### Exodus 5:8
"But make them produce the same number of bricks as before. Do not make the number smaller. They are idle, and that is why they cry, 'Let us go and sacrifice to our God.'

### Exodus 5:9
"Make the work harder for the men. Then they will keep working and will not listen to false words."

### Exodus 5:10
The taskmasters and officers went out and told the people, "This is what Pharaoh says: I will not give you straw.

### Exodus 5:11
"Go and get straw for yourselves wherever you can find it, but your work will not be made any less."

### Exodus 5:12
So the people spread out through all the land of Egypt to gather dry stalks instead of straw.

### Exodus 5:13
The taskmasters pushed them hard and said, "Finish your daily work, just as you did when straw was given to you."

### Exodus 5:14
Pharaoh's taskmasters beat the Israelite officers they had put over the people. They asked, "Why did you not finish your full number of bricks yesterday or today, as you did before?"

### Exodus 5:15
Then the Israelite officers went and cried out to Pharaoh, "Why are you treating your servants this way?

### Exodus 5:16
"No straw is given to your servants, but they still tell us, 'Make bricks.' Your servants are beaten, but the fault is with your own people."

### Exodus 5:17
Pharaoh said, "You are idle, idle. That is why you say, 'Let us go and sacrifice to the Lord.'

### Exodus 5:18
"Now go and work. No straw will be given to you, but you must still make the same number of bricks."

### Exodus 5:19
The Israelite officers saw that they were in trouble when they were told, "You must not make fewer bricks each day."

### Exodus 5:20
As they left Pharaoh, they met Moses and Aaron, who were standing there waiting for them.

### Exodus 5:21
They said to Moses and Aaron, "May the Lord look at you and judge, because you have made Pharaoh and his servants hate us. You have put a sword in their hand to kill us."

### Exodus 5:22
Moses went back to the Lord and said, "Lord, why have you brought trouble on this people? Why did you send me?

### Exodus 5:23
"Since I came to Pharaoh to speak in your name, he has done evil to this people, and you have not delivered your people at all."
