# Exodus 2

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
2

## Verses

### Exodus 2:1
A man from the family of Levi married a woman from the family of Levi.

### Exodus 2:2
The woman had a baby boy. She saw that he was a beautiful child, so she hid him for three months.

### Exodus 2:3
When she could not hide him any longer, she made a little basket from reeds and covered it so water could not get in. She put the baby in the basket and set it among the reeds by the riverbank.

### Exodus 2:4
The baby's sister stood far away and watched to see what would happen to him.

### Exodus 2:5
Pharaoh's daughter came down to the river to wash, and her servants walked along the riverbank. She saw the basket among the reeds and sent her servant to bring it to her.

### Exodus 2:6
When she opened the basket, she saw the child. The baby was crying, and she felt sorry for him. She said, "This is one of the Hebrew children."

### Exodus 2:7
Then the baby's sister asked Pharaoh's daughter, "Should I go and call a Hebrew woman to nurse the child for you?"

### Exodus 2:8
Pharaoh's daughter said, "Go." So the girl went and called the baby's own mother.

### Exodus 2:9
Pharaoh's daughter said to the mother, "Take this child and nurse him for me, and I will pay you." So the woman took the baby and cared for him.

### Exodus 2:10
When the child grew older, his mother brought him to Pharaoh's daughter, and he became her son. She named him Moses, saying, "I drew him out of the water."

### Exodus 2:11
When Moses was grown, he went out to see his own people and saw how hard they had to work. He saw an Egyptian hitting a Hebrew man, one of his own people.

### Exodus 2:12
Moses looked all around. When he saw no one nearby, he killed the Egyptian and hid him in the sand.

### Exodus 2:13
The next day Moses went out again. He saw two Hebrew men fighting, and he said to the one who was wrong, "Why are you hitting your neighbor?"

### Exodus 2:14
The man said, "Who made you a ruler and judge over us? Are you going to kill me the way you killed the Egyptian?" Moses became afraid and thought, "People know what I did."

### Exodus 2:15
When Pharaoh heard about it, he wanted to kill Moses. But Moses ran away from Pharaoh and lived in the land of Midian. There he sat down by a well.

### Exodus 2:16
The priest of Midian had seven daughters. They came to the well to draw water and fill the troughs for their father's flock.

### Exodus 2:17
Some shepherds came and pushed them away, but Moses stood up and helped them. Then he gave water to their flock.

### Exodus 2:18
When the daughters came home to their father Reuel, he asked, "How did you come home so quickly today?"

### Exodus 2:19
They said, "An Egyptian rescued us from the shepherds. He also drew water for us and watered the flock."

### Exodus 2:20
Their father said, "Where is he? Why did you leave the man there? Call him here so he can eat with us."

### Exodus 2:21
Moses agreed to stay with the man, and the man gave Moses his daughter Zipporah to be his wife.

### Exodus 2:22
Zipporah had a son. Moses named him Gershom, saying, "I have been a stranger in a land that is not my own."

### Exodus 2:23
After many days, the king of Egypt died. The children of Israel still groaned because they were slaves, and they cried for help. Their cry went up to God.

### Exodus 2:24
God heard their groaning. God remembered his covenant with Abraham, Isaac, and Jacob.

### Exodus 2:25
God saw the children of Israel, and God knew their pain.
