# Exodus 18

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
18

## Verses

### Exodus 18:1
Jethro, the priest of Midian and Moses' father-in-law, heard all that God had done for Moses and for Israel, God's people. He heard that the Lord had brought Israel out of Egypt.

### Exodus 18:2
Jethro had taken care of Zipporah, Moses' wife, after Moses had sent her back.

### Exodus 18:3
He also had Moses' two sons. One son was named Gershom, because Moses had said, "I have been a stranger in another land."

### Exodus 18:4
The other son was named Eliezer, because Moses had said, "The God of my father helped me and saved me from Pharaoh's sword."

### Exodus 18:5
Jethro came to Moses in the wilderness, bringing Moses' wife and sons to the mountain of God where Moses was camped.

### Exodus 18:6
Jethro sent word to Moses, "I, your father-in-law Jethro, am coming to you with your wife and her two sons."

### Exodus 18:7
Moses went out to meet his father-in-law. He bowed down and kissed him. They asked how each other was doing, and they went into the tent.

### Exodus 18:8
Moses told his father-in-law everything the Lord had done to Pharaoh and Egypt for Israel's sake. He told him about all the troubles on the journey and how the Lord rescued them.

### Exodus 18:9
Jethro was glad for all the good the Lord had done for Israel, because the Lord had rescued them from Egypt's power.

### Exodus 18:10
Jethro said, "Blessed be the Lord, who rescued you from Egypt and from Pharaoh, and who rescued the people from Egypt's power."

### Exodus 18:11
"Now I know that the Lord is greater than all gods, because he rescued the people when the Egyptians acted proudly against them."

### Exodus 18:12
Jethro brought a burned offering and sacrifices to God. Aaron and all the elders of Israel came to eat bread with Moses' father-in-law before God.

### Exodus 18:13
The next day Moses sat to judge the people. The people stood around Moses from morning until evening.

### Exodus 18:14
When Moses' father-in-law saw all that Moses was doing for the people, he said, "What is this you are doing? Why do you sit alone while all the people stand around you from morning until evening?"

### Exodus 18:15
Moses said to his father-in-law, "Because the people come to me to ask God what to do."

### Exodus 18:16
"When they have a problem, they come to me. I decide between one person and another, and I teach them God's laws and instructions."

### Exodus 18:17
Moses' father-in-law said to him, "What you are doing is not good."

### Exodus 18:18
"You and these people with you will wear yourselves out. This work is too heavy for you. You cannot do it alone."

### Exodus 18:19
"Listen to my advice, and may God be with you. You should stand before God for the people and bring their problems to God."

### Exodus 18:20
"Teach them the laws and instructions. Show them the way they should walk and the work they should do."

### Exodus 18:21
"Also choose able men from all the people, men who respect God, love truth, and hate dishonest gain. Put them over groups of thousands, hundreds, fifties, and tens."

### Exodus 18:22
"Let them judge the people at all times. They can bring every hard case to you, but they can judge the smaller cases themselves. That will make the load lighter, because they will share it with you."

### Exodus 18:23
"If you do this, and God commands it, you will be able to keep going, and all these people will go home in peace."

### Exodus 18:24
Moses listened to his father-in-law and did everything he said.

### Exodus 18:25
Moses chose able men from all Israel and made them leaders over the people, over thousands, hundreds, fifties, and tens.

### Exodus 18:26
They judged the people at all times. They brought the hard cases to Moses, but they judged the smaller cases themselves.

### Exodus 18:27
Then Moses let his father-in-law leave, and Jethro went back to his own land.
