# Exodus 34

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
34

## Verses

### Exodus 34:1
The Lord said to Moses, "Cut two stone tablets like the first ones. I will write on these tablets the words that were on the first tablets, the ones you broke."

### Exodus 34:2
"Be ready in the morning. Come up Mount Sinai in the morning and stand before me on the top of the mountain."

### Exodus 34:3
"No one may come up with you. No one may even be seen anywhere on the mountain. Do not let the flocks or herds feed in front of that mountain."

### Exodus 34:4
So Moses cut two stone tablets like the first ones. He got up early in the morning and went up Mount Sinai, as the Lord commanded him. He carried the two stone tablets in his hands.

### Exodus 34:5
The Lord came down in the cloud, stood with Moses there, and spoke the name of the Lord.

### Exodus 34:6
The Lord passed in front of Moses and said, "The Lord, the Lord God, is merciful and gracious, slow to anger, and full of goodness and truth."

### Exodus 34:7
"He keeps mercy for thousands. He forgives guilt, rebellion, and sin. Yet he will not leave the guilty unpunished, and the results of sin can reach children and grandchildren, to the third and fourth generation."

### Exodus 34:8
Moses quickly bowed his head down to the ground and worshiped.

### Exodus 34:9
He said, "Lord, if I have found favor with you, please go among us. These are stubborn people, but please forgive our guilt and our sin, and take us as your own."

### Exodus 34:10
The Lord said, "I am making a covenant. In front of all your people I will do wonders that have never been done in all the earth or in any nation. All the people around you will see the work of the Lord, because what I will do with you is awesome."

### Exodus 34:11
"Obey what I command you today. I will drive out ahead of you the Amorites, Canaanites, Hittites, Perizzites, Hivites, and Jebusites."

### Exodus 34:12
"Be careful not to make a covenant with the people who live in the land where you are going. If you do, it will become a trap among you."

### Exodus 34:13
"Instead, tear down their altars, break their sacred pillars, and cut down their carved poles."

### Exodus 34:14
"You must not worship any other god, because the Lord, whose name is Jealous, is a jealous God."

### Exodus 34:15
"Do not make a covenant with the people of the land. If they worship their gods and sacrifice to them, they may invite you, and you may eat from their sacrifice."

### Exodus 34:16
"You might take their daughters as wives for your sons. Their daughters may worship their gods and lead your sons to worship those gods too."

### Exodus 34:17
"Do not make gods from melted metal for yourselves."

### Exodus 34:18
"Keep the Feast of Bread Made Without Yeast. For seven days eat bread made without yeast, as I commanded you, at the set time in the month of Abib, because in that month you came out of Egypt."

### Exodus 34:19
"Every firstborn male belongs to me, including the firstborn males from your cattle, whether oxen or sheep."

### Exodus 34:20
"Redeem the firstborn donkey with a lamb. If you do not redeem it, break its neck. Redeem all your firstborn sons. No one may come before me with empty hands."

### Exodus 34:21
"Work for six days, but on the seventh day rest. Even during plowing time and harvest time, you must rest."

### Exodus 34:22
"Celebrate the Feast of Weeks with the firstfruits of the wheat harvest, and celebrate the Feast of Ingathering at the end of the year."

### Exodus 34:23
"Three times each year, all your males must appear before the Lord God, the God of Israel."

### Exodus 34:24
"I will drive out the nations before you and make your borders larger. No one will want to take your land when you go up three times each year to appear before the Lord your God."

### Exodus 34:25
"Do not offer the blood of my sacrifice with bread made with yeast. Do not let the sacrifice from the Passover Feast stay until morning."

### Exodus 34:26
"Bring the first of the firstfruits of your land to the house of the Lord your God. Do not boil a young goat in its mother's milk."

### Exodus 34:27
The Lord said to Moses, "Write these words, because with these words I have made a covenant with you and with Israel."

### Exodus 34:28
Moses was there with the Lord forty days and forty nights. He did not eat bread or drink water. He wrote on the tablets the words of the covenant, the Ten Commandments.

### Exodus 34:29
When Moses came down from Mount Sinai, he had the two tablets of testimony in his hands. Moses did not know that the skin of his face was shining because he had been speaking with the Lord.

### Exodus 34:30
Aaron and all the people of Israel saw Moses, and his face was shining. They were afraid to come near him.

### Exodus 34:31
Moses called to them. Aaron and all the leaders of the people came back to him, and Moses spoke with them.

### Exodus 34:32
After that, all the people of Israel came near. Moses gave them all the commands the Lord had spoken to him on Mount Sinai.

### Exodus 34:33
When Moses finished speaking with them, he put a curtain over his face.

### Exodus 34:34
But when Moses went in before the Lord to speak with him, he took the curtain off until he came out. Then he came out and told the people of Israel what he had been commanded.

### Exodus 34:35
The people of Israel saw that Moses' face was shining. Then Moses put the curtain over his face again until he went in to speak with the Lord.
