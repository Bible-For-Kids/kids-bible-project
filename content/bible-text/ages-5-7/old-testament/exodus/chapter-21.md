# Exodus 21

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
21

## Verses

### Exodus 21:1
These are the rules God told Moses to place before the people.

### Exodus 21:2
"If you buy a Hebrew servant, he must serve for six years. In the seventh year, he must go free without paying anything."

### Exodus 21:3
"If he came alone, he must leave alone. If he was married when he came, his wife must leave with him."

### Exodus 21:4
"If his master gave him a wife, and she had sons or daughters, the wife and children would belong to her master, and the man would leave by himself."

### Exodus 21:5
"But if the servant clearly says, 'I love my master, my wife, and my children, and I do not want to go free,'"

### Exodus 21:6
"then his master must bring him before the judges. He must bring him to the door or doorpost, mark his ear with an awl, and the servant will serve him for life."

### Exodus 21:7
"If a man sells his daughter as a female servant, she must not be set free in the same way male servants are."

### Exodus 21:8
"If she does not please the master who chose her for himself, he must let her be bought back. He has no right to sell her to foreigners, because he has treated her unfairly."

### Exodus 21:9
"If he chooses her for his son, he must treat her like a daughter."

### Exodus 21:10
"If he marries another wife, he must not take away the first woman's food, clothing, or marriage rights."

### Exodus 21:11
"If he does not give her these three things, she may leave free without paying money."

### Exodus 21:12
"Anyone who hits another person and causes that person to die must surely be put to death."

### Exodus 21:13
"But if it was not planned, and God allowed it to happen, I will choose a place where that person may run for safety."

### Exodus 21:14
"If someone plans with trickery to kill a neighbor, take that person even from my altar, so he may die."

### Exodus 21:15
"Anyone who strikes his father or mother must surely be put to death."

### Exodus 21:16
"Anyone who steals a person and sells him, or is found holding him, must surely be put to death."

### Exodus 21:17
"Anyone who curses his father or mother must surely be put to death."

### Exodus 21:18
"If two men fight, and one hits the other with a stone or with his fist, and the hurt man does not die but must stay in bed,"

### Exodus 21:19
"then if the hurt man gets up and walks outside with his staff, the one who hit him will not be put to death. But he must pay for the lost time and make sure the man is healed."

### Exodus 21:20
"If a man hits his male or female servant with a rod, and the servant dies right away, the man must surely be punished."

### Exodus 21:21
"But if the servant lives a day or two, the man will not be punished in that same way, because the servant was counted as his property."

### Exodus 21:22
"If men fight and hurt a pregnant woman so her baby comes early, but no other harm follows, the guilty man must pay what the woman's husband asks and the judges allow."

### Exodus 21:23
"But if more harm follows, then the punishment must match the harm: life for life,"

### Exodus 21:24
"eye for eye, tooth for tooth, hand for hand, foot for foot,"

### Exodus 21:25
"burn for burn, wound for wound, and bruise for bruise."

### Exodus 21:26
"If a man hits the eye of his male or female servant and destroys it, he must let the servant go free because of the eye."

### Exodus 21:27
"If he knocks out his male or female servant's tooth, he must let the servant go free because of the tooth."

### Exodus 21:28
"If an ox gores a man or woman so that the person dies, the ox must surely be stoned, and its meat must not be eaten. But the owner will not be punished."

### Exodus 21:29
"But if the ox had often pushed with its horns before, and the owner had been warned but did not keep it in, and it killed a man or woman, the ox must be stoned, and its owner must also be put to death."

### Exodus 21:30
"If a payment is required from the owner to spare his life, he must pay whatever is set for him."

### Exodus 21:31
"If the ox gores a son or a daughter, the same rule must be followed."

### Exodus 21:32
"If the ox gores a male or female servant, the owner must give thirty shekels of silver to the servant's master, and the ox must be stoned."

### Exodus 21:33
"If a man opens a pit, or digs a pit and does not cover it, and an ox or donkey falls into it,"

### Exodus 21:34
"the owner of the pit must make it right. He must pay money to the animal's owner, and the dead animal will be his."

### Exodus 21:35
"If one man's ox hurts another man's ox so that it dies, they must sell the living ox and share the money. They must also share the dead ox."

### Exodus 21:36
"But if everyone knew the ox had pushed with its horns before, and its owner did not keep it in, he must pay ox for ox, and the dead ox will be his."
