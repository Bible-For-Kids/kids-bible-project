# Exodus 11

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
11

## Verses

### Exodus 11:1
The Lord told Moses, "I will bring one more trouble on Pharaoh and on Egypt. After that, Pharaoh will let you go. When he lets you go, he will hurry you out completely."

### Exodus 11:2
The Lord told Moses to speak to the people. Each man and woman was to ask their Egyptian neighbors for silver and gold jewelry.

### Exodus 11:3
The Lord made the Egyptians look kindly on the Israelites. Moses was also greatly respected in Egypt by Pharaoh's servants and by the people.

### Exodus 11:4
Moses said to Pharaoh, "This is what the Lord says: About midnight I will go through Egypt.

### Exodus 11:5
Every firstborn in Egypt will die, from Pharaoh's firstborn child who sits near the throne to the firstborn child of the servant girl at the mill, and even the firstborn of the animals.

### Exodus 11:6
There will be loud crying all through Egypt, greater than any crying there has ever been or ever will be again.

### Exodus 11:7
But among the Israelites, not even a dog will bark against a person or an animal. Then you will know that the Lord makes a difference between Egypt and Israel.

### Exodus 11:8
All your servants will come down to me and bow before me. They will say, 'Go out, you and all the people who follow you.' After that, I will go." Then Moses left Pharaoh, very angry.

### Exodus 11:9
The Lord had told Moses, "Pharaoh will not listen to you, so that my wonders will be shown even more in Egypt."

### Exodus 11:10
Moses and Aaron did all these wonders in front of Pharaoh. But the Lord hardened Pharaoh's heart, and Pharaoh would not let the Israelites leave his land.
