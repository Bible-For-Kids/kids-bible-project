# Exodus 19

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
19

## Verses

### Exodus 19:1
In the third month after the people of Israel left Egypt, on that same day, they came into the wilderness of Sinai.

### Exodus 19:2
They left Rephidim, came to the wilderness of Sinai, and camped there. Israel camped in front of the mountain.

### Exodus 19:3
Moses went up to God, and the Lord called to him from the mountain. He said, "Say this to the family of Jacob and tell the people of Israel."

### Exodus 19:4
"You saw what I did to the Egyptians, and how I carried you on eagles' wings and brought you to myself."

### Exodus 19:5
"Now, if you truly obey my voice and keep my covenant, you will be my special treasure among all peoples, because all the earth is mine."

### Exodus 19:6
"You will be to me a kingdom of priests and a holy nation. These are the words you must speak to the people of Israel."

### Exodus 19:7
Moses came and called the elders of the people. He told them all the words the Lord had commanded him.

### Exodus 19:8
All the people answered together, "We will do everything the Lord has spoken." Moses brought the people's words back to the Lord.

### Exodus 19:9
The Lord said to Moses, "I will come to you in a thick cloud, so the people may hear when I speak with you and always believe you." Moses told the Lord what the people had said.

### Exodus 19:10
The Lord said to Moses, "Go to the people and make them ready today and tomorrow. Have them wash their clothes."

### Exodus 19:11
"They must be ready for the third day, because on the third day the Lord will come down on Mount Sinai in the sight of all the people."

### Exodus 19:12
"Set a boundary for the people all around the mountain. Tell them, 'Be careful not to go up the mountain or touch its edge. Anyone who touches the mountain must surely die.'"

### Exodus 19:13
"No hand may touch that person; the person must be stoned or shot through. Whether animal or person, it must not live. When the trumpet sounds long, they may come near the mountain."

### Exodus 19:14
Moses went down from the mountain to the people. He made the people ready, and they washed their clothes.

### Exodus 19:15
He said to the people, "Be ready for the third day. Do not come near your wives."

### Exodus 19:16
On the third day, in the morning, there was thunder and lightning. A thick cloud covered the mountain, and a very loud trumpet sound made everyone in the camp tremble.

### Exodus 19:17
Moses brought the people out of the camp to meet with God, and they stood at the bottom of the mountain.

### Exodus 19:18
Mount Sinai was covered with smoke, because the Lord came down on it in fire. Smoke rose like smoke from a furnace, and the whole mountain shook greatly.

### Exodus 19:19
The trumpet sound grew louder and louder. Moses spoke, and God answered him with a voice.

### Exodus 19:20
The Lord came down on Mount Sinai, on the top of the mountain. The Lord called Moses to the top, and Moses went up.

### Exodus 19:21
The Lord said to Moses, "Go down and warn the people, so they do not break through to look at the Lord and many of them fall."

### Exodus 19:22
"Even the priests who come near the Lord must make themselves holy, or the Lord may break out against them."

### Exodus 19:23
Moses said to the Lord, "The people cannot come up Mount Sinai, because you warned us, 'Set a boundary around the mountain and make it holy.'"

### Exodus 19:24
The Lord said to him, "Go down. Then come up again, you and Aaron with you. But do not let the priests or the people break through to come up to the Lord, or he may break out against them."

### Exodus 19:25
So Moses went down to the people and spoke to them.
