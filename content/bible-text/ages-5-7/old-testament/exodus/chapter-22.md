# Exodus 22

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
22

## Verses

### Exodus 22:1
"If a man steals an ox or a sheep and kills it or sells it, he must give back five oxen for one ox and four sheep for one sheep."

### Exodus 22:2
"If a thief is found breaking in at night and is struck so that he dies, the one who struck him is not guilty of bloodshed."

### Exodus 22:3
"But if it happens after sunrise, the one who struck him is guilty of bloodshed. A thief must pay back what he stole. If he has nothing, he must be sold because of his theft."

### Exodus 22:4
"If the stolen animal is found alive with him, whether ox, donkey, or sheep, he must pay back double."

### Exodus 22:5
"If a man lets his animal graze in a field or vineyard and it eats in another person's field, he must pay back from the best of his own field and vineyard."

### Exodus 22:6
"If fire spreads into thorns and burns stacks of grain, standing grain, or a field, the one who started the fire must surely pay for what was lost."

### Exodus 22:7
"If a man gives his neighbor money or goods to keep safe, and they are stolen from the neighbor's house, the thief, if found, must pay double."

### Exodus 22:8
"If the thief is not found, the owner of the house must come before the judges, so they can learn whether he took his neighbor's goods."

### Exodus 22:9
"For any argument about an ox, donkey, sheep, clothing, or anything lost that someone says is his, both people must bring the matter before the judges. The one the judges find guilty must pay double to his neighbor."

### Exodus 22:10
"If a man gives his neighbor a donkey, ox, sheep, or any animal to keep, and it dies, is hurt, or is driven away when no one sees,"

### Exodus 22:11
"then the two men must make an oath before the Lord. The neighbor must swear he did not take the animal, and the owner must accept it. The neighbor does not have to pay."

### Exodus 22:12
"But if the animal was stolen from him, he must pay its owner."

### Exodus 22:13
"If it was torn by wild animals, he must bring it as proof. He does not have to pay for what was torn."

### Exodus 22:14
"If a man borrows something from his neighbor, and it is hurt or dies while the owner is not with it, he must surely pay."

### Exodus 22:15
"If the owner is with it, the borrower does not have to pay. If it was hired, the money paid for hiring it covers the loss."

### Exodus 22:16
"If a man persuades a young woman who is not promised in marriage and lies with her, he must give the bride-price and marry her."

### Exodus 22:17
"If her father completely refuses to give her to him, the man must still pay the money given as a bride-price for young women."

### Exodus 22:18
"You must not allow a sorceress to live."

### Exodus 22:19
"Anyone who lies with an animal must surely be put to death."

### Exodus 22:20
"Whoever sacrifices to any god except the Lord alone must be destroyed."

### Exodus 22:21
"You must not mistreat or crush a foreigner, because you were foreigners in the land of Egypt."

### Exodus 22:22
"You must not hurt any widow or fatherless child."

### Exodus 22:23
"If you hurt them in any way, and they cry out to me, I will surely hear their cry."

### Exodus 22:24
"My anger will burn, and I will let the sword come against you. Then your wives will be widows, and your children will be fatherless."

### Exodus 22:25
"If you lend money to one of my poor people near you, do not act like a moneylender. Do not charge interest."

### Exodus 22:26
"If you take your neighbor's coat as a promise to pay, give it back before the sun goes down."

### Exodus 22:27
"That coat may be his only covering. What else will he sleep in? When he cries out to me, I will hear, because I am gracious."

### Exodus 22:28
"You must not speak against God or curse a ruler of your people."

### Exodus 22:29
"Do not hold back the first of your ripe crops and the first of your pressed juices. You must give me the firstborn of your sons."

### Exodus 22:30
"Do the same with your oxen and sheep. Let the firstborn stay with its mother seven days, and on the eighth day give it to me."

### Exodus 22:31
"You must be holy people to me. Do not eat meat from an animal torn by wild beasts in the field. Throw it to the dogs."
