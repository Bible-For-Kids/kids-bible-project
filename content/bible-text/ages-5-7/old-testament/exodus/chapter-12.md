# Exodus 12

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
12

## Verses

### Exodus 12:1
The Lord spoke to Moses and Aaron in the land of Egypt.

### Exodus 12:2
He said, "This month will be the beginning of months for you. It will be the first month of your year.

### Exodus 12:3
Tell all the people of Israel that on the tenth day of this month, each family is to choose a lamb for their home.

### Exodus 12:4
If a family is too small for a whole lamb, they must share with the nearest neighbor. They should count how many people will eat and choose enough for everyone.

### Exodus 12:5
The lamb must be a healthy one-year-old male. It may come from the sheep or from the goats.

### Exodus 12:6
Keep it until the fourteenth day of the month. Then all the people of Israel will kill their lambs in the evening.

### Exodus 12:7
They must take some of the blood and put it on the two side posts and the top of the doors of the houses where they eat the lamb.

### Exodus 12:8
That night they must eat the meat roasted over fire, with flat bread made without yeast and with bitter herbs.

### Exodus 12:9
Do not eat it raw or boiled in water. Roast it over fire, with its head, legs, and inner parts.

### Exodus 12:10
Do not leave any of it until morning. Whatever is left in the morning must be burned in the fire.

### Exodus 12:11
Eat it dressed and ready to travel, with your sandals on your feet and your staff in your hand. Eat it quickly. It is the Lord's Passover.

### Exodus 12:12
That night I will pass through Egypt and strike every firstborn in the land, both people and animals. I will bring judgment on all the gods of Egypt. I am the Lord.

### Exodus 12:13
The blood will be a sign on the houses where you are. When I see the blood, I will pass over you. This plague will not destroy you when I strike Egypt.

### Exodus 12:14
This day will help you remember what I have done. You must keep it as a feast to the Lord, year after year, through all your families.

### Exodus 12:15
For seven days you must eat bread made without yeast. On the first day, remove yeast from your houses. Anyone who eats bread with yeast during those seven days will be cut off from Israel.

### Exodus 12:16
On the first day you will gather for worship, and on the seventh day you will gather again. Do no work on those days, except preparing the food each person needs.

### Exodus 12:17
Keep the Feast of Bread Made Without Yeast, because on this very day I brought your people out of Egypt. Keep this day through all your families as a lasting rule.

### Exodus 12:18
In the first month, from the evening of the fourteenth day until the evening of the twenty-first day, you must eat bread made without yeast.

### Exodus 12:19
For seven days, no yeast may be found in your houses. Anyone who eats what is made with yeast will be cut off from Israel, whether that person is a foreigner or born in the land.

### Exodus 12:20
Do not eat anything made with yeast. Wherever you live, eat bread made without yeast."

### Exodus 12:21
Then Moses called all the elders of Israel and said, "Go and choose lambs for your families, and kill the Passover lamb.

### Exodus 12:22
Take a bunch of leafy hyssop branch, dip it in the blood in the bowl, and touch the blood to the top and sides of the doorframe. None of you must go out through the door of your house until morning.

### Exodus 12:23
The Lord will pass through to strike Egypt. When he sees the blood on the top and sides of the doorframe, the Lord will pass over that door and will not let the destroyer enter your houses.

### Exodus 12:24
You and your children must keep this command as a lasting rule.

### Exodus 12:25
When you come into the land the Lord promised to give you, you must keep this service.

### Exodus 12:26
When your children ask, 'What does this service mean?'

### Exodus 12:27
you will say, 'It is the Passover sacrifice to the Lord. In Egypt, he passed over the houses of the Israelites when he struck Egypt, and he saved our houses.'" Then the people bowed their heads and worshiped.

### Exodus 12:28
The Israelites went and did exactly what the Lord had commanded Moses and Aaron.

### Exodus 12:29
At midnight, the Lord struck every firstborn in Egypt, from Pharaoh's firstborn who sat near the throne to the firstborn of the prisoner in the dark prison, and the firstborn of the animals.

### Exodus 12:30
Pharaoh got up in the night, along with his servants and all the Egyptians. There was a great cry in Egypt, because there was death in every house.

### Exodus 12:31
During the night, Pharaoh called for Moses and Aaron. He said, "Get up and leave my people, both you and the Israelites. Go serve the Lord, just as you said.

### Exodus 12:32
Take your flocks and herds, as you asked, and go. And bless me also."

### Exodus 12:33
The Egyptians urged the people to leave the land quickly. They said, "We will all die if they stay."

### Exodus 12:34
So the people took their dough before yeast had been added. They wrapped their kneading bowls in their clothes and carried them on their shoulders.

### Exodus 12:35
The Israelites did what Moses had told them. They asked the Egyptians for silver, gold, and clothing.

### Exodus 12:36
The Lord made the Egyptians look kindly on the people, and they gave them what they asked for. In this way the Israelites took wealth from Egypt.

### Exodus 12:37
The Israelites traveled from Rameses to Succoth. There were about six hundred thousand men on foot, besides women and children.

### Exodus 12:38
Many other people went with them too, along with large flocks and herds.

### Exodus 12:39
They baked flat cakes from the dough they had brought out of Egypt. It had no yeast because they had been hurried out and had no time to prepare other food.

### Exodus 12:40
The Israelites had lived in Egypt for four hundred thirty years.

### Exodus 12:41
At the end of those four hundred thirty years, on that very day, all the Lord's people left Egypt.

### Exodus 12:42
That was a night to be remembered for the Lord, because he brought them out of Egypt. All the Israelites were to remember that night through all their families.

### Exodus 12:43
The Lord said to Moses and Aaron, "These are the rules for the Passover: No foreigner may eat it.

### Exodus 12:44
But a servant bought with money may eat it after he has been circumcised.

### Exodus 12:45
A visitor or a hired worker may not eat it.

### Exodus 12:46
It must be eaten inside one house. Do not carry any of the meat outside the house, and do not break any of its bones.

### Exodus 12:47
All the people of Israel must keep it.

### Exodus 12:48
If a foreigner living with you wants to keep the Passover to the Lord, all the males in his household must be circumcised. Then he may come near and keep it, and he will be like someone born in the land. No uncircumcised person may eat it.

### Exodus 12:49
The same law is for the person born in the land and for the foreigner living among you."

### Exodus 12:50
All the Israelites did just as the Lord commanded Moses and Aaron.

### Exodus 12:51
On that very day, the Lord brought the Israelites out of Egypt by their groups.
