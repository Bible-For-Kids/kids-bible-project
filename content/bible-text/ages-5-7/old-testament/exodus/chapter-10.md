# Exodus 10

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
10

## Verses

### Exodus 10:1
The Lord said to Moses, "Go in to Pharaoh, for I have hardened his heart and the hearts of his servants, so I may show these signs before him."

### Exodus 10:2
"I have done this so you may tell your son and your son's son what I did in Egypt, and the signs I showed among them, so you may know that I am the Lord."

### Exodus 10:3
Moses and Aaron came in to Pharaoh and said, "This is what the Lord God of the Hebrews says: How long will you refuse to humble yourself before me? Let my people go, so they may serve me."

### Exodus 10:4
"If you refuse to let my people go, tomorrow I will bring locusts into your land."

### Exodus 10:5
"They will cover the face of the ground so no one can see it. They will eat what is left after the hail and every tree growing for you in the field."

### Exodus 10:6
"They will fill your houses, your servants' houses, and the houses of all the Egyptians. Your fathers and grandfathers have never seen anything like this from their days on earth until now." Then Moses turned and left Pharaoh.

### Exodus 10:7
Pharaoh's servants said to him, "How long will this man be a trap to us? Let the men go, so they may serve the Lord their God. Do you not yet know that Egypt is ruined?"

### Exodus 10:8
Moses and Aaron were brought back to Pharaoh. He said to them, "Go, serve the Lord your God. But who will go?"

### Exodus 10:9
Moses said, "We will go with our young and our old, with our sons and daughters, and with our flocks and herds, because we must hold a feast to the Lord."

### Exodus 10:10
Pharaoh said to them, "Let the Lord be with you if I let you and your little ones go. Watch out, because trouble is ahead of you."

### Exodus 10:11
"No. Let only the men go and serve the Lord, since that is what you asked for." Then Moses and Aaron were driven out from Pharaoh.

### Exodus 10:12
The Lord said to Moses, "Stretch out your hand over the land of Egypt for the locusts, so they may come up on Egypt and eat every plant of the land, all that the hail has left."

### Exodus 10:13
Moses stretched his staff over the land of Egypt, and the Lord brought an east wind over the land all that day and all that night. In the morning, the east wind brought the locusts.

### Exodus 10:14
The locusts came up over all the land of Egypt and settled in every part of Egypt. They were very heavy; there had never been locusts like them before, and there would never be such locusts again.

### Exodus 10:15
They covered the whole land until it looked dark. They ate every plant and all the fruit on the trees that the hail had left. Nothing green remained on the trees or in the field through all the land of Egypt.

### Exodus 10:16
Then Pharaoh quickly called for Moses and Aaron and said, "I have sinned against the Lord your God and against you."

### Exodus 10:17
"Now please forgive my sin only this once, and pray to the Lord your God to take this death away from me."

### Exodus 10:18
Moses left Pharaoh and prayed to the Lord.

### Exodus 10:19
The Lord turned a very strong west wind, which lifted the locusts away and threw them into the Red Sea. Not one locust remained in all the borders of Egypt.

### Exodus 10:20
But the Lord hardened Pharaoh's heart, so he would not let the children of Israel go.

### Exodus 10:21
The Lord said to Moses, "Stretch out your hand toward the sky, so darkness may come over the land of Egypt, a darkness that can be felt."

### Exodus 10:22
Moses stretched his hand toward the sky, and thick darkness covered all the land of Egypt for three days.

### Exodus 10:23
The people could not see one another, and no one rose from his place for three days. But all the children of Israel had light in their homes.

### Exodus 10:24
Pharaoh called to Moses and said, "Go, serve the Lord. Only let your flocks and herds stay behind. Your little ones may also go with you."

### Exodus 10:25
Moses said, "You must also give us sacrifices and burned offerings, so we may sacrifice to the Lord our God."

### Exodus 10:26
"Our farm animals must go with us too. Not one hoof will be left behind, because we must take from them to serve the Lord our God. We will not know what we must use to serve the Lord until we get there."

### Exodus 10:27
But the Lord hardened Pharaoh's heart, and he would not let them go.

### Exodus 10:28
Pharaoh said to Moses, "Get away from me. Be careful never to see my face again, because on the day you see my face, you will die."

### Exodus 10:29
Moses said, "You have spoken rightly. I will not see your face again."
