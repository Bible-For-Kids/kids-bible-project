# Exodus 14

## Book
Exodus

## Chapter
14

## Verses

### Exodus 14:1
The Lord spoke to Moses.

### Exodus 14:2
He said, "Tell the Israelites to turn back and camp by the sea, in front of Pi-hahiroth, between Migdol and the sea, across from Baal-zephon.

### Exodus 14:3
Pharaoh will say, 'The Israelites are trapped in the land. The wilderness has closed them in.'

### Exodus 14:4
I will harden Pharaoh's heart, and he will chase after them. Then I will be honored over Pharaoh and all his army, and the Egyptians will know that I am the Lord." So the Israelites did this.

### Exodus 14:5
When the king of Egypt was told that the people had fled, Pharaoh and his servants changed their minds about them. They said, "What have we done? We let Israel go, and now they will not serve us."

### Exodus 14:6
So Pharaoh got his chariot ready and took his people with him.

### Exodus 14:7
He took six hundred of his best chariots, along with all the other chariots of Egypt, with officers over them.

### Exodus 14:8
The Lord hardened the heart of Pharaoh king of Egypt, and Pharaoh chased the Israelites. But the Israelites had gone out boldly.

### Exodus 14:9
The Egyptians chased them with all Pharaoh's horses, chariots, riders, and army. They caught up with the Israelites while they were camped by the sea, near Pi-hahiroth, across from Baal-zephon.

### Exodus 14:10
When Pharaoh came near, the Israelites looked up and saw the Egyptians marching after them. They were very afraid, and they cried out to the Lord.

### Exodus 14:11
They said to Moses, "Were there no graves in Egypt? Did you bring us into the wilderness to die? Why did you bring us out of Egypt?

### Exodus 14:12
Didn't we tell you in Egypt, 'Leave us alone so we can serve the Egyptians'? It would have been better for us to serve the Egyptians than to die in the wilderness."

### Exodus 14:13
Moses said to the people, "Do not be afraid. Stand still and see the Lord save you today. The Egyptians you see today, you will never see again.

### Exodus 14:14
The Lord will fight for you. You only need to be quiet."

### Exodus 14:15
The Lord said to Moses, "Why are you crying out to me? Tell the Israelites to move forward.

### Exodus 14:16
Lift up your staff and stretch your hand over the sea. Divide it, so the Israelites can walk through the middle of the sea on dry ground.

### Exodus 14:17
I will harden the hearts of the Egyptians, and they will follow them. I will be honored over Pharaoh, all his army, his chariots, and his riders.

### Exodus 14:18
The Egyptians will know that I am the Lord when I am honored over Pharaoh, his chariots, and his riders."

### Exodus 14:19
Then the angel of God, who had been leading Israel from the front, moved behind them. The pillar of cloud moved too and stood behind them.

### Exodus 14:20
The cloud stood between Egypt's camp and Israel's camp. On one side it made darkness, and on the other side it gave light, so the two camps could not reach each other all night.

### Exodus 14:21
Then Moses stretched his hand over the sea. All night the Lord drove the water back with a strong east wind. The sea split open, and the ground became dry.

### Exodus 14:22
The Israelites went into the middle of the sea on dry ground. The water stood like a wall on their right and on their left.

### Exodus 14:23
The Egyptians followed them into the middle of the sea with all Pharaoh's horses, chariots, and riders.

### Exodus 14:24
Near morning, the Lord looked down from the pillar of fire and cloud at the Egyptian army and threw them into confusion.

### Exodus 14:25
He made their chariot wheels come off, so the chariots were hard to drive. The Egyptians said, "Let us run away from Israel, because the Lord is fighting for them against Egypt."

### Exodus 14:26
Then the Lord said to Moses, "Stretch your hand over the sea so the waters will come back over the Egyptians, their chariots, and their riders."

### Exodus 14:27
Moses stretched his hand over the sea. At morning, the sea returned to its place. The Egyptians tried to flee, but the Lord swept them into the sea.

### Exodus 14:28
The waters returned and covered the chariots, the riders, and all Pharaoh's army that had followed Israel into the sea. Not one of them remained.

### Exodus 14:29
But the Israelites walked on dry ground through the middle of the sea, with water like a wall on their right and on their left.

### Exodus 14:30
That day the Lord saved Israel from the power of the Egyptians. Israel saw the Egyptians dead on the seashore.

### Exodus 14:31
Israel saw the great power the Lord used against Egypt. The people feared the Lord, and they trusted the Lord and his servant Moses.
