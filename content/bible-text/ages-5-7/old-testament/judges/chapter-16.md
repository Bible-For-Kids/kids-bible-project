# Judges 16

## Book
Judges

## Chapter
16

## Verses

### Judges 16:1
Samson went to Gaza and saw a woman whose life was sinful. He went in to her.

### Judges 16:2
The people of Gaza were told, "Samson is here!" They surrounded the place and waited quietly all night at the city gate, saying, "Wait until morning light. Then we will kill him."

### Judges 16:3
Samson lay there until midnight. At midnight he got up, grabbed the doors of the city gate with the two posts, pulled them up with the bar still attached, put them on his shoulders, and carried them to the top of the hill facing Hebron.

### Judges 16:4
After this, Samson loved a woman in the Valley of Sorek. Her name was Delilah.

### Judges 16:5
The Philistine rulers came to Delilah and said, "Persuade him to show you where his great strength comes from and how we can overpower him and tie him up. Each of us will give you eleven hundred pieces of silver."

### Judges 16:6
Delilah said to Samson, "Please tell me where your great strength comes from, and how someone could tie you up and overpower you."

### Judges 16:7
Samson said to her, "If they tie me with seven fresh bowstrings that have never been dried, then I will become weak like any other man."

### Judges 16:8
Then the Philistine rulers brought up to her seven green cords that had not been dried, and she bound him with them.

### Judges 16:9
Men were hiding in the inner room, waiting. Delilah said to him, "The Philistines are on you, Samson!" But he snapped the bowstrings as easily as flax thread snaps when it touches fire. So no one knew the secret of his strength.

### Judges 16:10
Delilah said to Samson, "Look, you have mocked me, and told me lies. Now please tell me how you might be bound."

### Judges 16:11
He said to her, "If they tie me tightly with new ropes that have never been used, then I will become weak like any other man."

### Judges 16:12
So Delilah took new ropes and tied him with them. Then she said, "The Philistines are on you, Samson!" Men were hiding in the inner room, but Samson snapped the ropes off his arms like thread.

### Judges 16:13
Delilah said to Samson, "Until now, you have mocked me and told me lies. Tell me with what you might be bound." He said to her, "If you weave the seven locks of my head with the fabric on the loom."

### Judges 16:14
Delilah fastened his hair with the pin and said, "The Philistines are on you, Samson!" He woke from sleep and pulled away the pin, the loom, and the fabric.

### Judges 16:15
She said to him, "How can you say, 'I love you,' when your heart is not with me? You have mocked me these three times, and have not told me where your great strength lies."

### Judges 16:16
Day after day she pressed him with her words and urged him, until his soul was tired to death.

### Judges 16:17
Finally Samson told Delilah everything in his heart. He said, "No razor has ever touched my head, because I have been set apart to God from my mother's womb. If my hair is shaved, my strength will leave me, and I will become weak like any other man."

### Judges 16:18
When Delilah saw that Samson had told her everything in his heart, she called for the Philistine rulers and said, "Come up this one more time, because he has told me everything in his heart." So the Philistine rulers came to her and brought the silver in their hands.

### Judges 16:19
Delilah let Samson fall asleep on her knees. Then she called for a man to shave off the seven locks of his hair. She began to bring him down, and his strength left him.

### Judges 16:20
She said, "The Philistines are on you, Samson!" He woke up and thought, "I will go out as before and shake myself free." But he did not know that the Lord had left him.

### Judges 16:21
The Philistines seized Samson, blinded him, and took him down to Gaza. They bound him with bronze chains, and he ground grain in the prison.

### Judges 16:22
However, the hair of his head began to grow again after he was shaved.

### Judges 16:23
The Philistine rulers gathered to offer a great sacrifice to Dagon their god and to celebrate. They said, "Our god has given Samson our enemy into our hand."

### Judges 16:24
When the people saw Samson, they praised their god and said, "Our god has given our enemy into our hand, the one who destroyed our land and killed many of us."

### Judges 16:25
When they were celebrating, they said, "Call for Samson, so he may perform for us." They brought Samson out of the prison, and he performed for them. Then they made him stand between the pillars.

### Judges 16:26
Samson said to the boy who held him by the hand, "Let me feel the pillars that hold up the house, so I can lean on them."

### Judges 16:27
The house was full of men and women. All the Philistine rulers were there, and about three thousand men and women were on the roof watching Samson perform.

### Judges 16:28
Samson called to the Lord and said, "Lord God, please remember me. Please strengthen me only this once, God, so I may be avenged on the Philistines for my two eyes."

### Judges 16:29
Samson took hold of the two middle pillars that held up the house. He leaned against them, one with his right hand and the other with his left.

### Judges 16:30
Samson said, "Let me die with the Philistines." Then he pushed with all his strength, and the house fell on the rulers and all the people inside. So the dead he killed at his death were more than those he killed during his life.

### Judges 16:31
Then his brothers and all the house of his father came down and took him, and brought him up and buried him between Zorah and Eshtaol in the burial site of Manoah his father. He judged Israel twenty years.
