# Genesis 24

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
24

## Verses

### Genesis 24:1
Abraham was now very old, and the Lord had blessed Abraham in every part of his life.

### Genesis 24:2
Abraham spoke to the oldest servant in his house, the servant who cared for all he had. He said, "Please put your hand under my thigh.

### Genesis 24:3
I want you to make a promise by the Lord, the God of heaven and the God of earth. Do not take a wife for my son from the daughters of the Canaanites, among whom I live.

### Genesis 24:4
Instead, go to my country and to my relatives, and take a wife there for my son Isaac."

### Genesis 24:5
The servant asked him, "What if the woman does not want to follow me back to this land? Should I take your son back to the land you came from?"

### Genesis 24:6
Abraham said to him, "Be careful. Do not take my son back there.

### Genesis 24:7
The Lord God of heaven took me from my father's house and from the land of my family. He spoke to me and promised me, 'I will give this land to your offspring.' He will send his angel ahead of you, and you will take a wife for my son from there.

### Genesis 24:8
If the woman will not follow you, then you will be free from this promise to me. Only do not take my son back there."

### Genesis 24:9
So the servant put his hand under the thigh of Abraham his master and made the promise about this matter.

### Genesis 24:10
Then the servant took ten of his master's camels and left, carrying many good things from his master. He traveled to Mesopotamia, to the city of Nahor.

### Genesis 24:11
Near evening, he made the camels kneel outside the city by a well of water. It was the time when women came out to draw water.

### Genesis 24:12
The servant prayed, "O Lord God of my master Abraham, please give me success today. Please show kindness to my master Abraham.

### Genesis 24:13
Here I am standing by this well of water, and the daughters of the men of the city are coming out to draw water.

### Genesis 24:14
May the young woman I speak to be the one you have chosen for Isaac. I will say, 'Please lower your jar so I may drink.' If she says, 'Drink, and I will give your camels water too,' then I will know you have shown kindness to my master."

### Genesis 24:15
Before he had even finished praying, Rebekah came out with her water jar on her shoulder. She was the daughter of Bethuel, who was the son of Milkah and Nahor, Abraham's brother.

### Genesis 24:16
The young woman was very beautiful. She was a virgin and had never been with a man as a wife. She went down to the well, filled her jar, and came back up.

### Genesis 24:17
The servant ran to meet her and said, "Please let me drink a little water from your jar."

### Genesis 24:18
She said, "Drink, my lord." Quickly she lowered the jar to her hand and gave him a drink.

### Genesis 24:19
When she finished giving him a drink, she said, "I will draw water for your camels too, until they have finished drinking."

### Genesis 24:20
She quickly emptied her jar into the trough and ran back to the well for more water. Again and again she drew water for all his camels.

### Genesis 24:21
The man watched her quietly, wondering whether the Lord had made his journey successful.

### Genesis 24:22
When the camels had finished drinking, the man took out a gold nose ring and two gold bracelets for her arms.

### Genesis 24:23
He asked, "Whose daughter are you? Please tell me. Is there room in your father's house for us to stay tonight?"

### Genesis 24:24
She said to him, "I am the daughter of Bethuel, the son Milkah bore to Nahor."

### Genesis 24:25
She also said, "We have plenty of straw and feed, and there is room for you to stay."

### Genesis 24:26
Then the man bowed his head and worshiped the Lord.

### Genesis 24:27
He said, "Blessed be the Lord God of my master Abraham. The Lord has not stopped showing mercy and truth to my master. The Lord has led me straight to the house of my master's relatives."

### Genesis 24:28
The young woman ran home and told her mother's household all these things.

### Genesis 24:29
Rebekah had a brother named Laban, and Laban ran out to the man at the well.

### Genesis 24:30
Laban had seen the nose ring and the bracelets on his sister's arms. He had heard Rebekah tell what the man said. So he came to the man, who was still standing by the camels at the well.

### Genesis 24:31
Laban said, "Come in, you who are blessed by the Lord. Why are you standing outside? I have made the house ready and prepared a place for the camels."

### Genesis 24:32
The servant came into the house. Laban unloaded the camels and gave them straw and feed. He also brought water so the servant and the men with him could wash their feet.

### Genesis 24:33
Food was set before the servant, but he said, "I will not eat until I have told why I came." Laban said, "Speak."

### Genesis 24:34
The servant said, "I am Abraham's servant.

### Genesis 24:35
The Lord has blessed my master very much, and he has become great. The Lord has given him flocks, herds, silver, gold, male servants, female servants, camels, and donkeys.

### Genesis 24:36
Sarah, my master's wife, had a son for my master when she was old. My master has given that son everything he has.

### Genesis 24:37
My master made me promise, saying, 'Do not take a wife for my son from the daughters of the Canaanites, in whose land I live.

### Genesis 24:38
Go instead to my father's house and to my family, and take a wife for my son.'

### Genesis 24:39
I said to my master, 'What if the woman will not follow me?'

### Genesis 24:40
He said to me, 'The Lord, before whom I walk, will send his angel with you and make your journey successful. You will take a wife for my son from my family and my father's house.

### Genesis 24:41
You will be free from my promise when you come to my relatives. If they will not give her to you, you will be free from my promise.'

### Genesis 24:42
Today I came to the well and prayed, 'O Lord God of my master Abraham, please make the journey I am taking successful.

### Genesis 24:43
Here I am standing by the well of water. When a young woman comes out to draw water, I will say to her, "Please give me a little water from your jar to drink."

### Genesis 24:44
If she says, "Drink, and I will also draw water for your camels," let her be the woman the Lord has chosen for my master's son.'

### Genesis 24:45
Before I had finished speaking in my heart, Rebekah came out with her jar on her shoulder. She went down to the well and drew water, and I said to her, 'Please let me drink.'

### Genesis 24:46
She quickly lowered her jar from her shoulder and said, 'Drink, and I will give your camels water too.' So I drank, and she gave the camels water also.

### Genesis 24:47
Then I asked her, 'Whose daughter are you?' She said, 'The daughter of Bethuel, Nahor's son, whom Milkah bore to him.' So I put the ring on her nose and the bracelets on her arms.

### Genesis 24:48
I bowed my head and worshiped the Lord. I blessed the Lord God of my master Abraham, because he had led me on the right road to find the daughter of my master's relative for his son.

### Genesis 24:49
Now tell me if you will deal kindly and faithfully with my master. If you will not, tell me, so I may know which way to turn."

### Genesis 24:50
Then Laban and Bethuel answered, "This matter comes from the Lord. We cannot say anything bad or good against it.

### Genesis 24:51
Look, Rebekah is here before you. Take her and go, and let her become the wife of your master's son, as the Lord has spoken."

### Genesis 24:52
When Abraham's servant heard their words, he bowed down to the ground before the Lord.

### Genesis 24:53
The servant brought out silver jewelry, gold jewelry, and clothing and gave them to Rebekah. He also gave precious gifts to her brother and her mother.

### Genesis 24:54
Then the servant and the men with him ate and drank and stayed all night. When they got up in the morning, the servant said, "Send me back to my master."

### Genesis 24:55
Rebekah's brother and mother said, "Let the young woman stay with us a few days, at least ten. After that she may go."

### Genesis 24:56
But the servant said, "Do not hold me back, since the Lord has made my journey successful. Send me away so I may go to my master."

### Genesis 24:57
They said, "We will call the young woman and ask her ourselves."

### Genesis 24:58
They called Rebekah and asked her, "Will you go with this man?" She said, "I will go."

### Genesis 24:59
So they sent away Rebekah their sister, her nurse, Abraham's servant, and his men.

### Genesis 24:60
They blessed Rebekah and said, "Our sister, may you become the mother of thousands upon thousands, and may your offspring possess the gate of those who hate them."

### Genesis 24:61
Then Rebekah and her young women got up, climbed onto the camels, and followed the servant. The servant took Rebekah and went on his way.

### Genesis 24:62
Isaac had come from the way of Beer Lahai Roi, because he lived in the Negev.

### Genesis 24:63
In the evening, Isaac went out into the field to meditate. He lifted his eyes and saw camels coming.

### Genesis 24:64
Rebekah lifted her eyes too. When she saw Isaac, she got down from the camel.

### Genesis 24:65
She had asked the servant, "Who is that man walking through the field to meet us?" The servant had said, "It is my master." So Rebekah took her veil and covered herself.

### Genesis 24:66
The servant told Isaac everything he had done.

### Genesis 24:67
Isaac brought Rebekah into the tent of Sarah his mother. Rebekah became his wife, and Isaac loved her. So Isaac was comforted after his mother's death.
