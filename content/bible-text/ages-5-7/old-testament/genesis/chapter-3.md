# Genesis 3

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
3

## Verses

### Genesis 3:1
The snake was craftier than any wild animal the Lord God had made. In the garden, he came to the woman and asked, "Did God really say, 'You must not eat from any tree in the garden'?"

### Genesis 3:2
The woman said to the snake, "We may eat fruit from the trees in the garden."

### Genesis 3:3
Then she said, "But God said, 'You must not eat fruit from the tree in the middle of the garden. You must not touch it, or you will die.'"

### Genesis 3:4
The snake said to the woman, "You will not surely die."

### Genesis 3:5
The snake said, "God knows that when you eat it, your eyes will be opened. You will be like God, knowing good and evil."

### Genesis 3:6
The woman looked at the tree. The fruit looked good for food. It was lovely to her eyes, and she wanted it to make her wise. So she took some fruit and ate it. She also gave some to her husband, who was with her, and he ate it too.

### Genesis 3:7
Then their eyes were opened. They knew they had no clothes on. So they sewed fig leaves together and made coverings for themselves.

### Genesis 3:8
When the day grew cool, they heard the Lord God walking in the garden. The man and his wife slipped among the garden trees and hid from him.

### Genesis 3:9
But the Lord God called to the man, "Where are you?"

### Genesis 3:10
The man said, "I heard you in the garden. I was afraid because I had no clothes on, so I hid."

### Genesis 3:11
God said, "Who told you that you had no clothes on? Did you eat from the tree I commanded you not to eat from?"

### Genesis 3:12
The man said, "The woman you gave to be with me gave me fruit from the tree, and I ate it."

### Genesis 3:13
Then the Lord God said to the woman, "What have you done?" The woman said, "The snake tricked me, and I ate."

### Genesis 3:14
So the Lord God said to the snake, "Because you have done this, you are cursed more than all farm animals and wild animals. You will crawl on your belly, and you will eat dust all your life."

### Genesis 3:15
God said, "I will make you and the woman enemies. Your offspring and her offspring will be enemies too. Someone from her family will crush your head, and you will strike his heel."

### Genesis 3:16
To the woman, God said, "Having children will now bring great pain. Trouble will also come between you and your husband. Your desire will be for him, and he will rule over you."

### Genesis 3:17
To Adam, God said, "You listened to your wife and ate from the tree I commanded you not to eat from. Because of you, the ground is cursed. All your life you will work hard and painfully to eat from it."

### Genesis 3:18
God said, "The ground will grow thorns and thistles for you. You will eat plants from the field."

### Genesis 3:19
God said, "By the sweat on your face, you will eat food until you return to the ground. You came from dust, and to dust you will return."

### Genesis 3:20
Adam named his wife Eve, because she would become the mother of all living people.

### Genesis 3:21
The Lord God made clothes from animal skins for Adam and his wife, and he dressed them.

### Genesis 3:22
Then the Lord God said, "The man has become like one of us, knowing good and evil. He must not reach out his hand, take from the tree of life, eat, and live forever."

### Genesis 3:23
So the Lord God sent the man out of the garden of Eden. The man would work the ground he had been taken from.

### Genesis 3:24
God drove the man out. East of the garden of Eden, he placed cherubim, mighty heavenly guards. A flaming sword flashed back and forth, guarding the way to the tree of life.
