# Genesis 21

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
21

## Verses

### Genesis 21:1
The Lord cared for Sarah just as he had said. The Lord did for Sarah what he had promised.

### Genesis 21:2
Sarah became pregnant and gave Abraham a son in his old age, at the very time God had promised.

### Genesis 21:3
Abraham named the son Sarah bore to him Isaac.

### Genesis 21:4
When Isaac was eight days old, Abraham gave him the covenant sign, just as God had commanded.

### Genesis 21:5
Abraham was 100 years old when his son Isaac was born.

### Genesis 21:6
Sarah said, "God has brought me laughter. Everyone who hears about this will laugh with me."

### Genesis 21:7
She also said, "Who would have told Abraham that Sarah would nurse children? Yet I have given him a son in his old age."

### Genesis 21:8
Isaac grew, and the day came for him to stop nursing. Abraham held a great feast that day.

### Genesis 21:9
Sarah saw the son Hagar the Egyptian had borne to Abraham laughing.

### Genesis 21:10
So Sarah said to Abraham, "Send away this servant woman and her son. That woman's son must not share the family inheritance with my son Isaac."

### Genesis 21:11
This troubled Abraham very much because Ishmael was his son.

### Genesis 21:12
But God said to Abraham, "Do not be troubled about the boy and your servant woman. Listen to Sarah, because the promised family line will be counted through Isaac.

### Genesis 21:13
I will also make the servant woman's son into a nation, because he is your child too."

### Genesis 21:14
Early the next morning, Abraham gave Hagar bread and a water-skin bag. He placed them on her shoulder and sent her away with the boy. She wandered in the wilderness of Beersheba.

### Genesis 21:15
When the water in the bag was gone, Hagar put the boy under one of the bushes.

### Genesis 21:16
Then she went and sat down a little way off, about as far as an arrow can fly. She said, "I cannot watch the boy die." She sat there and began to cry.

### Genesis 21:17
God heard the boy crying. The angel of God called to Hagar from heaven and said, "What is wrong, Hagar? Do not be afraid. God has heard the boy where he is.

### Genesis 21:18
Get up, lift the boy, and take him by the hand. I will make him into a great nation."

### Genesis 21:19
Then God opened Hagar's eyes, and she saw a well of water. She went, filled the skin with water, and gave the boy a drink.

### Genesis 21:20
God was with the boy as he grew up. He lived in the wilderness and became a man who used a bow and arrows.

### Genesis 21:21
Ishmael lived in the wilderness of Paran, and his mother got a wife for him from Egypt.

### Genesis 21:22
At that time, Abimelek and Phicol, the commander of his army, said to Abraham, "God is with you in everything you do.

### Genesis 21:23
Now promise me here before God that you will not lie or deal falsely with me, my children, or the family after me. Show me and the land where you live the same kindness I have shown you."

### Genesis 21:24
Abraham said, "I swear it."

### Genesis 21:25
Then Abraham complained to Abimelek about a well of water that Abimelek's servants had taken.

### Genesis 21:26
Abimelek said, "I do not know who did this. You did not tell me, and I only heard about it today."

### Genesis 21:27
So Abraham brought sheep and cattle and gave them to Abimelek, and the two men made a covenant, a serious promise agreement.

### Genesis 21:28
Abraham set seven female lambs from the flock by themselves.

### Genesis 21:29
Abimelek asked Abraham, "Why have you set these seven lambs apart?"

### Genesis 21:30
Abraham answered, "Accept these seven lambs from my hand. They will be a witness, a sign that I dug this well."

### Genesis 21:31
That is why the place was called Beersheba, because the two men made a serious promise there.

### Genesis 21:32
After they made the covenant at Beersheba, Abimelek and Phicol returned to the land of the Philistines.

### Genesis 21:33
Abraham planted a tamarisk tree in Beersheba. There he called on the name of the Lord, the God who lives forever.

### Genesis 21:34
Abraham stayed in the land of the Philistines for a long time.
