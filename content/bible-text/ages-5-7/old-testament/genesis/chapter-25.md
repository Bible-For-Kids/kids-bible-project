# Genesis 25

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
25

## Verses

### Genesis 25:1
Abraham took another wife, and her name was Keturah.

### Genesis 25:2
Keturah had sons for Abraham: Zimran, Jokshan, Medan, Midian, Ishbak, and Shuah.

### Genesis 25:3
Jokshan became the father of Sheba and Dedan. Dedan's sons were Asshurim, Letushim, and Leummim.

### Genesis 25:4
Midian's sons were Ephah, Epher, Hanoch, Abida, and Eldaah. All these were Keturah's children.

### Genesis 25:5
Abraham gave everything he had to Isaac.

### Genesis 25:6
Abraham also gave gifts to the sons of his other wives. While he was still alive, he sent them away from Isaac his son, toward the east country.

### Genesis 25:7
Abraham lived 175 years.

### Genesis 25:8
Then Abraham breathed his last and died at a good old age. He was an old man who had lived many years, and he was gathered to his people.

### Genesis 25:9
His sons Isaac and Ishmael buried him in the cave of Machpelah, in the field of Ephron son of Zohar the Hittite, near Mamre.

### Genesis 25:10
This was the field Abraham had bought from the sons of Heth. There Abraham was buried, and Sarah his wife was buried there too.

### Genesis 25:11
After Abraham died, God blessed Isaac his son. Isaac lived near Beer Lahai Roi.

### Genesis 25:12
This is the family line of Ishmael, Abraham's son. Hagar the Egyptian, Sarah's servant, had given birth to him for Abraham.

### Genesis 25:13
These are the names of Ishmael's sons, named by their family lines: Nebaioth, Ishmael's firstborn, then Kedar, Adbeel, Mibsam,

### Genesis 25:14
Mishma, Dumah, Massa,

### Genesis 25:15
Hadar, Tema, Jetur, Naphish, and Kedemah.

### Genesis 25:16
These were Ishmael's sons. Their names were remembered by their towns and camps. They were twelve leaders over their nations.

### Genesis 25:17
Ishmael lived 137 years. Then he breathed his last and died, and he was gathered to his people.

### Genesis 25:18
The family that came after him lived from Havilah to Shur, which is near Egypt as you go toward Assyria. Ishmael died near all his brothers.

### Genesis 25:19
This is the family line of Isaac, Abraham's son. Abraham became the father of Isaac.

### Genesis 25:20
Isaac was 40 years old when he married Rebekah. She was the daughter of Bethuel the Aramean from Paddan Aram and the sister of Laban the Aramean.

### Genesis 25:21
Isaac prayed to the Lord for his wife, because she could not have children. The Lord answered him, and Rebekah his wife became pregnant.

### Genesis 25:22
The children struggled together inside her. Rebekah said, "If this is happening, why is it happening to me?" So she went to ask the Lord.

### Genesis 25:23
The Lord said to her, "Two nations are in your womb, and two peoples will come from inside you. One people will be stronger than the other, and the older will serve the younger."

### Genesis 25:24
When the time came for Rebekah to give birth, there were twins inside her.

### Genesis 25:25
The first baby came out red, with hair all over him like a little coat, so they named him Esau.

### Genesis 25:26
After that, his brother came out holding Esau's heel, so he was named Jacob. Isaac was 60 years old when Rebekah had them.

### Genesis 25:27
The boys grew up. Esau became a skillful hunter, a man who loved the fields, and Jacob was a quiet man who lived in tents.

### Genesis 25:28
Isaac loved Esau because he ate the meat Esau hunted, but Rebekah loved Jacob.

### Genesis 25:29
One day Jacob was cooking stew. Esau came in from the field, tired and worn out.

### Genesis 25:30
Esau said to Jacob, "Please give me some of that red stew, because I am tired." That is why he was called Edom.

### Genesis 25:31
Jacob said, "Sell me your birthright today, the special family place that belongs to the firstborn son."

### Genesis 25:32
Esau said, "Look, I am about to die. What good will this birthright do for me?"

### Genesis 25:33
Jacob said, "Promise it to me today." So Esau made the promise, and he sold his birthright to Jacob.

### Genesis 25:34
Then Jacob gave Esau bread and lentil stew. Esau ate, drank, got up, and went away. In this way Esau treated his birthright as if it did not matter.
