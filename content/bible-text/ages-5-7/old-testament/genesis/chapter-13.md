# Genesis 13

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
13

## Verses

### Genesis 13:1
Abram went up from Egypt to the Negev with his wife and everything he had. Lot went with him.

### Genesis 13:2
Abram had become very rich. He had many flocks and herds, and he had silver and gold.

### Genesis 13:3
From the Negev, Abram traveled from place to place until he came back to Bethel, to the place where his tent had been before, between Bethel and Ai.

### Genesis 13:4
That was the place where Abram had built an altar earlier. There Abram called on the name of the Lord.

### Genesis 13:5
Lot, who traveled with Abram, also had flocks, herds, and tents.

### Genesis 13:6
The land could not support both families while they lived close together, because they had so many animals and belongings.

### Genesis 13:7
The men who cared for Abram's animals and the men who cared for Lot's animals began to argue. At that time, the Canaanites and Perizzites also lived in the land.

### Genesis 13:8
So Abram said to Lot, "Let there be no arguing between you and me, or between your animal-keepers and mine. We are family.

### Genesis 13:9
Is not the whole land before you? Let us separate. If you go left, I will go right. If you go right, I will go left."

### Genesis 13:10
Lot lifted his eyes and saw the whole plain of the Jordan. It had plenty of water everywhere, like the garden of the Lord and like the land of Egypt. This was before the Lord destroyed Sodom and Gomorrah.

### Genesis 13:11
So Lot chose the whole plain of the Jordan for himself. Lot set out toward the east, and the two men separated.

### Genesis 13:12
Abram lived in the land of Canaan, but Lot lived near the cities of the plain and moved his tents as far as Sodom.

### Genesis 13:13
Now the people of Sodom were very wicked and sinned greatly against the Lord.

### Genesis 13:14
After Lot separated from Abram, the Lord said to Abram, "Lift your eyes from where you are. Look north, south, east, and west.

### Genesis 13:15
All the land you see I will give to you and to the children and families who come after you forever.

### Genesis 13:16
I will make the children and families who come after you like the dust of the earth. If anyone could count the dust, then your family could be counted.

### Genesis 13:17
Go, walk through the land, from one end to the other, because I am giving it to you."

### Genesis 13:18
So Abram moved his tents and lived near the great trees of Mamre at Hebron. There he built an altar to the Lord.
