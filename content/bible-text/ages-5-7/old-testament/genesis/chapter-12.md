# Genesis 12

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
12

## Verses

### Genesis 12:1
The Lord spoke to Abram. He said, "Leave your country, your relatives, and your father's house. Go to the land I will show you.

### Genesis 12:2
I will make you into a great nation. I will bless you and make your name great, and you will be a blessing.

### Genesis 12:3
I will bless those who bless you. Whoever treats you as nothing, I will curse. All families on earth will be blessed through you."

### Genesis 12:4
So Abram went, just as the Lord had told him. Lot went with him. Abram was 75 years old when he left Harran.

### Genesis 12:5
Abram took his wife Sarai, his nephew Lot, and everything they owned. He also took the people who had joined his household in Harran. They set out for the land of Canaan, and they arrived there.

### Genesis 12:6
Abram traveled through the land to the great tree of Moreh at Shechem. At that time, the Canaanites lived in the land.

### Genesis 12:7
The Lord appeared to Abram and said, "I will give this land to the children and families who come after you." So Abram built an altar there to the Lord, who had appeared to him.

### Genesis 12:8
From there Abram moved to the hills east of Bethel. He set up his tent, with Bethel on the west and Ai on the east. There he built an altar to the Lord and called on the Lord's name.

### Genesis 12:9
Then Abram kept traveling, moving toward the Negev.

### Genesis 12:10
Then a famine came to the land, and there was very little food. Abram went down to Egypt to stay there for a while, because the famine was very heavy in the land.

### Genesis 12:11
As Abram was about to enter Egypt, he said to his wife Sarai, "I know you are a beautiful woman.

### Genesis 12:12
When the Egyptians see you, they will say, 'This is his wife.' Then they will kill me but let you live.

### Genesis 12:13
Please say you are my sister, so things will go well for me because of you, and my life will be spared."

### Genesis 12:14
When Abram entered Egypt, the Egyptians saw that Sarai was very beautiful.

### Genesis 12:15
Pharaoh's officials saw her and praised her to Pharaoh. So Sarai was taken into Pharaoh's house.

### Genesis 12:16
Pharaoh treated Abram well because of Sarai. Abram received sheep, cattle, donkeys, male and female servants, female donkeys, and camels.

### Genesis 12:17
But the Lord sent serious diseases on Pharaoh and his house because of Abram's wife Sarai.

### Genesis 12:18
So Pharaoh called Abram and said, "What have you done to me? Why did you not tell me she was your wife?

### Genesis 12:19
Why did you say, 'She is my sister,' so that I took her as my wife? Here is your wife. Take her and go!"

### Genesis 12:20
Then Pharaoh gave orders about Abram. His men sent Abram away with his wife and everything he had.
