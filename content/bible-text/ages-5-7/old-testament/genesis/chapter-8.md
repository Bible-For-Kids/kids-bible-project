# Genesis 8

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
8

## Verses

### Genesis 8:1
God remembered Noah and all the animals with him in the ark. God had not forgotten them. He sent a wind over the earth, and the waters began to go down.

### Genesis 8:2
The springs of the deep and the windows of the heavens were closed. The rain from the sky stopped.

### Genesis 8:3
The waters kept going down from the earth. At the end of 150 days, the waters had gone down.

### Genesis 8:4
On the seventeenth day of the seventh month, the ark came to rest on the mountains of Ararat.

### Genesis 8:5
The waters kept going down until the tenth month. On the first day of the tenth month, the tops of the mountains could be seen.

### Genesis 8:6
After 40 days, Noah opened the window he had made in the ark.

### Genesis 8:7
He sent out a raven. It kept flying back and forth until the waters dried up from the earth.

### Genesis 8:8
Then Noah sent out a dove to see if the waters had gone down from the ground.

### Genesis 8:9
But the dove found no place to rest its foot, because water still covered the whole earth. So it returned to Noah in the ark. Noah reached out his hand, took the dove, and brought it back inside.

### Genesis 8:10
Noah waited seven more days. Then he sent the dove out from the ark again.

### Genesis 8:11
When the dove came back to him in the evening, Noah saw a fresh olive leaf in its beak. Then Noah knew the waters had gone down from the earth.

### Genesis 8:12
Noah waited seven more days and sent the dove out again. This time, it did not return to him.

### Genesis 8:13
In Noah's 601st year, on the first day of the first month, the water had dried from the earth. Noah removed the covering from the ark and looked out. He saw that the ground was dry.

### Genesis 8:14
By the twenty-seventh day of the second month, the earth was dry.

### Genesis 8:15
Then God said to Noah,

### Genesis 8:16
"Come out of the ark. Bring your wife, your sons, and your sons' wives with you.

### Genesis 8:17
Bring out every living creature with you: birds, animals, and all creatures that move along the ground. Let them spread over the earth, have many young, and grow in number."

### Genesis 8:18
So Noah came out with his sons, his wife, and his sons' wives.

### Genesis 8:19
All the animals, crawling creatures, and birds came out of the ark by their kinds.

### Genesis 8:20
Then Noah built an altar to the Lord. He took some of every clean animal and clean bird and offered them as burned offerings on the altar.

### Genesis 8:21
The Lord smelled the pleasing aroma and said in his heart, "I will never again curse the ground because of humans, even though the human heart plans evil from childhood. I will never again destroy every living creature as I have done.

### Genesis 8:22
As long as the earth remains, seedtime and harvest, cold and heat, summer and winter, day and night will not stop."
