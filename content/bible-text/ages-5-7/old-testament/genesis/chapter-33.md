# Genesis 33

## Book
Genesis

## Chapter
33

## Verses

### Genesis 33:1
Jacob looked up and saw Esau coming with four hundred men. So he divided the children among Leah, Rachel, and the two servant-women.

### Genesis 33:2
He put the servant-women and their children first, Leah and her children next, and Rachel and Joseph last.

### Genesis 33:3
Jacob went ahead of them. As he came near his brother, he bowed down to the ground seven times.

### Genesis 33:4
But Esau ran to meet Jacob. He hugged him, put his arms around his neck, kissed him, and they both wept.

### Genesis 33:5
Esau looked up and saw the women and children. He asked, "Who are these with you?" Jacob answered, "These are the children God has kindly given your servant."

### Genesis 33:6
Then the servant-women came near with their children, and they bowed down.

### Genesis 33:7
Leah and her children also came near and bowed down. After them, Joseph and Rachel came near and bowed down.

### Genesis 33:8
Esau asked, "What did you mean by all those groups of animals I met?" Jacob answered, "They were to find kindness in my lord's eyes."

### Genesis 33:9
Esau said, "I have enough, my brother. Keep what you have."

### Genesis 33:10
Jacob said, "No, please. If you are pleased with me, accept my gift from my hand. Seeing your face is like seeing the face of God, because you have welcomed me.

### Genesis 33:11
"Please take the gift I brought you. God has been kind to me, and I have enough." Jacob urged him, and Esau accepted it.

### Genesis 33:12
Then Esau said, "Let us start our journey. I will go ahead of you."

### Genesis 33:13
Jacob said to him, "My lord knows that the children are young, and the flocks and herds have young ones with them. If they are driven too hard for one day, all the flock may die.

### Genesis 33:14
"Please let my lord go ahead of his servant. I will move slowly, at the pace of the animals and the children, until I come to my lord in Seir."

### Genesis 33:15
Esau said, "Let me leave some of my people with you." Jacob said, "Why do that? Let me find kindness in my lord's eyes."

### Genesis 33:16
So Esau turned back that day on his way to Seir.

### Genesis 33:17
Jacob traveled to Succoth. There he built a house for himself and shelters for his animals. That is why the place was named Succoth.

### Genesis 33:18
After Jacob came from Padan-aram, he arrived safely at the city of Shechem in the land of Canaan, and he set up his tent near the city.

### Genesis 33:19
He bought the piece of field where he had set up his tent from the sons of Hamor, Shechem's father, for one hundred pieces of money.

### Genesis 33:20
There Jacob built an altar and called it El-elohe-Israel, which means "God, the God of Israel."
