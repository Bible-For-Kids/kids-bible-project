# Joshua 4

## Book
Joshua

## Chapter
4

## Verses

### Joshua 4:1
When the whole nation had finished crossing the Jordan River, the Lord spoke to Joshua.

### Joshua 4:2
The Lord said, "Choose twelve men from the people, one man from each tribe.

### Joshua 4:3
Tell them to take twelve stones from the middle of the Jordan River, from the place where the priests' feet stood firm. Carry the stones with you and set them down where you camp tonight."

### Joshua 4:4
So Joshua called the twelve men he had chosen from the people of Israel, one man from each tribe.

### Joshua 4:5
Joshua said to them, "Go into the middle of the Jordan River before the Lord's covenant chest. Each of you must lift one stone onto his shoulder, one stone for each tribe of Israel.

### Joshua 4:6
Joshua said, "These stones will be a sign among you. In days to come, when your children ask, 'What do these stones mean?'

### Joshua 4:7
you will tell them, 'The water of the Jordan River stopped before the Lord's covenant chest. When the chest crossed the Jordan River, the water stopped.' These stones will help Israel remember this forever."

### Joshua 4:8
The people of Israel did as Joshua commanded. They took twelve stones from the middle of the Jordan River, just as the Lord told Joshua, one for each tribe of Israel. They carried the stones to the place where they camped and set them down there.

### Joshua 4:9
Joshua also set up twelve stones in the middle of the Jordan River, at the place where the priests carrying the covenant chest had stood. The stones remained there for a long time.

### Joshua 4:10
The priests carrying the covenant chest stood in the middle of the Jordan River until everything the Lord commanded Joshua to tell the people was finished, just as Moses had commanded Joshua. The people hurried across.

### Joshua 4:11
When all the people had completely crossed over, the Lord's covenant chest crossed over with the priests in the presence of the people.

### Joshua 4:12
The people of Reuben, Gad, and the half-tribe of Manasseh crossed over armed in front of the people of Israel, just as Moses had told them.

### Joshua 4:13
About forty thousand armed men crossed before the Lord, ready for battle on the plains of Jericho.

### Joshua 4:14
That day the Lord made Joshua great in the sight of all Israel. The people honored Joshua as they had honored Moses all the days of his life.

### Joshua 4:15
Then the Lord spoke to Joshua again.

### Joshua 4:16
He said, "Command the priests carrying the covenant chest to come up out of the Jordan River."

### Joshua 4:17
Joshua therefore commanded the priests, saying, "Come up out of the Jordan River!"

### Joshua 4:18
When the priests carrying the Lord's covenant chest came up from the middle of the Jordan River and their feet stepped onto dry ground, the waters of the Jordan River returned to their place and overflowed its banks as before.

### Joshua 4:19
The people came up from the Jordan River on the tenth day of the first month. They camped at Gilgal, east of Jericho.

### Joshua 4:20
At Gilgal, Joshua set up the twelve stones they had taken from the Jordan River.

### Joshua 4:21
He said to the people of Israel, "In days to come, when your children ask their fathers, 'What do these stones mean?'

### Joshua 4:22
tell your children, 'Israel crossed this Jordan River on dry ground.'

### Joshua 4:23
The Lord your God dried up the water of the Jordan River before you until you crossed, just as he dried up the Red Sea before us until we crossed.

### Joshua 4:24
He did this so all the peoples of the earth would know that the hand of the Lord is mighty, and so you would fear the Lord your God forever.
