# Joshua Chapter 5

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on after crossing the Jordan, Israel receives the covenant sign of circumcision, keeps the Passover, and eats food from the land as the manna stops.

## Important Keywords
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- Egypt: The land where Israel had been enslaved
- holy: Set apart for God
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jericho: The first city Israel faced after crossing the Jordan

## Chapter Summary
After crossing the Jordan, Israel receives the covenant sign of circumcision, keeps the Passover, and eats food from the land as the manna stops. Joshua then meets the commander of the Lord's army and learns that the place is holy.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God renews covenant identity**: Israel is marked again as the people of the Lord before entering battle.
2. **The Lord is the true commander**: Joshua must take off his sandals because he stands on holy ground.
