# Joshua Chapter 24

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on joshua gathers Israel at Shechem and retells the story of God's grace from Abraham to Egypt, the wilderness, and the land.

## Important Keywords
- Aaron: Moses' brother and Israel's first high priest
- blessed: Spoke good over someone or something
- command: Something God tells someone to do
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- Egypt: The land where Israel had been enslaved

## Chapter Summary
Joshua gathers Israel at Shechem and retells the story of God's grace from Abraham to Egypt, the wilderness, and the land. He calls the people to serve the Lord, renews the covenant, sets up a witness stone, and the book closes with burials of Joshua, Joseph's bones, and Eleazar.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God's grace comes first**: Joshua tells what the Lord did before calling Israel to serve him.
2. **Choose whom you will serve**: Joshua calls Israel to put away false gods and serve the Lord faithfully.

If serving the Lord seems wrong to you, choose today whom you will serve: the gods your ancestors served beyond the River, or the gods of the Amorites in whose land you live. But as for me and my house, we will serve the Lord. - Joshua 24:15

### Ages 8-10

## Discussion Questions by Age

### Ages 5-7
1. What happened in this chapter?
2. What did God show about himself?

### Ages 8-10
1. What does this chapter teach about God, people, or covenant life?
2. Which verse would need the most careful explanation for a younger child?
