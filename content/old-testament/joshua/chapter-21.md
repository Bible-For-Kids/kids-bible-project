# Joshua Chapter 21

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on the Levites receive towns and pasturelands among the tribes of Israel.

## Important Keywords
- Aaron: Moses' brother and Israel's first high priest
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Joshua: The servant God appointed to lead Israel after Moses
- Levites: The tribe set apart for service connected with worship
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles

**Original Reference**: Joshua 21:43 - So Yahweh gave to Israel all the land which he swore to give to their fathers. They possessed it, and lived in it.

#### Ages 5-7
So the Lord gave to Israel all the land that he swore to give to their ancestors. They took it, and lived in it.

#### Ages 8-10
So the Lord gave to Israel all the land that he swore to give to their ancestors. They took it, and lived in it.

**Translation Notes**:
Preserve the Lord's promise, Israel's obedience or disobedience, and the real places in the conquest and land-allotment story. Keep judgment language accurate but child-readable.

**Key Vocabulary**:
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles
- Yahweh: The Lord, God's covenant name in the Hebrew Scriptures

## Chapter Summary
The Levites receive towns and pasturelands among the tribes of Israel. The chapter ends by declaring that the Lord gave Israel all the land he swore to give, gave them rest, and kept every good promise.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God provides for worship servants**: The Levites receive towns throughout Israel.
2. **God keeps every good word**: Not one of the Lord's good promises fails.
