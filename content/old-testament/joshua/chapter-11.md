# Joshua Chapter 11

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on northern kings gather against Israel with many soldiers, horses, and chariots, but the Lord tells Joshua not to fear.

## Important Keywords
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Joshua: The servant God appointed to lead Israel after Moses
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles
- Moses: The servant God used to lead Israel out of Egypt
- Yahweh: The Lord, God's covenant name in the Hebrew Scriptures

## Chapter Summary
Northern kings gather against Israel with many soldiers, horses, and chariots, but the Lord tells Joshua not to fear. Joshua obeys, the Lord gives victory, and the land has rest from war.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Numbers do not frighten God**: A huge army cannot stop the Lord's promise.
2. **Obedience follows God's command**: Joshua does what the Lord commanded Moses.
