# Joshua Chapter 3

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on israel follows the Lord's covenant chest toward the Jordan River, and the priests step into the floodwater.

## Important Keywords
- command: Something God tells someone to do
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jericho: The first city Israel faced after crossing the Jordan

## Chapter Summary
Israel follows the Lord's covenant chest toward the Jordan River, and the priests step into the floodwater. The Lord stops the river, the priests stand on dry ground, and all Israel crosses over safely.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God goes before his people**: The covenant chest leads Israel toward the river.
2. **God opens the way**: The flooded Jordan stops because the Lord acts for his people.
