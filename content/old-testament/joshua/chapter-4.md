# Joshua Chapter 4

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on the Lord tells Joshua to choose twelve stones from the Jordan as a sign for future children.

## Important Keywords
- command: Something God tells someone to do
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jericho: The first city Israel faced after crossing the Jordan

## Chapter Summary
The Lord tells Joshua to choose twelve stones from the Jordan as a sign for future children. The stones remind Israel that the Lord dried up the Jordan just as he dried up the Red Sea, so all the earth may know his hand is mighty.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Children need the story of God's works**: The stones help parents answer when children ask what happened.
2. **God wants his mighty acts remembered**: The crossing becomes a witness to Israel and the nations.
