# Joshua Chapter 20

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on the Lord commands Israel to appoint cities of refuge.

## Important Keywords
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- judgment: God's right decision about sin
- Jericho: The first city Israel faced after crossing the Jordan
- Jordan: The river Israel crossed when the Lord brought them into the promised land
- Joshua: The servant God appointed to lead Israel after Moses

## Chapter Summary
The Lord commands Israel to appoint cities of refuge. These cities protect someone who killed another person without meaning to until there can be a fair hearing before the community.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God cares about justice**: The cities protect life while guilt or innocence is judged.
2. **God provides refuge**: The Lord makes a safe place for someone in danger before a fair hearing.
