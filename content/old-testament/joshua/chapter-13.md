# Joshua Chapter 13

## Book Overview
Joshua tells how the Lord brought Israel into the promised land, kept his promises, judged wicked cities, gave Israel their land, and called his people to serve him faithfully. This chapter focuses on when Joshua is old, the Lord tells him there is still land to be possessed, but also commands him to divide the land as an inheritance.

## Important Keywords
- Egypt: The land where Israel had been enslaved
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jericho: The first city Israel faced after crossing the Jordan
- Jordan: The river Israel crossed when the Lord brought them into the promised land

## Chapter Summary
When Joshua is old, the Lord tells him there is still land to be possessed, but also commands him to divide the land as an inheritance. The chapter describes land east of the Jordan and reminds Israel that Levi's inheritance is the Lord.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God's work continues across generations**: Joshua is old, but the Lord's promise still moves forward.
2. **The Lord is the Levites' inheritance**: Levi receives holy service and the Lord himself as their portion.
