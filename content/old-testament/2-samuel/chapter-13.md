# 2 Samuel Chapter 13

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 13, Amnon sins terribly against Tamar, and David's house fills with grief and anger. Absalom later kills Amnon and flees, leaving David mourning and the family torn apart.

## Important Keywords
- blessed: Spoke good over someone or something
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Baal: A false god worshiped by many Canaanites
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Absalom: David's son who rebelled against him

## Chapter Summary
Amnon sins terribly against Tamar, and David's house fills with grief and anger. Absalom later kills Amnon and flees, leaving David mourning and the family torn apart.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God names evil as evil**: Amnon's sin against Tamar is not softened or excused.
2. **Sin tears families apart**: David's household suffers deep sorrow and revenge.
