# 2 Samuel Chapter 12

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 12, Nathan confronts David with a parable, and David confesses that he has sinned against the Lord. The Lord forgives David, but painful consequences follow, and later Solomon is born.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Bathsheba: The woman David sinned against and later married

## Chapter Summary
Nathan confronts David with a parable, and David confesses that he has sinned against the Lord. The Lord forgives David, but painful consequences follow, and later Solomon is born.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God exposes sin to bring truth**: Nathan's parable helps David see his guilt.
2. **Forgiveness is real, and sin is still serious**: David is forgiven, yet sorrow follows his sin.
