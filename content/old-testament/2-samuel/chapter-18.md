# 2 Samuel Chapter 18

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 18, David's servants defeat Absalom's army, and Absalom dies after being caught in a tree. David grieves bitterly for Absalom, even though Absalom rebelled against him.

## Important Keywords
- blessed: Spoke good over someone or something
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Absalom: David's son who rebelled against him

## Chapter Summary
David's servants defeat Absalom's army, and Absalom dies after being caught in a tree. David grieves bitterly for Absalom, even though Absalom rebelled against him.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Rebellion brings bitter sorrow**: Absalom's rebellion ends in death and grief.
2. **A father's grief can be deep**: David mourns his son with heartbreaking words.
