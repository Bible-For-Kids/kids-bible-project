# 2 Samuel Chapter 4

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 4, Two men murder Ishbosheth and bring his head to David, expecting reward. David judges them because they killed an innocent man in his own house.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Jonathan: Saul's son and David's faithful friend
- Saul: Israel's first king, who later disobeyed the Lord

## Chapter Summary
Two men murder Ishbosheth and bring his head to David, expecting reward. David judges them because they killed an innocent man in his own house.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Wrong actions are not holy help**: The murderers think they are helping David, but David calls their act evil.
2. **Righteous rule protects the innocent**: David judges the killing of Ishbosheth instead of rewarding it.
