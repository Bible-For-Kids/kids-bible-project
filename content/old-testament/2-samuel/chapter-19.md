# 2 Samuel Chapter 19

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 19, Joab rebukes David for grieving in a way that discourages the people who saved him. David returns toward Jerusalem, showing mercy to Shimei and Mephibosheth while Israel and Judah argue.

## Important Keywords
- blessed: Spoke good over someone or something
- cursed: Placed under judgment instead of blessing
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob

## Chapter Summary
Joab rebukes David for grieving in a way that discourages the people who saved him. David returns toward Jerusalem, showing mercy to Shimei and Mephibosheth while Israel and Judah argue.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Leaders must care for their people**: David must rise and speak kindly to his servants.
2. **Mercy can mark a restored king**: David spares Shimei and listens to Mephibosheth.
