# 2 Samuel Chapter 1

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 1, David hears that Saul and Jonathan have died and grieves deeply instead of celebrating. He judges the Amalekite messenger and teaches Judah a lament for Saul and Jonathan.

## Important Keywords
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Jonathan: Saul's son and David's faithful friend

## Chapter Summary
David hears that Saul and Jonathan have died and grieves deeply instead of celebrating. He judges the Amalekite messenger and teaches Judah a lament for Saul and Jonathan.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Godly grief honors what is true**: David mourns Saul and Jonathan even though Saul had been his enemy.
2. **Life and death are serious before God**: David refuses to treat the death of the Lord's anointed king lightly.
