# 2 Samuel Chapter 3

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 3, David grows stronger while Saul's house grows weaker. Abner turns toward David, but Joab murders Abner, and David publicly grieves to show he is not guilty of Abner's blood.

## Important Keywords
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul

## Chapter Summary
David grows stronger while Saul's house grows weaker. Abner turns toward David, but Joab murders Abner, and David publicly grieves to show he is not guilty of Abner's blood.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God can establish his promise through messy times**: David's house grows stronger while the kingdom is divided.
2. **Revenge brings guilt**: Joab kills Abner, but David refuses to approve the murder.
