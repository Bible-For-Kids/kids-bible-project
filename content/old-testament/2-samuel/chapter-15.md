# 2 Samuel Chapter 15

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 15, Absalom steals the hearts of Israel and begins a rebellion. David flees Jerusalem, trusts the Lord with the ark, and sends friends back to help him with wisdom.

## Important Keywords
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- exile: Being sent away from home
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob

## Chapter Summary
Absalom steals the hearts of Israel and begins a rebellion. David flees Jerusalem, trusts the Lord with the ark, and sends friends back to help him with wisdom.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Pride can steal hearts**: Absalom wins people by flattery and false promises.
2. **Faith trusts God in exile**: David leaves Jerusalem and puts himself in the Lord's hands.
