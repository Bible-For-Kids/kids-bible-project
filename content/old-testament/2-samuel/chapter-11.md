# 2 Samuel Chapter 11

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 11, David stays in Jerusalem, takes Bathsheba, and arranges Uriah's death to hide his sin. David marries Bathsheba, but the thing David has done is evil in the Lord's sight.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Bathsheba: The woman David sinned against and later married
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles

## Chapter Summary
David stays in Jerusalem, takes Bathsheba, and arranges Uriah's death to hide his sin. David marries Bathsheba, but the thing David has done is evil in the Lord's sight.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Sin grows when it is hidden**: David's first sin leads to lies and murder.
2. **The Lord sees what kings do**: David can hide from people for a time, but not from the Lord.
