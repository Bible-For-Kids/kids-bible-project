# 2 Samuel Chapter 2

## Book Overview
2 Samuel tells how the Lord established David as king, made covenant promises to his house, exposed David's sin and its sorrow, and showed both judgment and mercy. In 2 Samuel 2, David asks the Lord where to go, and Judah anoints him king in Hebron. Abner makes Ishbosheth king over Israel, and conflict grows between David's servants and Saul's house.

## Important Keywords
- blessed: Spoke good over someone or something
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jordan: The river Israel crossed when the Lord brought them into the promised land
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul

## Chapter Summary
David asks the Lord where to go, and Judah anoints him king in Hebron. Abner makes Ishbosheth king over Israel, and conflict grows between David's servants and Saul's house.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Ask the Lord before moving forward**: David seeks the Lord before going to Hebron.
2. **Divided hearts bring conflict**: Judah follows David while other tribes follow Saul's house.
