# Ruth Chapter 4

## Book Overview
Ruth tells how the Lord cared for Naomi and Ruth through loyal love, ordinary work, Boaz's kindness, and the family line that would lead to King David. In Ruth 4, Boaz meets the closer redeemer at the city gate, receives the right to redeem Naomi's land and marry Ruth, and takes Ruth as his wife. The Lord gives them a son named Obed, who becomes the grandfather of King David.

## Important Keywords
- blessed: Spoke good over someone or something
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Boaz: The man from Bethlehem who showed covenant kindness to Ruth and Naomi
- Naomi: Ruth's mother-in-law, who returned to Bethlehem after great loss
- redeemer: A close relative who could protect family land and raise up a family line

## Chapter Summary
Boaz meets the closer redeemer at the city gate, receives the right to redeem Naomi's land and marry Ruth, and takes Ruth as his wife. The Lord gives them a son named Obed, who becomes the grandfather of King David.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **The Lord restores the empty**: Naomi began in grief, but the Lord gives her joy through Obed.
2. **God works through family lines**: Ruth and Boaz become part of the family line leading to David.
