# 1 Kings Chapter 13

## Book Overview
1 Kings tells how Solomon received wisdom and built the temple, how his heart turned away, how the kingdom divided, and how the Lord spoke through prophets like Elijah. In 1 Kings 13, A man of God speaks the Lord's word against Jeroboam's altar, but later disobeys the Lord after an old prophet lies to him. The Lord's word proves true.

## Important Keywords
- altar: A special place where offerings were given to the Lord
- command: Something God tells someone to do
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul

**Translation Notes**:
Preserve the Lord's covenant promises, Solomon's wisdom and failure, the divided kingdom, true worship, idolatry, and the prophet's word. Keep judgment and adult details truthful, restrained, and child-readable.

**Key Vocabulary**:
- Jeroboam: The first king of the northern kingdom of Israel after the kingdom divided
- sin: Disobeying God and going against his good way

---

## Chapter Summary
A man of God speaks the Lord's word against Jeroboam's altar, but later disobeys the Lord after an old prophet lies to him. The Lord's word proves true.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God's word must be obeyed**: The man of God must follow what the Lord told him.
2. **Lies can lead people into danger**: The old prophet's lie does not remove the need to obey God.
