# 1 Kings Chapter 15

## Book Overview
1 Kings tells how Solomon received wisdom and built the temple, how his heart turned away, how the kingdom divided, and how the Lord spoke through prophets like Elijah. In 1 Kings 15, The chapter compares kings of Judah and Israel. Abijam is not wholly devoted to the Lord, but Asa does what is right, removes idol worship, and trusts the Lord imperfectly amid conflict.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Jeroboam: The first king of the northern kingdom of Israel after the kingdom divided

## Chapter Summary
The chapter compares kings of Judah and Israel. Abijam is not wholly devoted to the Lord, but Asa does what is right, removes idol worship, and trusts the Lord imperfectly amid conflict.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **The Lord sees the heart of a king**: The chapter measures kings by faithfulness to the Lord.
2. **Doing right may require removing wrong**: Asa removes sinful worship practices from Judah.

## Memory Verses by Age

### Ages 5-7
Asa did what was right in the Lord's eyes, as David his father had done. - 1 Kings 15:11

### Ages 8-10
Asa did what was right in the Lord's eyes, as David his father had done. - 1 Kings 15:11

## Discussion Questions by Age

### Ages 5-7
1. What happened in this chapter?
2. What did God show about himself?

### Ages 8-10
1. What does this chapter teach about God, people, or covenant life?
2. Which verse would need the most careful explanation for a younger child?
