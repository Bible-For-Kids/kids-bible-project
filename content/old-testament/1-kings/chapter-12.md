# 1 Kings Chapter 12

## Book Overview
1 Kings tells how Solomon received wisdom and built the temple, how his heart turned away, how the kingdom divided, and how the Lord spoke through prophets like Elijah. In 1 Kings 12, Rehoboam rejects wise counsel and answers Israel harshly, so the kingdom divides. Jeroboam becomes king over Israel and makes golden calves, leading the people into sin.

## Important Keywords
- altar: A special place where offerings were given to the Lord
- Egypt: The land where Israel had been enslaved
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul

## Chapter Summary
Rehoboam rejects wise counsel and answers Israel harshly, so the kingdom divides. Jeroboam becomes king over Israel and makes golden calves, leading the people into sin.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Harsh pride can divide people**: Rehoboam refuses wise counsel and loses much of the kingdom.
2. **False worship is sin**: Jeroboam's calves turn the people away from the Lord's appointed worship.
