# 1 Kings Chapter 19

## Book Overview
1 Kings tells how Solomon received wisdom and built the temple, how his heart turned away, how the kingdom divided, and how the Lord spoke through prophets like Elijah. In 1 Kings 19, Elijah runs into the wilderness afraid and weary, and the Lord feeds him and meets him at Horeb. The Lord speaks in a gentle whisper and sends Elijah back with new work and a promise of a faithful remnant.

## Important Keywords
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Baal: A false god worshiped by many Canaanites
- Ahab: A wicked king of Israel who worshiped Baal

## Chapter Summary
Elijah runs into the wilderness afraid and weary, and the Lord feeds him and meets him at Horeb. The Lord speaks in a gentle whisper and sends Elijah back with new work and a promise of a faithful remnant.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **The Lord cares for tired servants**: God gives Elijah food, rest, and his word.
2. **God is not absent when we feel alone**: The Lord has kept seven thousand who have not bowed to Baal.
