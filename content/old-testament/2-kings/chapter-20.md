# 2 Kings Chapter 20

## Book Overview
2 Kings continues the story of Israel and Judah, showing Elisha's ministry, the fall of Israel, Hezekiah and Josiah's reforms, and Jerusalem's exile after the people would not listen to the Lord. In 2 Kings 20, Hezekiah becomes very sick, prays, and receives healing with a sign from the Lord. Later he shows his treasures to Babylonian messengers, and Isaiah announces that those treasures will one day be carried away.

## Important Keywords
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Hezekiah: A king of Judah who trusted the Lord when Assyria threatened Jerusalem
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles
- Yahweh: The Lord, God's covenant name in the Hebrew Scriptures

## Chapter Summary
Hezekiah becomes very sick, prays, and receives healing with a sign from the Lord. Later he shows his treasures to Babylonian messengers, and Isaiah announces that those treasures will one day be carried away.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God hears prayer and sees tears**: The Lord answers Hezekiah when he is near death.
2. **Pride can endanger the future**: Hezekiah shows his treasures, and Babylon will later carry them away.
