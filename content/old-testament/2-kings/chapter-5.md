# 2 Kings Chapter 5

## Book Overview
2 Kings continues the story of Israel and Judah, showing Elisha's ministry, the fall of Israel, Hezekiah and Josiah's reforms, and Jerusalem's exile after the people would not listen to the Lord. In 2 Kings 5, Naaman, a Syrian commander with a serious skin disease, hears from a young Israelite girl and comes to Elisha. He humbles himself, washes in the Jordan, is healed, and confesses that the Lord is God.

## Important Keywords
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jordan: The river Israel crossed when the Lord brought them into the promised land
- Elisha: The prophet who followed Elijah and spoke the Lord's word
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles

## Chapter Summary
Naaman, a Syrian commander with a serious skin disease, hears from a young Israelite girl and comes to Elisha. He humbles himself, washes in the Jordan, is healed, and confesses that the Lord is God.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God can use a child's witness**: The young girl points Naaman toward the prophet in Israel.
2. **Humble obedience receives mercy**: Naaman is healed when he listens and washes as Elisha said.
