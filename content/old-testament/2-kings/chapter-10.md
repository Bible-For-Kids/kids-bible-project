# 2 Kings Chapter 10

## Book Overview
2 Kings continues the story of Israel and Judah, showing Elisha's ministry, the fall of Israel, Hezekiah and Josiah's reforms, and Jerusalem's exile after the people would not listen to the Lord. In 2 Kings 10, Jehu destroys Ahab's house and removes Baal worship from Israel. Yet Jehu does not walk with the Lord wholeheartedly and keeps the golden calf sins of Jeroboam.

## Important Keywords
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jordan: The river Israel crossed when the Lord brought them into the promised land
- Baal: A false god worshiped by many Canaanites
- Ahab: A wicked king of Israel who worshiped Baal

## Chapter Summary
Jehu destroys Ahab's house and removes Baal worship from Israel. Yet Jehu does not walk with the Lord wholeheartedly and keeps the golden calf sins of Jeroboam.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Partial obedience is not wholehearted faithfulness**: Jehu removes Baal but keeps other false worship.
2. **God's word does not fall to the ground**: The Lord fulfills what he said about Ahab's house.
