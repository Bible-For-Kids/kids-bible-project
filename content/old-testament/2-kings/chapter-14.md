# 2 Kings Chapter 14

## Book Overview
2 Kings continues the story of Israel and Judah, showing Elisha's ministry, the fall of Israel, Hezekiah and Josiah's reforms, and Jerusalem's exile after the people would not listen to the Lord. In 2 Kings 14, Amaziah rules Judah and follows some right ways, but not like David. Israel also continues under troubled kings, yet the Lord sees Israel's bitter suffering and saves them for a time.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Jeroboam: The first king of the northern kingdom of Israel after the kingdom divided

## Chapter Summary
Amaziah rules Judah and follows some right ways, but not like David. Israel also continues under troubled kings, yet the Lord sees Israel's bitter suffering and saves them for a time.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **The Lord measures kings by faithfulness**: Amaziah does some right things but is not fully like David.
2. **God sees bitter suffering**: The Lord helps Israel even though the nation has not been faithful.
