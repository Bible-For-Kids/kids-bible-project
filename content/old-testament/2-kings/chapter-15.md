# 2 Kings Chapter 15

## Book Overview
2 Kings continues the story of Israel and Judah, showing Elisha's ministry, the fall of Israel, Hezekiah and Josiah's reforms, and Jerusalem's exile after the people would not listen to the Lord. In 2 Kings 15, The chapter lists kings of Judah and Israel, showing some right actions in Judah and repeated violence and instability in Israel. Assyria begins carrying parts of Israel away.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Jeroboam: The first king of the northern kingdom of Israel after the kingdom divided
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles

## Chapter Summary
The chapter lists kings of Judah and Israel, showing some right actions in Judah and repeated violence and instability in Israel. Assyria begins carrying parts of Israel away.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Sin makes kingdoms unstable**: Israel's throne changes through violence again and again.
2. **Judgment can begin before the final fall**: Assyria starts taking Israelite people away before Samaria falls.
