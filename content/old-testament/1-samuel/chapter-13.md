# 1 Samuel Chapter 13

## Book Overview
1 Samuel tells how the Lord answered Hannah, called Samuel, judged corrupt leadership, gave Israel a king, rejected Saul for disobedience, and began raising up David. In 1 Samuel 13, Saul grows afraid while waiting for Samuel and offers the burnt offering himself. Samuel rebukes him, saying his kingdom will not continue because he did not keep the Lord's command.

## Important Keywords
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- Jordan: The river Israel crossed when the Lord brought them into the promised land
- Jonathan: Saul's son and David's faithful friend
- Samuel: The prophet and judge whom the Lord called as a boy

## Chapter Summary
Saul grows afraid while waiting for Samuel and offers the burnt offering himself. Samuel rebukes him, saying his kingdom will not continue because he did not keep the Lord's command.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Fear must not lead obedience**: Saul acts wrongly when pressure builds.
2. **God desires a faithful king**: Samuel says the Lord has sought a man after his own heart.
