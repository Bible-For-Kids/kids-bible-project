# 1 Samuel Chapter 8

## Book Overview
1 Samuel tells how the Lord answered Hannah, called Samuel, judged corrupt leadership, gave Israel a king, rejected Saul for disobedience, and began raising up David. In 1 Samuel 8, Israel asks Samuel for a king like the other nations. The Lord says they are rejecting him as king, and Samuel warns them what a human king will take from them.

## Important Keywords
- Egypt: The land where Israel had been enslaved
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- judge: A leader God raised up to rescue and guide Israel
- Samuel: The prophet and judge whom the Lord called as a boy
- Lord: God's covenant name used in many English Bibles

## Chapter Summary
Israel asks Samuel for a king like the other nations. The Lord says they are rejecting him as king, and Samuel warns them what a human king will take from them.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Wanting to copy the nations is dangerous**: Israel asks for a king like everyone else.
2. **The Lord is the true king**: God says the people have rejected him from ruling over them.
