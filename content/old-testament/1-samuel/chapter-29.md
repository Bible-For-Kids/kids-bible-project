# 1 Samuel Chapter 29

## Book Overview
1 Samuel tells how the Lord answered Hannah, called Samuel, judged corrupt leadership, gave Israel a king, rejected Saul for disobedience, and began raising up David. In 1 Samuel 29, David marches with Achish, but the Philistine rulers do not trust him and send him away from the battle. Achish says David has been upright with him, but David must return.

## Important Keywords
- evil: What is wrong and against God
- God: The one true Creator and Lord over all
- Israel: God's covenant people descended from Jacob
- David: The shepherd the Lord chose to become king after Saul
- Saul: Israel's first king, who later disobeyed the Lord

## Chapter Summary
David marches with Achish, but the Philistine rulers do not trust him and send him away from the battle. Achish says David has been upright with him, but David must return.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God can close dangerous doors**: David is sent away from a battle that would trap him between loyalties.
2. **Reputation matters**: Achish has seen David act faithfully toward him.
