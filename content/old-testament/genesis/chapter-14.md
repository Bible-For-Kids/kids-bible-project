# Genesis Chapter 14

## Book Overview
Genesis 14 continues the covenant family story. Warring kings capture Lot, Abram rescues him, Melchizedek blesses Abram, and Abram refuses to become rich through the king of Sodom.

## Important Keywords
- king: A ruler over a people or land
- rescue: Being saved from danger
- priest: A worship leader who serves before God
- blessing: Good spoken or given by God
- oath: A serious promise made before God
- ally: Someone joined with another person to help
- tenth: One part out of ten

## Chapter Summary
Four kings fought against five kings in the Valley of Siddim. The defeated kings fled, and the winners took goods, food, and people from Sodom and Gomorrah, including Abram's nephew Lot. Abram gathered 318 trained men, rescued Lot and the other captives, and brought back the goods. Melchizedek king of Salem, priest of God Most High, blessed Abram and praised God for the victory. Abram gave Melchizedek a tenth, then refused the king of Sodom's offer so no one could say Sodom had made Abram rich.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **Rescue can be faithful courage**: Abram acted to rescue Lot and the captured people.
2. **Blessing belongs to God Most High**: Melchizedek praised God as Maker of heaven and earth and giver of victory.
3. **Abram trusted God for his wealth**: Abram refused Sodom's goods so the king of Sodom could not claim to make him rich.
