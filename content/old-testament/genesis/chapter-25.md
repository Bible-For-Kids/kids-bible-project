# Genesis Chapter 25

## Book Overview
Genesis 25 continues the covenant family story. Abraham dies, Ishmael's family is listed, and Isaac and Rebekah have Esau and Jacob, whose conflict begins even before birth.

## Important Keywords
- generation: People living in the same time, or a step in a family line
- promise: Words someone says and keeps
- birthright: The special family place and inheritance of the firstborn son
- Jacob: Isaac and Rebekah's younger son, whom God later named Israel
- Esau: Isaac and Rebekah's older son, Jacob's brother

## Chapter Summary
Abraham dies, Ishmael's family is listed, and Isaac and Rebekah have Esau and Jacob, whose conflict begins even before birth.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God keeps the story moving**: Even family conflict, journeys, and danger cannot stop the promises God has spoken.
2. **People need mercy and truth**: The chapter shows real human choices while keeping God as the faithful one.
3. **Details matter**: Names, places, blessings, and promises should be kept clear because they carry the story forward.
