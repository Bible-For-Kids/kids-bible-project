# Genesis Chapter 12

## Book Overview
Genesis 12 continues the covenant family story. God calls Abram to leave home, promises land, offspring, and blessing, and brings him to Canaan before Abram goes down to Egypt during famine.

## Important Keywords
- promise: Words someone says and keeps
- blessing: Good spoken or given by God
- land: The place God promised to give Abram's family
- offspring: Children and later family who come after someone
- famine: A time when there is very little food
- altar: A special place where someone worshiped the Lord with an offering

## Verse-by-Verse Translation

### Genesis 12:1
**Original Reference**: Genesis 12:1 - Now Yahweh said to Abram, "Leave your country, and your relatives, and your father's house, and go to the land that I will show you.

#### Ages 5-7
The Lord spoke to Abram. He said, "Leave your country, your relatives, and your father's house. Go to the land I will show you.

#### Ages 8-10
The Lord spoke to Abram and said, "Leave your country, your people, and your father's household. Go to the land I will show you.

**Translation Notes**:
Keep God as the one who initiates Abram's journey; Abram does not invent the promise.

## Chapter Summary
The Lord called Abram to leave his country and go to the land God would show him. God promised to make Abram into a great nation, bless him, make his name great, and bless all families on earth through him. Abram obeyed and came to Canaan, where the Lord promised the land and Abram built altars. During a severe famine, Abram went down to Egypt and acted out of fear about Sarai, but the Lord protected Sarai and Abram left Egypt with her.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God calls and promises**: Abram's journey begins because the Lord speaks first.
2. **Blessing is meant to spread**: God promised to bless all families on earth through Abram.
3. **God stays faithful when people are fearful**: Abram's fear in Egypt did not stop the Lord from protecting Sarai and the promise.

2. What did God promise Abram?
3. What did Abram build when the Lord appeared to him?

### Ages 8-10
1. How is God's promise to make Abram's name great different from Babel's plan?
2. How did Abram respond when he came into the land of Canaan?
3. What does the Egypt scene show about Abram's fear and the Lord's faithfulness?
