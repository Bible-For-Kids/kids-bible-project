# Genesis Chapter 17

## Book Overview
Genesis 17 continues the covenant family story. God gives Abram the covenant sign of circumcision, changes Abram and Sarai's names, and promises that Sarah will bear Isaac.

## Important Keywords
- covenant: A serious promise relationship God makes
- promise: Words someone says and keeps
- offspring: Children and later family who come after someone
- sign: Something given to point to a promise or truth
- Isaac: Abraham and Sarah's promised son
- God Almighty: A name for God that shows his power
- blameless: Living faithfully and rightly before God
- Abraham: Abram's new name, connected with being father of many nations
- Sarah: Sarai's new name as the mother in God's promise
- circumcision: The body sign God gave to the males in Abraham's covenant family

## Chapter Summary
When Abram is 99 years old, God appears as God Almighty and confirms his covenant. God changes Abram's name to Abraham and Sarai's name to Sarah, promises that nations and kings will come from them, and says Sarah will bear Isaac. God also promises blessing for Ishmael, while making the everlasting covenant line through Isaac. Abraham obeys that same day by giving the covenant sign to the males in his household.

## Key Lessons for Children
1. **God's covenant promise is serious and kind**: God promises, names, blesses, and calls Abraham to faithful life.
2. **God can promise what looks impossible**: Abraham and Sarah are very old, but God's word names Isaac before he is born.
3. **God does not forget Ishmael**: Isaac receives the covenant line, and Ishmael still receives a real blessing from God.
4. **Faith responds with obedience**: Abraham acts on God's command that same day.
