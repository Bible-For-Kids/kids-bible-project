### Ages 8-10
Before anything existed, there was only darkness. But God was there, and He had a wonderful plan. God spoke, "Let there be light!" Suddenly, brilliant light filled the darkness. God separated the light from the darkness, calling them "day" and "night." That was the first day.

On the second day, God created the sky, a beautiful blue dome above the earth. He separated the waters in the sky from the waters on the earth, creating the atmosphere we breathe.

The third day brought amazing changes. God gathered the waters together, making dry land appear. He called the land "earth" and the waters "seas." Then God covered the earth with plants - tiny flowers, mighty trees, green grass, and delicious fruits and vegetables.

On day four, God placed the sun in the sky to rule the day and the moon to rule the night. He scattered countless stars across the darkness, creating the beautiful night sky we still see today.

The fifth day was filled with life! God filled the oceans with swimming fish of every size and color. He filled the sky with flying birds - eagles soaring high, sparrows chirping in trees, and hummingbirds hovering over flowers.

On the sixth day, God created all the land animals - lions roaring, monkeys playing, rabbits hopping, and butterflies dancing. Then God created His most special creation - people. He made man and woman in His own image, giving them the ability to think, love, and create. He named them Adam and Eve.

On the seventh day, God rested from His work. He looked at His perfect creation - the light, the earth, the plants, the animals, and the people - and declared it was very good!

### Ages 11-12
In the beginning, when time itself began, the universe was formless and empty, shrouded in darkness. But the Spirit of God was hovering over the waters, ready to bring forth life and order. God spoke with creative power: "Let there be light!" Instantly, light burst forth, illuminating the cosmos. God saw that the light was good and separated it from the darkness, establishing the rhythm of day and night that still governs our world.

On the second day, God established the atmosphere, creating the sky that separates the waters above from the waters below. This expanse would provide the air we breathe and protect life on earth.

The third day revealed God's artistic power as He commanded the waters to gather, allowing dry land to emerge. He called the land "earth" and the waters "seas." Then God commanded the earth to produce vegetation - every kind of plant, tree, and flower. The earth bloomed with life in countless varieties, each with its own unique beauty and purpose.

On the fourth day, God set celestial bodies in their places. He created the sun to govern the day, providing light and warmth for life. He placed the moon to rule the night, reflecting the sun's light in gentle silver. He scattered stars across the heavens, creating constellations that would guide travelers and mark seasons.

The fifth day exploded with animal life. God filled the seas with creatures from the tiny plankton to the great whales. He created birds of every kind - from the mighty eagle to the tiny hummingbird, each perfectly designed for its environment and purpose.

On the sixth day, God populated the land with animals - mammals, reptiles, and insects of every description. Then, as the crown of His creation, God made humanity. He said, "Let us make mankind in our image, in our likeness." He created man and woman, giving them dominion over the earth, the ability to reason, the capacity to love, and the freedom to choose. He named them Adam and Eve, the first parents of all humanity.

On the seventh day, God rested from His creative work, not because He was tired, but to establish a pattern of rest for humanity. He surveyed His completed creation - the perfect harmony of light and dark, land and sea, plants and animals, and humanity made in His image - and declared it was very good, perfect in every detail.

## Key Lesson
God is powerful, creative, and loving. He made everything with purpose and care, and He made people special in His image.

## Discussion Questions

### Ages 5-7
1. What was the first thing God made?
2. Which day did God make animals?
3. How did God make people special?

### Ages 8-10
1. Why do you think God rested on the seventh day?
2. How does taking care of plants and animals show we're made in God's image?
3. What's your favorite thing God created and why?

### Ages 11-12
1. What does it mean to be made "in God's image"?
2. How does the order of creation show God's wisdom and planning?
3. How can we care for God's creation as His representatives?

## Memory Verse
"In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth." - Genesis 1:1

## Prayer
Dear God, thank you for creating this beautiful world and for making us special. Help us take care of your creation and remember that we are made in your image. Amen.
