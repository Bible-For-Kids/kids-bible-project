# Noah's Ark

## Bible Reference
Genesis 6:9 - 9:17

## Age Group
5-12 years

## Story Content

### Ages 5-7
Long ago, people were not being kind to each other. God was sad because people were making bad choices. But there was one good man named Noah. Noah loved God and tried to do what was right.

God told Noah, "I'm going to send a big flood to wash away all the bad things. But you and your family will be safe. Build a big boat called an ark."

Noah obeyed God. He built a huge boat. God told Noah to bring two of every animal onto the ark. So Noah brought two elephants, two monkeys, two lions, two birds, and two of every kind of animal.

It rained for 40 days and 40 nights. Water covered the whole earth, but Noah and his family and all the animals were safe and dry inside the ark.

After the rain stopped, Noah sent out a bird to look for dry land. Finally, the waters went down, and the ark landed on a mountain.

God told Noah, "You can all come out now!" Noah, his family, and all the animals left the ark. God put a beautiful rainbow in the sky and promised, "I will never send a flood to cover the whole earth again. The rainbow will remind you of my promise."

### Ages 8-10
As the world grew, people became more and more wicked. They fought, lied, and hurt each other. God saw how evil humanity had become, and His heart was broken. But there was one man who stood out from the rest - Noah. Noah was righteous and blameless, and he walked faithfully with God.

God said to Noah, "I am going to put an end to all people because the earth is filled with violence. But I will save you and your family. Build an ark of gopher wood. Make rooms in it and coat it with pitch inside and out."

God gave Noah specific instructions: "The ark will be 450 feet long, 75 feet wide, and 45 feet high. Make a roof and finish the ark to within 18 inches of the top. Put a door in the side and make lower, middle, and upper decks."

God also told Noah, "Bring two of every kind of animal into the ark - male and female. Also take seven pairs of every kind of clean animal. Take every kind of food and store it away."

Noah obeyed God completely. He built the ark exactly as God commanded. When the ark was finished, God said, "Go into the ark, you and your whole family, because I have found you righteous in this generation."

Seven days later, the flood came. All the springs of the great deep burst forth, and the floodgates of the heavens opened. Rain fell on the earth for 40 days and 40 nights. The waters rose and covered the mountains to a depth of more than 20 feet. Every living thing on earth perished, but Noah and his family and the animals with them were safe in the ark.

The waters flooded the earth for 150 days. Then God remembered Noah and sent a wind over the earth, and the waters receded. The ark came to rest on the mountains of Ararat.

Noah sent out a raven, but it kept flying back and forth. Then he sent a dove, but it found no place to rest. Seven days later, Noah sent the dove again, and it returned with an olive leaf. Noah knew the waters had receded.

When Noah and his family and all the animals came out of the ark, Noah built an altar to God. God was pleased and made a promise: "Never again will I destroy all living creatures. I have set my rainbow in the clouds as a sign of my covenant between me and all life on earth."

### Ages 11-12
In the generations following Adam and Eve, humanity multiplied across the earth. But as the population grew, so did wickedness. People's thoughts became "only evil all the time." The earth was filled with violence, corruption, and rebellion against God. The Lord regretted making human beings and His heart was deeply troubled.

But Noah found favor in the eyes of the Lord. Noah was a righteous man, blameless among the people of his time, and he walked faithfully with God. God decided to judge the world while preserving a remnant through Noah.

God said to Noah, "I am going to destroy both people and animals. I am going to wipe them from the face of the earth. But I will establish my covenant with you. You are to build an ark of cypress wood. Make rooms in it and coat it with pitch inside and out."

God provided detailed architectural specifications: "This is how you are to build it: The ark is to be 450 cubits long, 75 cubits wide, and 45 cubits high. Make a roof for it, leaving below the roof an opening 18 inches high all around. Put a door in the side of the ark and make lower, middle, and upper decks."

God also commanded Noah regarding the passengers: "You are to bring into the ark two of all living creatures, male and female, to keep them alive with you. Two of every kind of bird, every kind of animal, and every kind of creature that moves along the ground will come to you to be kept alive. You are also to take every kind of food that is to be eaten and store it away as food for you and for them."

Noah did everything just as God commanded him. When the ark was completed, God said, "Go into the ark, you and your whole family, because I have found you righteous in this generation. Seven days from now I will send rain on the earth for 40 days and 40 nights, and I will wipe from the face of the earth every living creature I have made."

On that day, Noah and his sons Shem, Ham, and Japheth, together with his wife and the wives of his three sons, entered the ark. They had with them every kind of wild animal, livestock, creature that moves along the ground, and bird. Pairs of all creatures that have the breath of life in them came to Noah and entered the ark.

The flood lasted 40 days. The waters rose and lifted the ark high above the earth. The waters completely covered the highest mountains. Every living thing on dry land died. Only Noah and those with him in the ark survived.

After 150 days, God remembered Noah and sent a wind over the earth. The waters receded steadily. On the first day of the tenth month, the tops of the mountains became visible. Noah sent out a raven, then a dove. After seven days, the dove returned with an olive leaf, indicating the waters had receded.

When Noah emerged from the ark, he built an altar and sacrificed burnt offerings. The Lord smelled the pleasing aroma and said in His heart: "Never again will I curse the ground because of humans, even though every inclination of the human heart is evil from childhood. Never again will I destroy all living creatures as I have done."

God established His covenant: "This is the sign of the covenant I am making between me and you and every living creature with you: a rainbow. Whenever the rainbow appears in the clouds, I will see it and remember the everlasting covenant between God and all living creatures on earth."

## Key Lesson
God protects those who trust Him, and He always keeps His promises. Obedience to God brings safety and blessing.

## Discussion Questions

### Ages 5-7
1. Why did God tell Noah to build an ark?
2. What kinds of animals went on the ark?
3. What was God's promise after the flood?

### Ages 8-10
1. How did Noah show he trusted God?
2. Why do you think God saved the animals too?
3. What does the rainbow remind us of?

### Ages 11-12
1. What does Noah's obedience teach us about faith?
2. How does the flood story show both God's justice and mercy?
3. How can we be like Noah in our world today?

## Memory Verse
"But Noah found favor in the eyes of the Lord." - Genesis 6:8

## Prayer
Dear God, thank you for keeping your promises. Help us to trust you like Noah did, even when things seem difficult. Thank you for the rainbow that reminds us of your love and faithfulness. Amen.
